# Wayground Comprehensive Style Guide
*Source: Cody's PMM/Enablement folder • March 2026*
*This is the canonical brand voice reference for Vox (brand voice copywriter).*

## 1. Brand Framework

### Core Belief
Every student deserves access to grade-level resources in ways that work for their unique needs, and every teacher deserves standards-aligned resources their students love using.

### Mission
Help every student find their way forward and make curriculum go.

### Positioning
Wayground is an instructional hub where educators find and personalize standards-aligned resources that make grade-level achievement faster for every student.

### Brand Pillars

**Pillar 1: Teacher First**
- Respect teacher intuition, honor the craft of teaching
- Provide resources that reduce unnecessary burdens
- Offer supportive, not prescriptive, guidance

**Pillar 2: Earned Trust**
- Prioritize data privacy, ensure instructional quality
- Be transparent, show don't tell
- Demonstrate reliability through specific examples

**Pillar 3: Joyful Progress**
- Create opportunities for genuine "aha" moments
- Make student learning visible and measurable
- Emphasize depth over superficial achievements

## 2. Educator Persona (Core Target)

**Role:** Middle school teachers (grades 5-8), 10+ years experience
**Subjects:** Core subjects (Math, Social Studies, ELA, Science)
**Context:** Public school, US, 5-6 classes/day, 20-30 students/class, 45-50 min periods
**Mindset:** Student-centered, data-informed, standards-driven, practical over theoretical

## 3. Voice Persona: The Tech Coach Aficionado

### Who We Are
- A supportive tech coach who's been in classroom trenches
- Passionate about pedagogical excellence
- Neither a distant academic nor a salesperson
- The trusted colleague who makes teachers' lives easier

### 5 Voice Principles

**1. Say it the Teacher Way**
- Traditional: "Equip every student with evidence-based differentiation."
- Wayground: "5 reading levels? That's a normal day at Wayground."

**2. Be a Colleague, Not a Vendor**
- Traditional: "Fewer headaches and secure connections. Optimize learning with LMS, LTI, and rostering solutions."
- Wayground: "No more password resets or missing assignments. Everything connects seamlessly with your school systems."

**3. Respect Educators' Time with Clarity**
- Traditional: "Focus on what matters most when instruction is all in one place."
- Wayground: "Spend time teaching students, not hunting for resources."

**4. Stay Clever, Not Cute**
- Traditional: "Gamify for growth"
- Wayground: "Students complete work when it feels like a win and their scores prove they're understanding the content."

**5. Frame Problems, Focus on Solutions**
- Traditional: "Close gaps, open doors with accommodations."
- Wayground: "When language barriers or learning differences hold students back from grade-level content, accommodations remove obstacles without lowering expectations."

## 4. Tone Calibration

| More ✅ | Less ❌ |
|---------|---------|
| Confident | Boastful |
| Knowledgeable | Lecturing |
| Supportive | Patronizing |
| Practical | Theoretical |
| Empathetic | Emotional |

## 5. Copywriting Rules

### Language Foundations
- Standard American English
- **7th-grade reading level** for teacher-facing content
- Modified AP style with teacher-friendly nuances
- Oxford commas: YES
- Present tense, first/second person
- Singular "they" where appropriate

### DO USE
- "Your students", "In your classroom", "During your math block"
- "Standards-aligned", "Student outcomes", "Time-saving", "Evidence-based"

### AVOID
- "Kids", "Teaching session", "Learning outcomes"
- "Revolutionary", "Game-changing", "Just", "Simply", "Should", "Must"
- Never lead or write hooks with a question

### Sentence Structure
- Active voice, 15-25 words per sentence
- Vary length for rhythm
- Lead with the benefit
- One key idea per sentence
- 2-4 sentences per paragraph

### Fonts
- Primary: DM Sans (Bold/Semi-Bold for headings, Regular for body)

## 6. Content Lengths

### Email
- Subject lines: 35 characters
- Preview text: 35 characters
- Body: 150-200 words
- Paragraphs: 2-3 sentences
- CTA: Clear, single action

### Blog Posts
- Headlines: 50-60 characters
- Intro: 60-80 words
- Body: 800-1,200 words
- Subheads: Every 2-3 paragraphs
- Paragraphs: 3-4 sentences max
- Conclusion: 50-75 words

### Social Media
- LinkedIn: 150-200 characters
- Facebook: 100-150 characters
- Twitter: 240-280 characters

### Product Pages
- Headlines: 40-60 characters
- Feature descriptions: 50-75 words
- Benefit statements: 15-25 words
- Total page: 400-600 words

## 7. Messaging Formulas

### Problem-Solution
**[Pain Point] + [Solution] + [Outcome]**
"Struggling with differentiation? Create leveled assignments automatically and watch every student succeed at their own pace."

### Benefit-Feature
**[Benefit] + [Feature] + [Proof]**
"Save hours on grading with auto-scored assessments—just like the 10,000+ teachers who reclaimed their evenings."

### Success Story
**[Challenge] + [Implementation] + [Result]**
"When Ms. Rodriguez needed to support multilingual learners, she used translation features to help them access grade-level content—resulting in 40% growth in comprehension scores."

## 8. AI Content Approach

### DO:
- Explain AI features in practical terms
- Show concrete classroom applications
- Highlight human-centered design
- Emphasize AI as a supportive tool

### AVOID:
- Overpromising AI capabilities
- Using technical jargon
- Minimizing teacher expertise
- Presenting AI as a replacement for teachers

## 9. Trust-Building Principles

- Reference specific classroom scenarios
- Share authentic teaching stories
- Validate educator experiences
- Use correct educational terminology
- Include specific, concrete examples
- Provide data-driven insights
- Admit limitations
- Show continuous learning and adaptation

## 10. Communication North Star

Before every piece of content:
1. Would a teacher find this valuable?
2. Is the benefit immediately clear?
3. Does this support our platform positioning?
4. Is the tone authentic and helpful?
5. Are we building genuine trust?

**Our Ultimate Goal:** To be the trusted partner that helps every teacher and student find their way forward.

## OUR POSITION ON AI

**"AI where it counts"**

We believe AI works in the background to support, not replace, the human connection and critical thinking that make learning happen. AI won't sideline teachers. It can't implement curriculum or build authentic motivation, and students shouldn't be isolated behind screens. Technology can help us build connections instead. AI should complement your ability to quickly read the room and make adjustments that help each student find their way.

- Amplify shared human experiences, not digital isolation
- Respect teacher agency, while also saving time
- Prioritize privacy and impact

## BRAND PILLAR: TEACHER-FIRST

Wayground believes that teachers are the experts in their classrooms. We don't dictate how to teach—we amplify what teachers already know works. By providing intuitive resources that respect teacher judgment, honor their expertise, and reduce unnecessary burdens, we give educators the agency to shape learning experiences that meet their unique classroom needs. Because when teachers have the right resources at their fingertips, they can focus on what matters most: connecting with students and inspiring meaningful growth.

## BRAND PILLAR: EARNED TRUST

Wayground provides the reliable foundation that educators deserve. In a landscape filled with flashy promises and inconsistent results, we deliver dependable technology, evidence-based practices, and standards-aligned resources that schools can truly count on. Our commitment to data privacy, instructional quality, and measurable outcomes ensures that administrators can make confident decisions, teachers can rely on us day after day, and students receive consistent, high-quality learning experiences. We earn trust not through marketing claims, but through products that consistently deliver real results in real classrooms.

## BRAND PILLAR: JOYFUL PROGRESS

Wayground focuses on creating meaningful learning moments that build lasting understanding. We believe true progress isn't measured by engagement statistics alone, but by those transformative "aha moments" when concepts click and confidence grows. Teachers use us to help identify where students truly are, provide personalized pathways to growth, and make learning visible through actionable insights. By ensuring every student can access grade-level content in ways that work for them, we help close achievement gaps and create classroom experiences where genuine progress—not just participation—is the standard.
