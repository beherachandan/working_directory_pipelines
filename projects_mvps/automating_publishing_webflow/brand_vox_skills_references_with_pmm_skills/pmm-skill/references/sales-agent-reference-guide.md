## How to Use This Document
This document is designed as a structured knowledge base for an AI sales agent. When a sales rep asks about what to say or show for a specific persona, feature, or use case, the AI agent should:
* Identify the persona the rep is speaking to (see Section 2 for persona profiles)
* Look up the relevant value propositions mapped to that persona (see Section 3)
* Pull the specific talk track language and demo recommendations (see Sections 3–5)
* Cross-reference with subject-specific or use-case-specific messaging when relevant (see Section 4)
* Use competitive positioning when the prospect mentions a competitor (see Section 6)
* Match customer signals to the right messaging pillar: Plan, Teach, or Measure (see Section 7)

## Core Messaging Framework
All Wayground messaging is organized around three pillars that mirror the instruction-assessment loop:

### PLAN (with precision)
AI-powered content creation, curriculum alignment, resource management.
- Teacher headline: "Find the right materials at the right time."
- Admin headline: "Protect your curriculum investment."

### TEACH (with access & engagement)
25+ accommodations, gamification, differentiation, student access.
- Teacher headline: "Engagement starts with access."
- Admin headline: "Students engage when they can access the content."

### MEASURE (with success)
Formative assessment, real-time data, common assessments, reporting.
- Teacher headline: "Get data that reflects what students know."
- Admin headline: "Formative data you can trust."

## Persona Quick-Reference Profiles

### Teacher (The Engagement-Focused Educator)
- **Role:** K-12 classroom teachers across all subjects and grade levels
- **Experience:** Wide range; 1–30+ years. Mix of tech-savvy and tech-hesitant
- **Primary Goal:** Keep students engaged while efficiently managing workload
- **What Keeps Them Up at Night:** Differentiating for 30+ students, grading piles, finding quality resources, competing with TikTok for attention
- **Top Pain Points:**
  - Spending evenings creating different versions of assignments to differentiate
  - Hunting for quality resources that align to standards or curriculum
  - Drowning in grading with no time for timely feedback
  - Students zoning out during traditional lessons; same 5 students answering every question
  - Multilingual students can't access grade-level content; IEP accommodations feel manual
  - Too many platforms: 3+ logins, students lose assignments between tools
- **What They Value:** Time savings above all else; intuitive tools; engagement that lasts; ready-made standards-aligned content; real-time data
- **Decision Role:** Influencers and end-users. Their enthusiasm (or resistance) makes or breaks adoption.

### Director of Curriculum & Instruction
- **Role:** District-level curriculum directors, coordinators, content specialists
- **Experience:** 15+ years in education with curriculum leadership background
- **Primary Goal:** Ensure rigorous, coherent curriculum implementation across all schools
- **What Keeps Them Up at Night:** Teachers going off-curriculum with random internet worksheets, inconsistent quality, inability to see if adopted curriculum is working
- **Top Pain Points:**
  - Teachers supplement with low-quality resources instead of adopted curriculum
  - Digital resources don't align with scope and sequence
  - Assessment data is fragmented across platforms
  - PD focuses on tool features instead of instructional best practices
  - Curriculum fidelity impossible when everyone uses different tools
- **What They Value:** Standards alignment and curriculum coherence; quality over quantity; evidence that supplemental materials reinforce adopted curriculum; district-wide visibility
- **Decision Role:** Primary buyer for supplemental content and curriculum-aligned tools. Controls instructional materials budgets.

### Director of Instructional Technology
- **Role:** District-level IT/EdTech directors, digital learning directors
- **Experience:** 15+ years in education, 7+ in technology leadership; manages 5–20 tech coaches
- **Primary Goal:** Drive effective technology integration while consolidating and simplifying the tech stack
- **What Keeps Them Up at Night:** Tool proliferation (2,700+ tools/year), security vulnerabilities, budget waste on unused software, uneven adoption
- **Top Pain Points:**
  - Too many disconnected solutions creating complexity and security risk
  - Surface-level technology use without pedagogical transformation
  - Limited staff to support the growing tech stack
  - Difficulty connecting technology use to measurable student outcomes
  - SSO integration nightmares; student data privacy compliance
- **What They Value:** Integration with existing ecosystem (LMS, SIS, SSO); consolidation; data privacy/security compliance; scalability
- **Decision Role:** Key technical decision-maker and gatekeeper. Their "no" can kill a deal.

### Principal / Assistant Principal
- **Role:** K-12 school principals and assistant principals
- **Experience:** 15+ years in education, 5+ as administrators; former classroom teachers
- **Primary Goal:** Improve school-wide academic outcomes and close achievement gaps
- **What Keeps Them Up at Night:** State test scores, teacher burnout and retention, proving ROI to school board, inconsistent instruction
- **Top Pain Points:**
  - No visibility into what's happening in classrooms day-to-day
  - Achievement gaps widening despite technology investments
  - Teachers burning out from technology fatigue
  - Too many disconnected platforms
  - Paying for 12 different platforms that do the same thing
- **What They Value:** Evidence-based solutions (ESSA Level 3); ROI; tools teachers actually use; consolidated reporting
- **Decision Role:** Building-level budget authority. Champions tools that help meet school improvement goals.

### Technology Coach / Instructional Coach
- **Role:** Instructional technology coaches, digital learning specialists, innovation coaches
- **Experience:** 8–15 years in education; former innovative classroom teachers
- **Primary Goal:** Empower teachers to use technology effectively for student learning
- **What Keeps Them Up at Night:** Supporting 120+ teachers across 7+ platforms, teachers abandoning tools after training
- **Top Pain Points:**
  - Constantly shifting between different tools
  - Teachers abandon tools after initial training
  - Troubleshooting tech problems more than supporting instruction
  - Measuring usage not impact
- **What They Value:** Teacher-friendly tools requiring minimal training; quick wins; one platform to go deep on; progressive complexity
- **Decision Role:** Critical influencer and implementation partner. They make or break adoption success.

## Customer Signals → Lead with This Messaging

### Lead with PLAN When You Hear:
- Recent curricular adoption (1–3 years into cycle)
- Upcoming or ongoing curriculum adoption
- In-house authored or heavily modified curricula
- Expansive tech stack relying on skills instead of standards
- Recent AI initiative with slow teacher adoption
- Lacking supplemental resources
- Fear of "wild west" supplementing
- Recent staff cuts leading to low TOSA bandwidth
- **Key message:** "Protect your curricular investments with a supplemental resource library specifically aligned to fill content gaps."

### Lead with TEACH When You Hear:
- Recent/ongoing compliance issue (IEP, 504, ELL)
- Initiatives around persistence or graduation rates
- Dry curricula lacking activities and engagement
- Tech stack stuffed with subject-specific tools
- Strong equity mandates
- Science of Reading mandate
- Strong value for teacher autonomy
- **Key message:** "Support access and engagement district-wide with 25+ accommodations that shape a unique learning experience for every student."

### Lead with MEASURE When You Hear:
- Recent failed common assessment adoption
- Stale use of a legacy assessment tool
- Relying on formative data spread across a bloated tech stack
- Strong PLC culture and practices
- In-building TOSAs needing insights for coaching/PD
- **Key message:** "Build structures and routines around data insights born out of an engaging platform teachers and students actually love to use."
