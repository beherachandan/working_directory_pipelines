## 🚨 The Rule (Read This First — Always)

**"Saves time" is not the benefit. It's the vehicle.**

Every AI product in edtech claims to save teachers time. It's become meaningless — the edtech equivalent of "easy to use." When everyone says the same thing, nobody is saying anything.

The question is always: **what does that time give back?**

For teachers, reclaimed time means:
- A Sunday that doesn't involve a laptop
- The mental bandwidth to actually be present with students on Monday
- Hobbies, rest, time with their own families
- The feeling that their paycheck reflects the hours they actually put in
- **Being a coach at their own kid's game** ← the canonical bar

**Write to that.** Not "save time on lesson planning" — but "plan in seconds, not weekends" or "time you'd otherwise lose to prep."

**The canonical bar (Carrie, Mar 14 2026):**
> *"Be a coach at their own kid's game."*

That's the level of specificity and humanity we're writing toward. Not "work-life balance." Not "personal time." A coach on a sideline on a Saturday. That image is what we're giving back. Write to that.

---

## 🤖 The AI Angle Makes This Even More Critical

In a world where every tool promises AI-powered time savings, the differentiator isn't the time. It's what you do with it.

**Wayground's position is NOT:** "AI that saves you time."
**Wayground's position IS:** AI that handles the stuff that *shouldn't* require a human — so teachers can do the work that *does.*

That's a different claim. A harder one to copy.

If you've written "saves time" or "reduces time spent on X" in a draft, stop and ask: **time for what?**

If we can't answer that in a way that feels real and specific to a teacher's actual life — rewrite it.

## 🗣️ Standing Prompt Rule (Carrie, Mar 14 2026)

**Whenever talking with anyone about time savings — AEs, CSMs, teachers, anyone — ask:**

> *"What are teachers actually doing with the time they've saved?"*

Don't skip this. It takes a minute and it surfaces the specific, human stories that make the value prop real. A teacher who says "I coached my kid's soccer game for the first time in three years" is worth a hundred "saves time on lesson planning" claims.

Collect the answers. If something specific and compelling comes up, flag it to Carrie (U05DLPHNH8T) — it may become a customer story or copy anchor.

## 🕐 What Time Savings Categories Exist (Use As Input, Not Headlines)

These are the *mechanisms* behind time savings. They support the story — they are not the story itself. Always lead with what the time gives back.

### 1. Content Creation Time (AI-powered)
**The mechanism:** AI Create generates standards-aligned activities from a prompt, PDF, or objective in under 2 minutes.

**Sourced customer stories:**
- **Henderson County HS (KY):** Assessment creation time reduced to ~10 minutes. *(Source: Wayground case study — reference before using externally)*
- **Falling Creek MS (VA, Title I, 60% multilingual):** Eliminated Google Forms + paper patchwork entirely. *(Source: case study)*
- **Arlington ISD (TX, 77 schools):** "Quizizz provided more of what we needed, in one platform, than other apps did separately." — Steve Simpson. *(Consolidation + efficiency story; no specific time number)*

**Modeling framework (Artie — internal use only):**
- Average teacher creates ~3 new activities/week
- Cold-start time without AI: 30–45 min/activity
- With AI Create: 5–10 min to review/refine a generated draft
- Estimated time savings: 60–90 min/teacher/week on content creation alone

**Lead with the "what for":** Don't say "create a quiz in 2 minutes." Say "plan Sunday night in 10 minutes — the rest of the weekend stays yours."

---

### 2. Platform Management Overhead (Consolidation)
**The mechanism:** One platform replaces 3–5 separate tools. One login, one contract, one admin interface.

**Modeling framework (from ROI Calculator v3 — Carrie approved):**
- Assumption: 45 minutes/teacher/week overhead per extra platform (beyond 1)
- School year: 36 weeks
- Example: 3-platform district with 200 teachers = **1,800 hours/year** in overhead
- At loaded teacher cost ($35/hr): ~**$63,000/year** in time cost
- **Always label as "estimated" in external-facing content.**

**Lead with the "what for":** Don't say "reduce platform overhead." Say "your teachers shouldn't be spending Friday afternoons exporting CSVs."

---

### 3. Auto-Grading & Reporting Time
**The mechanism:** All activities auto-grade. Grade passback to Canvas/Google Classroom is automatic. Reports are instant.

**Talking points (directional — not hard numbers):**
- No manual grade entry
- No CSV exports to LMS
- Instant per-question performance data after every session
- Accommodations (extended time, extra attempts) tracked automatically

**Lead with the "what for":** Don't say "instant reporting." Say "no more Sunday night gradebook catch-up."

## 🎯 Time Savings by Persona (for Artie discovery briefs)

Always anchor to what the time enables — not the time itself.

### Superintendent / CAO
Lead with **teacher retention** — tool fragmentation increases burnout. Time savings = teachers who stay.
> *"Your teachers are managing 4 platforms. That's 3 hours every week just on logistics. That's not a tool problem — it's a retention problem. Consolidating to Wayground gives that time back."*

### Curriculum Director / Instructional Coach
Lead with **present-in-the-classroom** — AI Create means less time building, more time with students.
> *"Instead of building from scratch, teachers start from a standards-aligned draft. The first 80% is done in 2 minutes. They own the last 20% — and their evenings."*

### Procurement / CFO / Business Officer
Lead with **combined ROI** — cost savings + time savings in dollars.
> *"At [N] teachers and [X] platforms, we estimate $[Y]/year in time costs alone, before you count the licensing."*

### IT Director
Lead with **operations simplicity** — one contract, one rostering integration, one SSO.
> *"Every additional platform is a separate SIS sync, a separate support ticket, a separate renewal negotiation. One platform eliminates all of that."*

## 📝 Language Guidelines (Vox)

**Use:**
- "plan in seconds, not weekends"
- "the rest of the weekend stays yours"
- "so you can be present on Monday"
- "the stuff that shouldn't require a human"
- "less setup, more actual teaching"
- "teachers get [X] hours/week back" *(only if sourced)*

**Avoid:**
- "saves time" as a standalone claim — always follow with "for what"
- Specific unverified numbers without a source
- "ROI" as a standalone word in teacher-facing copy (too corporate)
- Comparing to a specific competitor by name in external copy
- Vendor-speak: "maximize efficiency," "streamline workflows," "time savings benefits"

**The test:** If you've written "saves time" — read it back and ask: *"Time for what?"* If you can't answer with something real and human, rewrite it.
