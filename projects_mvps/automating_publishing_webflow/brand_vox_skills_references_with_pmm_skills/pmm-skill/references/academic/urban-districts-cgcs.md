## About CGCS

The Council of the Great City Schools is the only national organization exclusively representing the needs of urban public schools. Founded in 1956.

**Members include:** NYC, LA Unified, Chicago, Miami-Dade, Houston, Clark County (Las Vegas), Hillsborough County (Tampa), Orange County (Orlando), Fairfax County, Philadelphia, Dallas, and 70+ other major urban districts.

**What they do:**
- Publish research on urban education performance, policy, and best practices
- Conduct peer reviews and technical assistance visits to member districts
- Advocate for federal policy that supports urban education
- Convene superintendents and district leaders twice annually
- Set the agenda for what large urban districts prioritize

## Key CGCS Themes and Publications (Current)

### Academic Performance & KPIs
- CGCS publishes Academic KPI reports tracking urban district performance on NAEP, graduation rates, achievement gaps
- Key metric: Trial Urban District Assessment (TUDA) — NAEP results broken out for CGCS member cities
- Districts care deeply about: closing achievement gaps, improving reading and math proficiency, graduation rates

**Wayground angle:** Our formative data helps districts monitor the leading indicators (standards mastery, teacher engagement, assessment frequency) that predict the lagging indicators (NAEP scores, graduation rates) CGCS tracks.

### NAEP Analysis — Intersectional Achievement
- Latest report: "NAEP's Hidden Power: Understanding Achievement at the Intersections"
- Examines performance at intersections of SES, race/ethnicity, language, and disability
- Reveals what works for specific student populations, where gaps persist

**Wayground angle:** Our accommodations (25+) and multilingual support mean we serve the exact populations CGCS highlights in intersectional analysis. Our standards-level data can be disaggregated by student group to mirror the kind of analysis CGCS promotes.

### Newcomer and Immigrant Students
- CGCS published guidance on newcomer students and SLIFE (Students with Limited or Interrupted Formal Education)
- Includes Educational Background Questionnaire framework
- Focus on: identifying needs, placement decisions, instructional programming, wraparound supports

**Wayground angle:** Our multilingual support and accommodations serve newcomer populations. Our platform doesn't require English proficiency to demonstrate content knowledge (image-based questions, drag-and-drop, manipulatives).

### Equity and Access
- Central to everything CGCS does
- Urban districts serve the most diverse student populations in the country
- Equity means: access to high-quality instruction, appropriate accommodations, culturally responsive practices, equitable technology access

**Wayground angle:** Free accommodations for all US educators is an equity story. Every student gets the support they need without the district buying an add-on module.

### Technology and Digital Learning
- CGCS monitors technology adoption across urban districts
- Focus areas: device equity, digital curriculum, assessment technology, data privacy
- Post-pandemic: urban districts invested heavily in technology infrastructure

## Key Signals That a District is CGCS-Connected
- District is in a major metro area (population 250K+)
- Superintendent participates in national conferences
- Board materials reference NAEP, TUDA, achievement gaps
- District has dedicated equity, multilingual, or newcomer programs
- Strategic plan mentions "urban education challenges"
