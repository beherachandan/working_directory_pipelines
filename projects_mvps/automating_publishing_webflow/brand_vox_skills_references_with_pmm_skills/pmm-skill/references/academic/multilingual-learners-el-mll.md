## Terminology — Get This Right

| Term | What It Means | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **EL (English Learner)** | Federal/legal term for students receiving English language services | ESSA compliance, federal reporting |
| **MLL (Multilingual Learner)** | Asset-based term emphasizing that students speak multiple languages | Increasingly preferred by educators and organizations |
| **ELL (English Language Learner)** | Older term, still used in some states | Acceptable but shifting to EL or MLL |
| **Emergent Bilingual** | Research/advocacy term highlighting bilingual development | Academic contexts, equity-focused districts |
| **LTEL (Long-Term English Learner)** | Student classified as EL for 5+ years without reclassification | Intervention discussions — these students need different support |
| **SLIFE** | Students with Limited or Interrupted Formal Education | Newcomer/immigrant contexts — high-need, distinct from typical EL |
| **Reclassification / Exit** | Student demonstrates proficiency and exits EL services | Assessment and progress monitoring discussions |
| **Newcomer** | Recently arrived immigrant student (typically <1-2 years in US) | Newcomer programs, placement discussions |

**Brand rule:** Wayground uses "multilingual" not "ESL" in our own marketing/brand copy. (Carrie, brand guidelines)

## WIDA — The Dominant EL Framework

### What Is WIDA?
WIDA (World-class Instructional Design and Assessment) is a consortium of 41 US states + territories + international schools that provides:
- **English Language Development (ELD) Standards** — The framework for what ELs should know and be able to do at each proficiency level
- **ACCESS for ELLs** — The annual English proficiency assessment used by WIDA consortium states to measure EL progress and make reclassification decisions
- **Professional development** — Courses, workshops, resources for teachers of ELs
- **Research** — Ongoing studies on multilingual learner development

### WIDA ELD Standards Framework (2020 Edition)
Five standards areas:
1. **Social and Instructional Language** — Language for social interactions and navigating school
2. **Language of Language Arts** — Academic language for ELA contexts
3. **Language of Mathematics** — Academic language for math contexts
4. **Language of Science** — Academic language for science contexts
5. **Language of Social Studies** — Academic language for social studies contexts

### WIDA Proficiency Levels
Six levels describing English language development:

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Entering** | Knows and uses minimal social and academic language |
| 2 | **Emerging** | Knows and uses some social and academic language |
| 3 | **Developing** | Knows and uses social and academic language with developing complexity |
| 4 | **Expanding** | Knows and uses social and academic language with increasing complexity |
| 5 | **Bridging** | Knows and uses social and academic language approaching grade-level complexity |
| 6 | **Reaching** | Knows and uses grade-level social and academic language |

**Why these matter:** Teachers with EL students need to know their proficiency level to provide appropriate scaffolding. Assessment tools need to work across all levels.

### WIDA's "Can Do" Philosophy
WIDA emphasizes what multilingual learners CAN do at each proficiency level, not what they can't. This is an asset-based approach that:
- Values students' home languages as resources
- Builds on existing linguistic knowledge
- Rejects deficit framing

**Wayground alignment:** Our multilingual support and accommodations align with this philosophy. An EL student at Level 2 can still demonstrate math knowledge through image-based questions, drag-and-drop, or manipulatives — the language barrier doesn't have to be a knowledge barrier.

## EL Success Forum (ELSF)

### What Is ELSF?
A national organization focused on ensuring high-quality instructional materials serve multilingual learners. They work with:
- **Content developers** — Help them build better products for MLLs
- **Education leaders** — Support curriculum adoption decisions
- **Professional learning partners** — Ensure PD addresses MLL needs
- **States and districts** — Partner on implementation

### ELSF's Key Message
"Language and content are two sides of the same coin. The best way to master content is through language. The best way to master a new language is through content." — Richard Heffernan, Dallas ISD

This means: ELs shouldn't be pulled out of content instruction to "learn English first." They should learn English THROUGH rigorous content instruction with appropriate scaffolding.

### ELSF Guidelines for Evaluating Materials
ELSF has developed criteria for evaluating whether instructional materials serve MLLs effectively:
- Does the material build on students' linguistic and cultural assets?
- Are language demands explicitly identified and scaffolded?
- Do assessments allow MLLs to demonstrate content knowledge regardless of English proficiency level?
- Is there guidance for teachers on differentiating for various proficiency levels?

**Wayground connection:** These criteria directly apply to us as an assessment platform. Our accommodations (read-aloud, extended time, reduced choices, immersive reader, multilingual support) help ELs demonstrate content knowledge while developing English proficiency.

## Wayground + EL/MLL Alignment

### What We Do Well
| EL/MLL Need | Wayground Capability |
|---|---|
| Reduce language barrier in content assessment | Image-based questions, drag-and-drop, visual representations |
| Scaffold for lower proficiency levels | Read-aloud, extended time, reduced answer choices, immersive reader |
| Track language AND content development | Standards-tagged content + accommodation usage data |
| Support multilingual classrooms | Multilingual platform support |
| Assess without penalizing language | Multiple response types let students show knowledge through non-written means |
| Progress monitoring for EL reclassification | CA on regular cycles can track academic language growth in content areas |

### What We DON'T Do (Be Honest)
- We are NOT an English proficiency assessment (ACCESS for ELLs is the reclassification tool in WIDA states)
- We are NOT a language development program (we're an assessment and practice platform)
- We don't replace dedicated ELD instruction
- We complement ELD programs by providing formative assessment in content areas with appropriate scaffolding

### Title III Funding Connection
Title III (Language Instruction for English Learner and Immigrant Students) provides federal funding for:
- Supplemental language instruction programs
- Professional development for teachers of ELs
- Parent engagement
- **Assessment tools that help monitor EL academic progress** ← This is us

Wayground can be funded through Title III when used to assess and monitor EL academic progress in content areas with appropriate accommodations.
