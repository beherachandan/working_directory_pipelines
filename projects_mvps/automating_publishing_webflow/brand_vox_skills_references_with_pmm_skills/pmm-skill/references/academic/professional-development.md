## The Problem: $18B Spent, 71% Dissatisfied

The Gates Foundation / BCG study surveyed 1,300+ stakeholders and 1,600 additional teachers. The headline findings:

- **$18 billion** spent annually on professional development in US K-12
- Only **29%** of teachers are highly satisfied with current PD
- Only **34%** think PD has improved over time
- Teachers spend **68 hours/year** on district-directed PD; **89 hours/year** including self-guided
- Workshops dominate (80%+ participation) despite **-26 net satisfaction**
- Only **7%** of schools have strong collaboration models
- Only **30%** of teachers choose most or all of their PD
- Teachers with choice are **more than twice as satisfied** (40% vs. 15% highly satisfied)

## What Teachers Actually Want from PD

Teachers describe effective PD as:

1. **Relevant** to their specific context and classroom
2. **Interactive and hands-on** — not lecture/presentation
3. **Sustained over time** — semester or year-long, not one-off workshops
4. **Treats teachers as professionals** — adult learners, not students
5. **Delivered by practitioners** who understand classroom reality
6. **Teacher-driven** — with administration supporting, not directing
7. **Immediately applicable** — specific actions they can use tomorrow

### The Disconnect That Matters for Sales

| Format | Teacher Net Satisfaction | What Districts Want MORE Of |
|---|---|---|
| Courses | +22 | +25 |
| Conferences | +5 | +18 |
| Lesson observation | 0 | **+84** |
| Coaching | -6 | **+82** |
| PLCs | **-45** | **+74** |
| Workshops | **-26** | +22 |

**Translation:** Districts are pouring resources into coaching, PLCs, and observation — the formats teachers are LEAST satisfied with. The problem isn't the concept; it's the execution. PLCs without good tools, data, and structure feel like "being held hostage" (direct teacher quote). PLCs WITH those things show dramatically better outcomes.

## The Collaboration Finding That Changes Everything

Teachers in schools with **strong collaboration models** report dramatically higher satisfaction across EVERY measure:

| Metric | Strong Collaboration Schools |
|---|---|
| Overall workflow satisfaction | +47 (net) |
| Perceived PD effectiveness | +59 |
| Effectively use student data | +40 |
| Effectively differentiate instruction | +50 |
| Effectively integrate digital tools | +34 |
| Effectively implement standards | +47 |

**"Strong collaboration" is defined by:**
- Formal collaboration time built into the master schedule
- Shared instructional planning responsibilities
- Positive culture — grade-level/subject-area teams trust and support each other
- Structured agendas and mutual accountability

**But only 7% of schools have this.** The rest describe collaboration as: "Feels like I'm being held hostage," "Not another meeting," "Don't read PowerPoint presentations to me."

## Coaching: Promise vs. Reality

**What research says works:**
- Content-expert coaches (not generalists)
- Intensive frequency (weekly or more)
- Separate from evaluation
- Specific, immediately applicable feedback

**What actually happens:**
- Only 49% of teachers received coaching in the past 12 months
- Only 24% receive it weekly or more
- 60% of coaching time goes to new or struggling teachers (not growth-oriented)
- Coaching carries a stigma — perceived as "last resort" for low performers
- Administrators as coaches face credibility issues: "Most administrators don't have enough classroom seat time to coach teachers"

## Key Barriers to Effective PD

| Barrier | % Calling It "Extremely Limiting" |
|---|---|
| Not enough time in teachers' schedules | 44% |
| Admin tasks prevent leaders focusing on instruction | 42% |
| School leaders lack time to support PD | 29% |
| Insufficient financial resources | 29% |
| PD priorities change too often | 17% |
| Leaders lack coaching/feedback training | 15-22% |

**The market is broken:** Of $18B spent, only $3B comes from external providers. Districts are both purchaser AND supplier. 82% of administrators find vendors through personal networks. Teachers (end users) have almost no say in vendor selection.

## Emerging Technology Solutions (Identified by the Study)

The report flagged six categories of technology innovation addressing teacher PD needs:

1. **Content aggregation platforms** — professional learning and content-specific resources
2. **Video platforms** — PD resources and classroom modeling
3. **Collaboration tools** — sharing lesson plans, materials, and resources
4. **Teacher evaluation platforms** — coaching and effectiveness support
5. **Data analysis tools** — identifying individual student needs
6. **Assessment platforms** — new ways to monitor student progress

**Wayground touches categories 1, 3, 5, and 6.** We are a content platform, a collaboration tool (CA/shared assessments), a data analysis tool (standards mastery, item analytics), and an assessment platform. We don't directly address teacher evaluation or video-based PD.
