## RTI (Response to Intervention)

### What It Is
A multi-tiered framework for identifying and supporting students who are struggling academically. The core principle: don't wait for a student to fail before providing help. Use data to intervene early.

### The Three Tiers

**Tier 1 — Core Instruction (ALL Students)**
- Universal, high-quality classroom instruction
- 80-85% of students should succeed at this tier
- Teacher uses formative assessment to monitor progress
- Differentiated instruction within the general classroom
- If a student isn't responding → move to Tier 2

**Tier 2 — Targeted Intervention (10-15% of Students)**
- Small-group instruction (3-6 students) in addition to Tier 1
- Focused on specific skill gaps identified by data
- Typically 20-40 minutes, 3-5 times per week
- More frequent progress monitoring (biweekly)
- Duration: 8-12 weeks, then reassess
- If student responds → return to Tier 1 monitoring
- If student doesn't respond → move to Tier 3

**Tier 3 — Intensive Intervention (5-10% of Students)**
- Individualized or very small group (1-3 students)
- Intensive, targeted to specific deficits
- Daily progress monitoring
- May involve specialists (reading interventionist, math coach, special ed)
- Duration: varies, often ongoing
- If student still doesn't respond → may trigger special education evaluation

### The Data Engine
RTI runs on data. At every tier, teachers and teams need:
1. **Screening data** — Who needs help? (Universal screener, 3x/year)
2. **Diagnostic data** — What specifically do they need help with?
3. **Progress monitoring data** — Is the intervention working?
4. **Outcome data** — Did they close the gap?

Without reliable, frequent data, the tiers are just labels. This is where formative assessment tools like Wayground become critical infrastructure.

## MTSS (Multi-Tiered System of Supports)

### What It Is
Think of MTSS as RTI 2.0 — expanded and more comprehensive. Same tiered structure, but broader scope.

**RTI** focused primarily on academics (reading and math interventions).

**MTSS** wraps in:
- **Academic supports** (what RTI covered)
- **Behavioral supports** (PBIS — Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports)
- **Social-emotional learning** (SEL programs, mental health supports)
- **Attendance and engagement** monitoring
- **College and career readiness** (in secondary)

### Why Districts Are Shifting to MTSS
- Federal policy (ESSA) encourages "whole child" approaches
- Growing recognition that academic struggles often have behavioral/emotional roots
- Districts want ONE framework, not separate silos for academics vs. behavior
- Board members and superintendents talk about "whole child support" — they mean MTSS

### MTSS in Practice

**At the district level:**
- MTSS coordinator or team oversees framework implementation
- Data systems integrate academic, behavioral, attendance, and SEL data
- Professional development for all staff on tiered supports
- Decision rules for moving students between tiers

**At the school level:**
- Grade-level or department teams meet regularly (often weekly PLC meetings)
- Review screening data, progress monitoring data, behavior data
- Make tier-change decisions as a team (not individual teacher judgment)
- Document interventions and outcomes

**At the classroom level:**
- Teachers deliver high-quality Tier 1 instruction with embedded formative assessment
- Use data to differentiate within the classroom
- Flag students who aren't responding for team review
- Implement accommodations and modifications per student plans

### Key MTSS Components

| Component | Description | Data Source |
|---|---|---|
| Universal screening | Identify at-risk students 3x/year | MAP, iReady, Star, DIBELS |
| Progress monitoring | Track response to intervention | Formative assessments (Wayground CA), curriculum-based measures |
| Data-based decision making | Teams use data to adjust supports | All of the above, aggregated |
| Evidence-based interventions | Use programs with research backing | District-selected intervention programs |
| Fidelity monitoring | Is the intervention being implemented correctly? | Walkthrough data, coach observations |

### Wayground's Role in MTSS
- **Academic data layer:** Our formative assessment data (games, homework, CA) feeds the academic strand of MTSS
- **Engagement signal:** Student participation rates and completion data can serve as early indicators of disengagement (a behavioral/attendance precursor)
- **Progress monitoring tool:** CA on regular cycles gives MTSS teams the frequent data they need without adding a separate progress monitoring platform
- **Accommodation documentation:** Our 25+ accommodations support IEP/504 compliance within the MTSS framework
- **Standards-level reporting:** MTSS teams need to know which specific skills a student is struggling with, not just an overall score. Our standards-based reporting provides that granularity.

## PBIS (Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports)

### What It Is
The behavioral counterpart to academic RTI. Same tiered logic, applied to student behavior.

- **Tier 1:** Schoolwide expectations, positive reinforcement systems, consistent routines (all students)
- **Tier 2:** Check-in/check-out, social skills groups, mentoring (some students)
- **Tier 3:** Individualized behavior plans, wraparound services, functional behavior assessment (few students)

### Why It Matters for Sales
When a district mentions PBIS, they're signaling they think systematically about student support. These are often the same districts implementing MTSS. They value data-driven approaches and are likely receptive to tools that provide structured, ongoing data (like Wayground).

**Wayground connection:** Our gamification and engagement features (points, leaderboards, avatars) align with PBIS Tier 1 positive reinforcement philosophy. Students earn recognition for participation and effort, not just correct answers. This is a subtle but real alignment point when talking to PBIS-minded administrators.

## Progress Monitoring Deep Dive

Progress monitoring is the connective tissue of RTI/MTSS. Without it, tiers are just labels.

### What Good Progress Monitoring Looks Like
- **Frequent:** Weekly or biweekly for Tier 2/3 students (not quarterly)
- **Standardized:** Same type of measure each time so you can track growth
- **Sensitive to change:** Can detect small improvements over short periods
- **Aligned to intervention:** Measures the specific skill being targeted
- **Visualized:** Teachers and teams need to SEE the trend (graph/chart), not just numbers

### Traditional Progress Monitoring Tools
- Curriculum-Based Measurement (CBM): timed probes in reading fluency, math computation
- AIMSweb, DIBELS, easyCBM: dedicated progress monitoring platforms
- Running records (reading), timed math facts

### Where Wayground Fits
Traditional progress monitoring tools are clinical and narrow (1-minute reading probes, timed math facts). They measure a sliver of skill. Wayground provides broader standards-aligned formative data that can serve a similar progress monitoring function:

- **CA administered weekly/biweekly** on targeted standards = progress monitoring on the actual curriculum, not a proxy measure
- **Game-level analytics** show improvement over time on specific question types
- **Standards mastery tracking** shows movement toward proficiency on individual standards

**The honest nuance:** We're not a replacement for CBM-style progress monitoring in a formal MTSS evaluation. But for classroom-level instructional decisions ("is this student improving in fractions?"), our data is more curriculum-aligned and more actionable than a 1-minute computation probe.
