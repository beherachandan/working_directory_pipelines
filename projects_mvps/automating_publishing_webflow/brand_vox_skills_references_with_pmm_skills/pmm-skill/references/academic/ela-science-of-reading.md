## What Is the Science of Reading?

The Science of Reading (SoR) is not a program or curriculum. It's a body of research spanning 50+ years across cognitive science, neuroscience, linguistics, and education that tells us how the brain learns to read.

**Key finding:** Reading is NOT natural. Unlike spoken language (which humans are wired for), reading requires explicit instruction in the code that maps speech sounds to written symbols. The brain must build new neural pathways for reading.

## The 5 Big Ideas of Beginning Reading

These are the evidence-based pillars of effective reading instruction (from the National Reading Panel, 2000, reaffirmed by subsequent research):

### 1. Phonemic Awareness
- The ability to hear, identify, and manipulate individual sounds (phonemes) in spoken words
- Example: knowing that "cat" has three sounds: /k/ /æ/ /t/
- Precursor to phonics; happens orally before connecting to print
- Critical for K-2; foundational for everything that follows

### 2. Phonics
- The relationship between letters (graphemes) and sounds (phonemes)
- Systematic, explicit phonics instruction teaches the alphabetic code
- NOT a program — it's a component of instruction
- The political/pedagogical debate: phonics vs. "whole language" / "balanced literacy" has largely been settled in favor of systematic phonics, but implementation varies widely

### 3. Fluency
- The ability to read text accurately, quickly, and with expression
- Fluency is the bridge between decoding and comprehension
- Without fluency, cognitive resources are consumed by decoding, leaving nothing for meaning-making
- Measured by: words correct per minute (WCPM), prosody (expression)

### 4. Vocabulary
- Understanding word meanings; both breadth (number of words known) and depth (richness of understanding)
- Taught through: explicit instruction, wide reading, context, morphology (word parts)
- Critical gap: low-income students may enter school knowing 30,000 fewer words than peers

### 5. Reading Comprehension
- The ultimate goal: understanding and constructing meaning from text
- Requires all four previous components PLUS background knowledge, inferencing, metacognition
- Strategies: summarizing, questioning, predicting, monitoring, visualizing

## Structured Literacy

Structured Literacy is the instructional approach aligned with SoR research. Endorsed by the International Dyslexia Association (IDA).

**Characteristics:**
- **Systematic** — Skills taught in a logical, sequential order
- **Cumulative** — Each lesson builds on previous ones
- **Explicit** — Teacher directly teaches concepts (no guessing or discovery)
- **Diagnostic** — Instruction is based on assessment data (formative assessment!)
- **Multi-sensory** — Engages visual, auditory, and kinesthetic pathways

**Contrast with "balanced literacy":**
- Balanced literacy often relied on leveled readers, running records, three-cueing (MSV), and discovery-based approaches
- SoR/structured literacy argues these approaches are NOT supported by evidence for beginning readers
- Many districts are in active transition from balanced literacy to structured literacy (this is politically charged in some places)

## The Simple View of Reading

A foundational model: **Reading Comprehension = Decoding × Language Comprehension**

- **Decoding** — Translating print to speech (phonemic awareness + phonics → fluency)
- **Language Comprehension** — Understanding spoken language (vocabulary + background knowledge + syntax + reasoning)

Both are necessary. Neither is sufficient alone. A student who can decode but doesn't understand the words has no comprehension. A student who understands spoken language but can't decode can't read independently.

## The Reading Landscape — What Districts Are Doing

### State Legislation
40+ states have passed SoR-related laws since 2019, typically requiring:
- Evidence-based reading curricula (often must be from an approved list)
- Teacher training in SoR/structured literacy
- Universal screening for reading difficulties (often including dyslexia screening)
- Elimination of three-cueing (MSV) as a primary instructional strategy

### Curriculum Wars
Districts are choosing between curricula that are "SoR-aligned" vs. legacy programs:
- **SoR-aligned:** UFLI Foundations, CKLA (Core Knowledge Language Arts), Wit & Wisdom, EL Education, Amplify CKLA
- **Transitioning:** Many districts using Fountas & Pinnell, Lucy Calkins (Units of Study) are being required to supplement or replace
- **Political sensitivity:** This is deeply emotional for teachers who built careers on balanced literacy. Be aware.

### MTSS-R (MTSS in Reading)
The National Center on Improving Literacy provides an MTSS-R guide for implementing tiered reading supports:
- Tier 1: Core structured literacy instruction for all
- Tier 2: Small group intervention (systematic phonics, fluency practice)
- Tier 3: Intensive, individualized reading intervention
- Progress monitoring through formative assessment at every tier
