## About NCTM

The National Council of Teachers of Mathematics is the world's largest mathematics education organization, with over 40,000 members. They set the direction for math instruction through:
- **Standards documents** (Principles and Standards, Principles to Actions, Catalyzing Change)
- **Position statements** on key issues (assessment, equity, technology, homework)
- **Professional journals** (Mathematics Teacher: Learning & Teaching PK-12, Journal for Research in Mathematics Education)
- **Conferences** (annual + regional, major PD events)
- **Classroom resources** (1,000+ activities, problems of the week, illuminations)

## Key NCTM Frameworks

### Principles and Standards for School Mathematics (2000)
The foundational document. Six principles and five content standards plus five process standards.

**Six Principles:**
1. **Equity** — High expectations and strong support for all students
2. **Curriculum** — Coherent, focused on important mathematics, well-articulated across grades
3. **Teaching** — Understanding what students know and need to learn, then challenging and supporting them
4. **Learning** — Students must learn with understanding, actively building new knowledge
5. **Assessment** — Should support the learning of important mathematics and furnish useful information to teachers and students
6. **Technology** — Essential in teaching and learning mathematics; influences what is taught and enhances learning

**Five Content Standards:** Number & Operations, Algebra, Geometry, Measurement, Data Analysis & Probability

**Five Process Standards:** Problem Solving, Reasoning & Proof, Communication, Connections, Representation

### Principles to Actions: Ensuring Mathematical Success for All (2014)
The action-oriented successor. Defines 8 Mathematics Teaching Practices that effective math instruction requires:

1. **Establish mathematics goals to focus learning** — Clear learning targets, not just "do page 47"
2. **Implement tasks that promote reasoning and problem solving** — High cognitive demand, not just procedures
3. **Use and connect mathematical representations** — Visual, symbolic, contextual, verbal, physical
4. **Facilitate meaningful mathematical discourse** — Students talk about math, not just answer questions
5. **Pose purposeful questions** — Questions that probe thinking, not just check answers
6. **Build procedural fluency from conceptual understanding** — Understand WHY before drilling HOW
7. **Support productive struggle in learning mathematics** — Struggle is learning, not failure
8. **Elicit and use evidence of student thinking** — Formative assessment in real time

### Catalyzing Change Series (2018-2020)
Three books (high school, elementary, middle school) calling for:
- Each student developing a positive mathematical identity
- Eliminating tracking and detracking pathways
- Broadening the purposes of math education beyond college prep
- Creating equitable math structures

### NCTM Position Statements (Selected, Relevant to Wayground)

**On Assessment:**
- Formative assessment should be ongoing and embedded in instruction
- Assessment should go beyond computation to include reasoning, problem-solving, and communication
- Multiple forms of assessment provide a more complete picture than tests alone
- Technology-enhanced items can assess higher-order thinking

**On Technology:**
- Technology is essential, not optional, in modern math education
- Strategic use of technology can support conceptual understanding
- Technology should be used to enhance, not replace, mathematical reasoning
- All students deserve equitable access to technology tools

**On Equity:**
- Every student deserves access to high-quality mathematics instruction
- Tracking perpetuates inequity; heterogeneous grouping with differentiation is preferred
- Cultural responsiveness in mathematics is essential
- Accommodations and supports must be readily available
