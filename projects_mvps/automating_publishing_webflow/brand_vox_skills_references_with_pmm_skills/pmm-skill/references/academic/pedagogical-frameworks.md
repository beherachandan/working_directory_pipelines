## Universal Design for Learning (UDL)

### What It Is
A framework for designing instruction that works for ALL learners from the start, rather than retrofitting accommodations after the fact. Developed by CAST (Center for Applied Special Technology).

### The Three Principles
1. **Multiple Means of Engagement** (the WHY of learning)
   - How do you motivate and sustain student interest?
   - Options for self-regulation, sustaining effort, recruiting interest
   - Relates to: choice, relevance, challenge level, collaboration

2. **Multiple Means of Representation** (the WHAT of learning)
   - How do you present information in different ways?
   - Options for perception, language/symbols, comprehension
   - Relates to: visual, auditory, text, video, manipulatives

3. **Multiple Means of Action & Expression** (the HOW of learning)
   - How do students demonstrate what they know?
   - Options for physical action, expression/communication, executive function
   - Relates to: writing, speaking, drawing, building, performing

### Why It Matters in Sales
UDL is everywhere in education right now. It's in teacher prep programs, PD catalogs, strategic plans, and board resolutions. When a district says "we believe in UDL" or "we follow UDL principles," they're saying: we want tools that offer multiple ways to learn and demonstrate learning.

## Differentiated Instruction (DI)

### What It Is
Adapting instruction to meet individual student needs. Coined and popularized by Carol Ann Tomlinson. Teachers differentiate by:
- **Content** — What students learn (different reading levels, different complexity)
- **Process** — How students learn it (direct instruction vs. inquiry vs. collaboration)
- **Product** — How students show what they know (test vs. project vs. presentation)
- **Learning environment** — Where and how the classroom is organized

### The Difference from UDL
- **UDL** = Design it right from the start for everyone
- **DI** = Adjust during instruction for specific students based on their needs

In practice, most teachers blend both. UDL is the design principle; DI is the daily practice.

### Wayground Connection

- **Accommodations:** 25+ options allow per-student adjustments without creating separate assignments
- **Data-driven grouping:** CA and game analytics help teachers identify which students need which differentiation

## Standards-Based Grading (SBG)

### What It Is
A grading system where students are evaluated on their mastery of specific standards rather than accumulating points through homework, participation, and tests.

**Traditional grading:** 45% tests + 25% homework + 20% quizzes + 10% participation = letter grade
**Standards-based:** Each standard is rated independently (e.g., 4 = exceeds, 3 = meets, 2 = approaching, 1 = below)

### Why It's Relevant
Districts adopting SBG need assessment tools that report at the standard level, not just an overall score. They need to know: "Did this student master standard 4.NF.1 (equivalent fractions)?" not "Did this student get a B on the fractions test?"

### Wayground Connection
- **Standards-tagged content:** Every item can be mapped to specific standards
- **Standards mastery reporting:** CA and game reports break down performance by individual standard
- **Reassessment friendly:** Students can retake or practice specific standards (mastery mode) without retaking an entire test
- **CA for SBG schools:** Common assessments aligned to specific standards, with standard-level reporting across classrooms — exactly what SBG schools need

## Competency-Based Education (CBE)

### What It Is
Students advance when they demonstrate mastery, not when the calendar says to move on. Also called mastery-based learning, proficiency-based education.

**Key principles:**
- Students advance upon demonstrated mastery
- Explicit, measurable learning objectives
- Assessment is meaningful and positive (shows what students CAN do)
- Students receive timely, differentiated support
- Learning outcomes emphasize competencies (applied skills, not just knowledge)

### Why It's Growing
- Several states (NH, VT, ME, CO, ID) have adopted CBE frameworks
- ESSA allows for innovative assessment pilots that align with CBE
- Appeals to districts frustrated with seat-time requirements and social promotion
- Connects to personalized learning movements

### Wayground Connection
- **Mastery Mode and Mastery Peak:** Students work until they demonstrate mastery, not just complete an assignment
- **Standards-level tracking:** Progress measured against specific competencies/standards
- **Self-paced options:** Students can move at their own speed
- **Reassessment:** Built into the platform (students can retry without teacher creating a new assignment)

## Professional Learning Communities (PLCs)

### What It Is
Teams of educators who meet regularly to:
1. Clarify what students should learn (standards)
2. Monitor each student's learning through common assessments
3. Provide systematic interventions and enrichment
4. Build a collaborative culture focused on results

Popularized by Richard DuFour. The four essential PLC questions:
1. What do we want students to learn?
2. How will we know if they've learned it?
3. What will we do when they haven't learned it?
4. What will we do when they already know it?

### Why It Matters for Sales
PLCs are the operational backbone of how many schools function. Teachers in PLCs:
- Create and share common assessments (CA is literally built for this)
- Compare student results across classrooms
- Make collaborative decisions about reteaching and intervention
- Need data that's comparable across teachers/classrooms

### Wayground Connection
- **Common Assessments (CA)** was designed for PLC workflows. Same assessment, multiple classrooms, comparable data.
- **Standards-level reporting** answers PLC question #2 directly
- **Cross-classroom comparison** helps teachers identify effective practices (if Ms. Smith's students all mastered fractions and Mr. Jones's didn't, what's Ms. Smith doing differently?)
- **Shared content library** lets PLC teams build and share resources

**Key talking point:** "CA was built for how PLCs actually work. One assessment, multiple classrooms, instant comparable data. No more waiting two weeks for hand-scored results."

## Backward Design (Understanding by Design / UbD)

### What It Is
Curriculum design framework by Grant Wiggins and Jay McTighe. Design instruction by starting with the end goal, not the activity.

**Three stages:**
1. **Identify desired results** — What should students understand and be able to do?
2. **Determine acceptable evidence** — How will you know they've learned it? (assessment design)
3. **Plan learning experiences** — What instruction will get them there?

### Why It Matters
Most teachers plan forward (Monday's lesson → Tuesday's lesson → Friday's test). Backward design flips it: decide what mastery looks like FIRST, then design the assessment, then design the instruction.

### Wayground Connection
- Teachers who use backward design need assessment tools that align directly to their learning targets — not generic quizzes
- Our platform lets teachers create assessments aligned to specific standards FIRST, then use that data to plan instruction
- CA supports backward design at scale: a PLC team agrees on the standard, creates the assessment, then each teacher designs their path to get students there

## Quick Reference: Framework Signals in Conversation

| When You Hear... | They Mean... | Framework |
|---|---|---|
| "We follow UDL principles" | We want tools that work for all learners without retrofitting | UDL |
| "We differentiate instruction" | We adapt teaching to individual needs | DI |
| "We're moving to standards-based grading" | We need standard-level reporting, not just percentages | SBG |
| "We're exploring competency-based" | Students advance by mastery, not seat time | CBE |
| "Our PLCs meet weekly" | Teacher teams share assessments and data collaboratively | PLC |
| "We use backward design" | Assessment drives instruction, not the other way around | UbD |
| "We believe in the whole child" | Academics + behavior + SEL + wellness | MTSS/UDL |
| "Personalized learning" | Could mean DI, CBE, adaptive tech, or all of the above | Ask what they mean specifically |
