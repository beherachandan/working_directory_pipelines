## 1. US Education System Overview

### Grade Structure
K–12 system (Kindergarten through 12th grade):
- **Elementary (K–5):** Ages 5–11
- **Middle School (6–8):** Ages 11–14
- **High School (9–12):** Ages 14–18 (Freshman/Sophomore/Junior/Senior)

| Grade Level | Key Details |
|---|---|
| K–2 (Early Elementary) | Focus on foundational literacy and numeracy. Phonics, reading fluency, number sense. |
| 3–5 (Upper Elementary) | State testing begins (ELA + Math). Reading comprehension, fractions, writing development. |
| 6–8 (Middle School) | Separate building, multiple teachers per student. Pre-algebra/algebra readiness critical. |
| 9–12 (High School) | Course selection, graduation requirements, SAT/ACT prep. |

### School Types

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Public School | Free, government-funded. ~90% of students. Assigned by geographic zone. |
| Charter School | Publicly funded but independently operated. No selective admissions. Decide tech purchases independently. |
| Magnet School | Public school with specialized focus (STEM, arts). May draw across district lines. |
| Private School | Tuition-funded. Not bound by state curriculum. Includes religious schools. |
| Title I School | Public school receiving extra federal funding for high-poverty populations. A funding designation, not a type. |

**SALES TIP:** Public schools buy through districts; charters often decide independently. Always ask which type.

### Standards & Testing
Every state sets its own academic standards. There is NO single national curriculum. Under ESSA, students are tested annually in ELA and Math (grades 3–8 + once in HS) and Science (once per grade band).

| Concept | What to Know |
|---|---|
| State Standards | Each state has its own. Even Common Core states may brand differently. Always ask WHICH state. |
| Standards-Aligned | Content maps to specific state standards. CRITICAL for ed-tech sales. |
| NGSS | Next Generation Science Standards. K–12 science, adopted by many states. |
| Common Core | Shared math/ELA standards, ~41 states. Controversial in some regions. |
| State Testing | Annual spring tests determine school ratings and trigger intervention. |
| SAT/ACT | College entrance exams. Some states use SAT as their state test. |

**SALES TIP:** "Is Wayground standards-aligned?" means: does content map to THEIR state's specific standards. Top buying criterion.

### Small Districts vs. Large Districts
**This is the single most important variable in US ed-tech sales.** 70% of districts have <2,500 students. Only ~5% have 10,000+.

| Dimension | Large District (10K+) | Small District (<2,500) |
|---|---|---|
| Decision-maker | Committee: Curriculum Dir + IT Dir + Principal | Principal or Superintendent (1–2 people) |
| Sales cycle | 6–18 months | 2–8 weeks |
| Pilot required? | Almost always | Sometimes; often straight to purchase |
| Stakeholders | 5–10 | 1–3 |
| IT Director exists? | Yes — dedicated gatekeeper | No — Principal handles tech |
| Curriculum Dir exists? | Yes — controls instructional budget | No — Principal decides curriculum |
| Best entry point | Teacher → Coach → Curriculum Director | Principal directly |
| How it feels | Enterprise B2B sales | Relationship-based, faster |

### Data Privacy & Compliance

| Law/Requirement | What to Know |
|---|---|
| FERPA | Federal law protecting student education records. Vendors must sign a DPA. |
| COPPA | Protects online privacy of children under 13. Schools consent on behalf of parents. |
| DPA | Data Privacy Agreement — legal contract for handling student data. Required before data flows. |
| State Privacy Laws | Many states have additional laws (e.g., California SOPIPA, New York Ed Law 2-d). |

**SALES TIP:** "Can you sign our DPA?" = buying signal. They're seriously evaluating you.

## 2. Critical US Education Terms

### ESSA (Every Student Succeeds Act)
- **Definition:** Federal law (2015) requiring annual testing in ELA/Math (grades 3–8 + HS) and Science (once per band).
- **In practice:** Results determine school ratings, trigger interventions, affect funding.
- **Who cares:** Superintendents (careers depend on scores), Curriculum Directors, Teachers (feel daily pressure).
- **Wayground fit:** Standards-aligned practice embedded in regular instruction. "Test prep that doesn't feel like test prep."
- **Buying signal:** ESSA accountability, state ratings, or CSI/TSI designation = motivated buyers.

### MTSS (Multi-Tiered System of Supports)
- **Definition:** Framework for student support: Tier 1 (all students), Tier 2 (targeted 15–20%), Tier 3 (intensive 5%).
- **In practice:** Universal screening 3x/year. MTSS teams meet regularly. Every decision documented. MASSIVE data needs.
- **Wayground fit:** Universal screening, Tier 2 intervention practice, built-in progress monitoring, auto-documentation.
- **Buying signal:** "We're implementing MTSS" = one of the strongest buying signals.

### IEP (Individualized Education Program)
- **Definition:** Legally binding document for students with disabilities under IDEA. ~15% of US students have IEPs.
- **In practice:** Schools LEGALLY REQUIRED to provide every listed service. Non-compliance leads to lawsuits.
- **Wayground fit:** Differentiation features (different difficulty, extended time, TTS) directly serve IEP requirements. Compliance selling point.

### 504 Plan
- **Definition:** Accommodation plan under Section 504. Less intensive than IEP. About 3–5% of students.
- **Wayground fit:** Invisible differentiation — dignity preserved while legal requirements met.

### Formative Assessment
- **Definition:** Ongoing checks during instruction. Low-stakes, frequent, adjust teaching in real time.
- **In practice:** Bell ringers, exit tickets, polling, quick quizzes.
- **Wayground fit:** This IS Wayground's core use case.

### Standards Alignment
- **Critical nuance:** "Aligned to Common Core" ≠ aligned to every state. Texas uses TEKS (different). Always ask WHICH state.

### Subgroup Accountability
- **Definition:** Under ESSA, scores reported by race/ethnicity, income, EL status, disability.
- **Wayground fit:** Reporting by subgroup is extremely valuable. Show a principal performance by specific populations.

### Tool Consolidation
- Average US school uses 1,400+ tools. Districts actively want FEWER platforms.
- **Wayground fit:** One platform for assessment, practice, review, test prep, engagement, differentiation across every subject.

### Additional Key Terms

| Term | Quick Definition |
|---|---|
| RTI | Response to Intervention — predecessor to MTSS. Many use RTI/MTSS interchangeably. |
| ELL / ML | English Language Learners / Multilingual Learners. ~10% of US students. Translation features key. |
| CTE | Career & Technical Education. 85% of HS students take CTE. Federally funded (Perkins V). Underserved market. |
| SEL | Social-Emotional Learning. Growing but politically sensitive in some regions. |
| PLC | Professional Learning Community. Teacher teams analyzing data. Wayground data for PLCs very valuable. |
| Title I | Federal funding for high-poverty schools. Extra money AND extra accountability. |
| CSI / TSI | Schools flagged low-performing under ESSA. Extra funding + pressure. Strong buying signal. |
| SIS | Student Information System (PowerSchool, Infinite Campus). Core district database. |
| LMS | Learning Management System (Google Classroom, Canvas, Schoology). Integration expected. |
| SSO | Single Sign-On (Clever, ClassLink). Table stakes for IT Directors. |
| Chromebook | Dominant student device in US K–12. Products MUST work on Chrome OS. |
| DPA | Data Privacy Agreement. Required before student data flows. |

## 3. Subject-by-Subject Guide

### Subject Priority Hierarchy

| Subject | US Priority | Wayground Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| ELA | HIGHEST | Core: reading comprehension, vocabulary, grammar, writing scaffolds |
| Math | HIGHEST | Core: fact fluency, equation editor, mastery mode, gap analysis |
| Science | High | Strong: lab pre-work, vocabulary, CER scaffolds, NGSS assessment |
| Social Studies | Medium | Growing: primary source analysis, DBQ support, critical thinking |
| CTE | Growing | Opportunity: certification prep, safety training, industry vocabulary |
| World Languages | Medium | Differentiator: all 4 domains (read, write, speak, listen) in one tool |
| Arts/Music/PE | Required | Video submission, theory quizzes, digital portfolios |

### ELA (English Language Arts)
**Elementary (K–5):**
- 90–120 min/day — largest instructional block
- Key: Guided reading station rotations — one station runs Wayground while teacher works with small group
- **SALES TIP:** Show Wayground keeping 20 kids engaged while teacher works with 5 = you win

**Secondary (6–12):**
- 5–6 periods, 25–30 students each. GRADING ESSAYS = most time-consuming task
- **SALES TIP:** Ask "How much time do you spend grading?" — immediate engagement

### Math
**Elementary (K–5):**
- Fact fluency, visual models, fractions (#1 stumbling block)
- **SALES TIP:** Lead with "show the work, not just the answer." US math has moved away from timed tests.

**Secondary (6–12):**
- Algebra readiness = critical gateway. Students 2–3 grade levels behind in same class as advanced
- **SALES TIP:** For admins: "Close the algebra gap before it becomes a crisis." Algebra I is most consequential single course.

### Science
- Elementary squeezed: only 30–45 min, 2–4x/week
- Secondary: NGSS three-dimensional assessment, CER writing, AP prep
- **SALES TIP:** Lead with NGSS alignment and three-dimensional assessment. Differentiates from flashcard competitors.

### Social Studies
- **SALES TIP:** Social studies teachers are often most enthusiastic adopters — underserved and hungry for tools. Great internal champions.

### CTE, World Languages & Specials
- **CTE:** 85% of HS students take CTE. Perkins V funded. Ask about CTE in every HS conversation.
- **Specials (Art/Music/PE):** See EVERY student. Winning the music teacher = 500+ students weekly. Hidden adoption multiplier.

### Cross-Cutting Use Cases

| Use Case | Key Messaging |
|---|---|
| Test Prep | Peak Jan–Apr. "State-aligned practice embedded in regular instruction." |
| Differentiation | Legally required for IEP/504. "Same assignment, different difficulty — students don't know." |
| Student Engagement | Teacher priority #1. "Engagement that lasts all year, not just the first week." |
| Tool Consolidation | Admin priority. "One platform across every subject, replacing 5–10 point solutions." |
| MTSS/RTI | "Screening, intervention, progress monitoring, documentation — all built into regular instruction." |

## 4. Buyer Personas

### PERSONA 1: The Educator (Teacher)
- 3.7 million in the US. The end user and champion.
- **Primary goal:** Keep students engaged while tracking progress and meeting standards
- **Decision power:** Champions and evaluators, NOT the buyer. Recommend tools upward.
- **Sales approach:** Win teachers first. Help them see time savings + engagement, then help them advocate upward.

### PERSONA 2: The Principal (School Leader)
- Results-driven. In small districts = THE buyer.
- **Key question:** "Show me the data that proves this helps students perform better on state assessments."
- **Large district:** Influencer. **Small district:** Decision-maker. Bring pricing and timeline.

### PERSONA 3: The Curriculum Administrator
- Standards-obsessed. Does NOT exist in small districts.
- **Non-negotiable:** Standards alignment. If content doesn't map, conversation ends immediately.
- **Decision power:** Key decision-maker for district instructional tool purchases.

### PERSONA 4: The IT Director
- Gatekeeper. Does NOT exist in small districts.
- **Key question:** "Does it integrate with Clever? Is it FERPA compliant? Can you sign our DPA?"
- **Sales approach:** Have integration and privacy answers BEFORE the call. Lead with consolidation.

| Standards alignment | Important | Important | CRITICAL | Moderate |
| Student engagement | CRITICAL | Important | Moderate | Low |

## 6. Quick-Reference Cheat Sheet

| They Say... | They Mean... | Sales Signal |
|---|---|---|
| "Our district" | Group of schools under one admin body | Ask about size to calibrate |
| "We're a Title I school" | High % low-income, extra federal funding | Have dedicated money AND higher accountability |
| "I need it standards-aligned" | Content must map to their state's standards | Non-negotiable. Show standards filtering immediately |
| "Does it integrate with Google Classroom?" | Can it connect to our LMS? | Confirm LMS integration |
| "We're 1:1 with Chromebooks" | Every student has a Chromebook | Confirm Chrome OS support. Table stakes. |
| "I have IEP/504 kids" | Students with legally mandated accommodations | Lead with differentiation — compliance selling point |
| "We need FERPA compliance" | Product must protect student data | Have compliance docs ready. Gatekeeper requirement. |
| "We're doing a pilot" | Trial before district purchase | Ask about success metrics upfront |
| "The board needs to approve it" | School Board must vote (usually $50K+) | Prepare ROI materials for non-educators |
| "We use bell ringers and exit tickets" | Quick formative assessments | This IS Wayground's core use case |
| "We're in MTSS" | Tiered support system | Major buying signal — they need data tools |
| "We need to close the gap" | A subgroup is underperforming | Show subgroup reporting and filtering |
| "We're looking to consolidate" | Too many ed-tech tools | Wayground's sweet spot |
| "We're a CSI school" | Flagged as low-performing under ESSA | Strong buying signal. They NEED improvement tools. |
| "Show me the data" | Need evidence of outcomes | Come with case studies and outcome metrics |
| "What does implementation look like?" | How will you help us roll out? | They're seriously evaluating. Bring deployment plan. |
| "We're in testing season" | State tests coming (Mar–May). High-stress. | Lead with test prep. Be sensitive to time constraints. |
| "Can you sign our DPA?" | Data Privacy Agreement required | Buying signal. Have DPA process ready. |
| "We need PD" | Professional Development (teacher training) | Budget for this in your proposal. Expected, not optional. |
