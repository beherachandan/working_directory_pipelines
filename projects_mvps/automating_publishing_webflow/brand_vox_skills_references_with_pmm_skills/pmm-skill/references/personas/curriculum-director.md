# Persona: Curriculum & Instruction Director

## Role & Title
- **Title:** Director of Curriculum & Instruction (also: Asst. Superintendent of C&I, Instructional Coach Lead)
- **Level:** Senior administrator, reports to superintendent
- **Scope:** Standards alignment, assessment strategy, instructional quality, PD

## Primary Concerns
1. **Standards alignment** — ensuring instruction maps to state/national standards
2. **Assessment strategy** — formative vs. summative, benchmark testing, Common Assessment
3. **Instructional quality** — evidence-based practices, differentiated instruction
4. **Teacher professional development** — training, adoption, capacity building
5. **Data-informed instruction** — using assessment data to drive teaching decisions

## Language They Use
- "Standards-aligned" / "curriculum-mapped"
- "Formative assessment" / "benchmark data"
- "Differentiated instruction" / "tiered intervention"
- "Professional learning community" (PLC)
- "Data-driven instruction" / "instructional cycle"
- "Fidelity of implementation"
- "Scope and sequence"

## Decision Authority
- **Can approve:** Instructional tool evaluations, pilot programs, PD budget allocation
- **Can recommend:** District-wide adoption to superintendent
- **Needs escalation:** District-wide contracts go to superintendent/board
- **Delegates to:** Instructional coaches (classroom evaluation), department heads (subject-specific review)
- **Influenced by:** Teacher feedback, assessment data, state standards changes

## Objection Patterns
- "How does this align with our adopted curriculum?"
- "Can teachers create assessments aligned to our specific standards?"
- "We need Common Assessment capabilities across buildings"
- "Does it support differentiated instruction and accommodations?"
- "How does the data integrate with our SIS/LMS?"
- "We already have an assessment platform"

## Win Triggers
- **Assessment need:** District is building or revamping formative assessment strategy
- **Common Assessment:** Need to standardize assessments across buildings (CA feature)
- **Standards adoption:** New state standards requiring new instructional tools
- **Teacher-created content:** Teachers want to build their own assessments, not just use publisher content
- **Differentiation mandate:** Focus on IEP/504 accommodations and tiered instruction
- **Data gaps:** Current tools don't provide the instructional data they need

## Data Points That Matter
Lead with instructional metrics:
- **Assessment activity** — quizzes created, questions authored, CA usage
- **Standards coverage** — content mapped to state standards
- **Teacher-created vs. library content** ratio (shows teacher investment)
- **Accommodation usage** — differentiation features adopted
- **Cross-building adoption** — which schools are using, consistency metrics
- **Student performance data** — completion rates, accuracy trends

## Problem Statements (from their perspective)
- "Teachers find random worksheets online instead of our adopted curriculum"
- "Digital resources don't align with our scope and sequence"
- "Curriculum fidelity is impossible with our current free-for-all approach"
- "PD focuses on tool features instead of instructional best practices"
- "Curriculum coaches spend time troubleshooting tech instead of instruction"
- "Assessment data is fragmented across platforms"

## How to Engage
- Start with their assessment and curriculum priorities
- Show how Wayground fits into their instructional cycle
- Demonstrate Common Assessment workflow
- Highlight teacher-created content capabilities
- Provide differentiation and accommodation features
- Offer to align demo to their specific standards/curriculum
- Connect to PLC workflows — how teacher teams use the data

## Pillar Routing
- **Primary:** PLAN (curriculum alignment, BYOC, district content library)
- **Secondary:** MEASURE (standards mastery, CA analytics, cross-school data)
- **When they say:** "Teachers are going off-curriculum" → Lead with PLAN
- **When they say:** "We need common assessments" → Lead with MEASURE
- **Don't say:** "Save teacher time" (wrong priority for this persona — they care about quality and alignment)
