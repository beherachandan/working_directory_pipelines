# Persona: Instructional Coach / Technology Coach

## Role & Title
- **Title:** Instructional Coach, Technology Coach, Digital Learning Specialist, Innovation Coach, TOSA (Teacher on Special Assignment)
- **Level:** Building or district-level support role — critical influencer
- **Scope:** Teacher PD, tool recommendations, pilot selection, instructional quality, adoption support
- **Nickname:** "The Influencer"

**Why This Persona Matters:**
Instructional coaches drive a huge percentage of district tool decisions. They act as confidants for admin on best practices. If a coach is excited about Wayground, we become a recommended solution — not a random vendor. They also make or break adoption success post-purchase.

## Primary Concerns
1. **Instructional quality & consistency** — getting all teachers using effective practices
2. **Tool consolidation** — can't keep supporting 7+ platforms across 120+ teachers
3. **Teacher adoption** — tools get abandoned after initial training
4. **Measurable impact** — need to prove they're improving instruction, not just managing tech
5. **PD bandwidth** — limited staff to support growing tech stack + district initiatives

## Language They Use
- "We need something teachers will actually use"
- "We can't keep supporting 7 different tools"
- "I spend all my time troubleshooting tech instead of supporting instruction"
- "By the time I'm expert in one tool, we've adopted three more"
- "Teachers abandon tools after training because ongoing support isn't there"
- "Success stories don't translate because everyone uses different tools"
- "We measure usage, not impact — and neither looks good"
- "I know what teachers need, but I can't get to them all"

## Decision Authority
- **Can approve:** Nothing directly (rarely has budget authority)
- **Can influence:** ENORMOUS — drives tool recommendations, pilot selections, admin narrative (good and bad)
- **Can champion:** Recommend to curriculum director, principal, superintendent — their word carries weight
- **Can block:** Vocal criticism or lack of support kills adoption dead
- **Influenced by:** Teacher feedback, peer coaches, conferences, EdTech communities
- **Key insight:** They lack direct managerial authority but are still responsible for learning outcomes

## Objection Patterns
- "I support 120 teachers across 7 different platforms — it's impossible to add another"
- "Teachers will abandon this after training like they do everything else"
- "How do I support this without becoming a help desk?"
- "We need depth, not breadth — I can't go shallow on 47 tools"
- "Can I measure instructional impact, not just logins and usage?"
- "This is easy to learn" (they need depth, not simplicity)

## Win Triggers
- **Tool fatigue:** Currently supporting too many platforms, looking to consolidate
- **Adoption failure:** Previous tool rollout failed due to complexity or lack of engagement
- **Impact mandate:** Being asked to prove coaching impact with data
- **Progressive complexity:** Need a tool with low entry but deep ceiling
- **Teacher love:** Teachers already using Wayground voluntarily — coach wants to formalize
- **PD efficiency:** One platform to go deep on beats shallow support across many

## Data Points That Matter
Lead with coaching and adoption metrics:
- **Teacher adoption depth** — not just "used it once" but games per teacher, content created
- **Building-wide adoption** — percentage of teachers active, growth over time
- **Feature progression** — teachers moving from basic to advanced features
- **Impact correlation** — usage tied to student outcome improvements
- **Cross-teacher comparison** — which teachers create best practices worth scaling
- **Implementation metrics** — adoption by department, subject, grade level

## Value Proposition Mapping

| Value Prop | Key Features | What to Say | What to Show |
|---|---|---|---|
| One Platform to Champion | Comprehensive functionality, progressive complexity, all subjects/grades | "Stop supporting 47 different tools. Train teachers deeply on one platform." | Full feature walkthrough; Subject-specific use cases; Progressive feature path |
| Drive Adoption Through Quick Wins | Intuitive interface, ready-to-use content, AI Create, gamification | "Give me 30 minutes and I'll show you a tool teachers implement successfully the next day." | 5-minute quickstart; AI Create demo; Immediate engagement |
| Scale Best Practices Building-Wide | Shared resource libraries, district content curation, coach dashboards, implementation metrics | "See which teachers create magic and share their resources school-wide." | Coach dashboard; Shared library; Usage reports |
| Prove Your Impact | Usage-to-outcome data, teacher progression tracking, adoption metrics, engagement analytics | "You finally measure impact, not just usage." | Impact dashboard; Teacher progression; Building-wide metrics |

## How to Engage
- **Position as their force multiplier** — Wayground makes their coaching more effective
- Start with consolidation: "One platform to go deep on"
- Show progressive complexity: low entry for resistant teachers, depth for power users
- Demonstrate the coach dashboard: what they'll see without extra effort
- Provide quick-win stories: "Give me 30 minutes and I'll show you a tool teachers implement successfully the next day"
- Emphasize impact over usage: "You finally measure impact, not just logins"
- Share exemplar flows: see which teachers create magic and share resources building-wide
- Connect to their PD workflow: how teams use Wayground data in PLCs

## Champion Enablement
When an instructional coach champions Wayground:
1. **Coach dashboard access** — show them adoption data across their building
2. **Quick-start PD template** — 30-minute session they can run with teachers
3. **Progressive complexity pathway** — tiered adoption plan (basic → intermediate → advanced)
4. **Impact evidence kit** — usage-to-outcome data they can share with admin
5. **Peer coach network** — connect with other Wayground coach champions
6. **Best practice library** — exemplar resources from top teachers in their building to scale

## Pillar Routing
- **Primary:** PLAN (consolidation, one platform to champion)
- **Secondary:** MEASURE (prove coaching impact with data)
- **When they say:** "I spend all my time troubleshooting" → Lead with PLAN
- **When they say:** "We measure usage, not impact" → Lead with MEASURE
