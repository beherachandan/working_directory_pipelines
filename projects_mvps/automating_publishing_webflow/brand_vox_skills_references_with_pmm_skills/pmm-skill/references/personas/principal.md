# Persona: School Principal

## Role & Title
- **Title:** Principal (also: Assistant Principal, Dean of Instruction)
- **Level:** Building-level administrator
- **Scope:** School-level decisions, teacher management, student outcomes, parent relations

## Primary Concerns
1. **Teacher adoption & morale** — tools that teachers actually use and like
2. **Student engagement** — participation, behavior, motivation metrics
3. **School-level data** — actionable insights for their building
4. **Ease of implementation** — minimal training burden, quick wins
5. **Parent communication** — being able to show what's happening in classrooms

## Language They Use
- "Teacher buy-in" / "teacher voice"
- "Classroom walkthrough data"
- "Student engagement" / "on-task behavior"
- "Building-level" / "our school"
- "Low lift" / "easy to implement"
- "Quick wins"
- "Already using it" / "teachers love it"

## Decision Authority
- **Can approve:** School-level purchases (typically under $5K-$10K), pilot participation
- **Can recommend:** District-wide adoption to curriculum director/superintendent
- **Can champion:** Push up to district leadership based on building success
- **Influenced by:** Teacher satisfaction, student response, peer principal feedback

## Objection Patterns
- "My teachers are already overwhelmed with tools"
- "Will this actually get used, or is it another shelfware?"
- "I need to see it working in my school first"
- "How much training does this require?"
- "What data will I see as an admin?"
- "Some of my teachers aren't tech-savvy"

## Win Triggers
- **Teacher enthusiasm:** Teachers already using free version and vocal about it
- **Visible engagement:** Student participation data that's easy to share with parents/district
- **Low implementation friction:** Minimal training, teachers self-adopt
- **Admin dashboard:** Building-level usage and performance data
- **Peer validation:** Another school in the district (or similar school) seeing results
- **Quick proof:** Can show impact within weeks, not months

## Data Points That Matter
Lead with building-level metrics:
- **Active teachers** at their specific school (from `clean.user` + `clean.org`)
- **Games per teacher** — adoption depth, not just breadth
- **Student participation** — players per game, completion rates
- **Usage trend** — is adoption growing or flat?
- **Feature adoption** — which premium features teachers are hitting paywalls on
- **Peer school comparison** — other schools in their district using Wayground

## Problem Statements (from their perspective)
- "No visibility into what's happening in classrooms day-to-day"
- "Achievement gaps are widening despite technology investments"
- "Teachers are burning out from technology fatigue"
- "Too many disconnected platforms — paying for 12 different tools"
- "I can't demonstrate improvement to the school board without unified data"
- "We aren't sure how well teachers implement 504/IEP accommodations in gen ed classrooms"

## How to Engage
- Start with what their teachers are already doing (usage data)
- Frame as "supporting what's already working" not "adding a new tool"
- Show the admin view — what they'll see without extra effort
- Keep it practical — demos should be classroom-realistic
- Offer a school-level pilot with clear success metrics
- Connect teacher enthusiasm to their evaluation goals

## Pillar Routing
- **Primary:** MEASURE (school-wide outcomes, visibility, ESSA evidence)
- **Secondary:** TEACH (equity, accommodations, engagement)
- **When they say:** "I need to prove ROI to the board" → Lead with MEASURE
- **When they say:** "Teachers are overwhelmed with tools" → Lead with PLAN (consolidation)
- **Don't say:** Feature lists or technical details (wrong lens for principals)
