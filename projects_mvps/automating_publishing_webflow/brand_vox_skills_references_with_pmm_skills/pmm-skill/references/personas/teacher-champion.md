# Persona: Teacher Champion

## Role & Title
- **Title:** Teacher (any subject/grade), Instructional Coach, Department Head
- **Level:** Classroom practitioner — THE most important persona
- **Scope:** Classroom decisions, peer influence, grassroots advocacy

**Why This Persona Matters Most:**
Teacher requests are the #1 reason deals close. Of 1,030 closed-won deals, 619 (60%) were driven by teacher champions requesting the paid version. This is the engine of Wayground's GTM.

## Primary Concerns
1. **Student engagement** — keeping students motivated and participating
2. **Time savings** — less time on grading, more time on teaching
3. **Content quality** — access to good questions and ability to create their own
4. **Ease of use** — works in their existing workflow without friction
5. **Premium features** — specific features they've hit paywalls on and want

## Language They Use
- "My students love it" / "they actually want to do the review"
- "Saves me hours" / "I used to spend my weekends grading"
- "I can see who gets it and who doesn't"
- "The kids ask for it" / "reward time"
- "I wish I could..." (feature requests = buying signals)
- "My department/team all uses it"

## Decision Authority
- **Can approve:** Nothing (rarely has budget authority)
- **Can influence:** EVERYTHING — teacher requests drive 60% of closed-won deals
- **Can champion:** Request to principal → curriculum director → superintendent
- **Can block:** Refuse to adopt, vocal criticism kills deals
- **Influenced by:** Peer teachers, social media (EdTech Twitter/TikTok), conferences

## Objection Patterns
- "The free version does what I need" (→ show what they're missing)
- "I don't want to ask my admin for another thing" (→ make it easy for them to champion)
- "My school won't pay for it" (→ frame the teacher request + data for their admin)
- "I've already built all my content in [competitor]" (→ import/migration support)
- "I'm not sure my students' data is safe" (→ privacy/security resources)

## Win Triggers
- **Paywall frustration:** Hit premium feature limits repeatedly (tracked in product data)
- **Peer adoption:** Other teachers in their school/department using paid version
- **Feature need:** Specific premium feature critical to their workflow (accommodations, reports, CA)
- **Admin support:** Their principal or curriculum director is receptive
- **Content investment:** Created significant content on the platform (switching cost = high)
- **Student response:** Students actively requesting to use Wayground in class

## Data Points That Matter
Lead with classroom-level metrics:
- **Games run** — how actively they're using the platform
- **Students reached** — total unique players in their games
- **Content created** — quizzes and questions authored (investment indicator)
- **Paywall hits** — which premium features they've tried to access
- **Peer usage** — other teachers at their school on the platform
- **Engagement metrics** — average completion rate, student participation

## How to Engage
- **Don't sell to them — empower them to champion**
- Acknowledge they're already a power user
- Show them what premium unlocks for their specific workflow
- Give them "the email to send their admin" — make championing easy
- Provide data they can share: "X students, Y games, Z time saved"
- Connect them with other teacher champions (community)
- Make them feel valued — they ARE the GTM engine

## Champion Enablement
When a teacher champion is identified, equip them with:
1. **Usage summary** they can forward to admin (auto-generated from their data)
2. **ROI talking points** framed for admin audience
3. **Peer evidence** — other teachers/schools in their district
4. **Simple ask template** — email/conversation framework for requesting purchase
5. **Quick wins** — premium features they'll see value from immediately
