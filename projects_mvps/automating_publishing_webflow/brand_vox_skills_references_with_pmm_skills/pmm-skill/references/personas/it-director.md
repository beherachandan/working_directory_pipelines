# Persona: IT / EdTech Director

## Role & Title
- **Title:** Director of Instructional Technology, Director of EdTech, Digital Learning Director, Chief Technology Officer (CTO), IT Director
- **Level:** District-level — key technical decision-maker and gatekeeper
- **Scope:** Tech stack management, security/compliance, SSO/integrations, procurement approval, adoption analytics
- **Nickname:** "The Risk Manager"

**Why This Persona Matters:**
Their "no" can kill a deal regardless of teacher love or admin support. They control procurement, security approval, and technical vetting. But if they champion Wayground as a consolidation play, they become a powerful ally who accelerates the deal. They typically manage 5-20 tech coaches and oversee 2,700+ tools per year in an average district.

## Primary Concerns
1. **Tool proliferation & security** — 2,700+ tools/year creates massive attack surface and compliance risk
2. **Integration requirements** — SSO, LMS, SIS, roster sync, grade passback
3. **Data privacy & compliance** — COPPA, FERPA, state-level student privacy laws
4. **Budget waste on shelfware** — paying for tools nobody uses
5. **Adoption analytics** — connecting technology use to measurable outcomes
6. **Support burden** — limited staff to support growing tech stack

## Language They Use
- "We already have too many tools"
- "Does this integrate with our LMS?"
- "What data are you collecting?"
- "SSO integration is a nightmare with our vendor mix"
- "Each new platform is another security vulnerability"
- "Account provisioning for thousands of users across platforms is unsustainable"
- "Help desk tickets are mostly password resets and login issues"
- "Teachers bypass IT approval, creating bigger problems"
- "What's the adoption rate? Are people actually using what we paid for?"

## Decision Authority
- **Can approve:** Technical procurement, security review, integration requirements
- **Can block:** Can kill any deal on security, compliance, or integration grounds
- **Can champion:** Consolidation deals that reduce tool count and complexity
- **Needs escalation:** Budget approval goes to superintendent for large contracts
- **Influenced by:** Security incidents, compliance audits, support ticket volume, tool audit data

## Objection Patterns
- "We already have too many tools — we're cutting, not adding"
- "Does it support our SIS? Our LMS? Clever? ClassLink?"
- "What's your SOC 2 status? FERPA compliance? State privacy law adherence?"
- "How many tools does this actually replace? Prove it."
- "Teachers will just use the free version and bypass procurement anyway"
- "Our bandwidth can't support another platform rollout"

## Win Triggers
- **Consolidation opportunity:** Currently paying for Kahoot + Nearpod + Edpuzzle + assessment tool separately
- **Tool audit results:** Recent audit revealed massive waste on unused subscriptions
- **Security incident or audit:** Increased pressure to reduce vendor count
- **SSO/integration pain:** Current tools don't integrate cleanly
- **Teacher bypass:** Teachers using free tools IT can't monitor — Wayground paid plan brings them under IT umbrella
- **Budget pressure:** Post-ESSER cliff, need to cut subscriptions while maintaining capability

## Data Points That Matter
Lead with technical and operational metrics:
- **Current tool overlap** — which tools Wayground replaces (side-by-side comparison)
- **Integration capabilities** — SSO (Clever, ClassLink), LMS (Canvas, Schoology, Google Classroom), SIS roster sync, grade passback
- **Adoption rates** — actual teacher usage vs. paid licenses (for their current tools AND Wayground)
- **Security credentials** — COPPA, FERPA, SOC 2, state-specific compliance
- **Support reduction** — fewer tools = fewer help desk tickets
- **Cost comparison** — TCO of current stack vs. Wayground consolidation

## Value Proposition Mapping

| Value Prop | Key Features | What to Say | What to Show |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consolidate Tech Stack | Replaces Kahoot, Quizlet, EdPuzzle, Nearpod, Newsela, IXL, Google Forms; 18+ question types; one login | "Districts average 2,700 tools per year. Wayground replaces 5-7 single-purpose tools." | Side-by-side: tools replaced; Student view: single login; ROI calculator |

## How to Engage
- **Lead with consolidation** — "We're not adding a tool, we're replacing 5-7"
- Show TCO comparison: current stack vs. Wayground
- Open with integration capabilities: SSO, LMS, SIS — answer their first question before they ask
- Provide security documentation upfront: COPPA, FERPA, SOC 2, state privacy compliance
- Emphasize voluntary teacher adoption: "90% of US schools already have teachers using it — this isn't a rollout, it's a formalization"
- Show adoption analytics: what they'll see in admin dashboard about actual usage
- **Don't lead with pedagogy** — that's not their lens. Lead with operations, security, and cost.

## Pillar Routing
- **Primary:** PLAN (consolidation, integration, tech stack simplification)
- **Secondary:** MEASURE (adoption analytics, usage-to-outcome data for board)
- **When they say:** "We have too many tools" → Lead with PLAN (consolidation)
- **When they say:** "We need to prove ROI on tech spend" → Lead with MEASURE
- **Don't say:** Curriculum or pedagogical language — not their lens
