# Persona: Chief Academic Officer / Asst. Superintendent

## Role & Title
- **Title:** Chief Academic Officer (CAO), Assistant Superintendent of Curriculum & Instruction, Deputy Superintendent for Academics
- **Level:** C-suite equivalent — second only to superintendent on academic decisions
- **Scope:** Instructional budget, PD & coaching (Title II), assessment strategy, curriculum adoption, academic outcomes
- **Nickname:** "The Academic Buyer"

**Why This Persona Matters:**
They own the instructional budget and academic outcomes but don't control the classroom conditions. They're the bridge between superintendent-level strategy and building-level reality. For district-wide deals, they're often the economic buyer or the person who moves budget — especially for curriculum, assessment, and PD spend.

## Primary Concerns
1. **Fidelity of program adoption** — are teachers actually using adopted curriculum and tools as intended?
2. **Teacher buy-in** — mandates fail without teacher support; need voluntary adoption
3. **Pacing consistency** — ensuring all schools are on the same instructional timeline
4. **Evidence of learning** — showing progress on high-stakes tests and benchmarks
5. **Board meeting presentations** — needs clean data stories for board reporting
6. **Subgroup progress** — closing achievement gaps (race, ELL, SPED, socioeconomic)
7. **Budget stewardship** — justifying instructional spend to superintendent and board

## Language They Use
- "Fidelity of implementation"
- "Are teachers using it with fidelity?"
- "I need to show the board we're making progress"
- "We invested in [curriculum] — I need to know it's working"
- "Show me subgroup data"
- "How does this connect to our strategic plan?"
- "I own the outcomes but don't control the conditions"
- "We need PLC data that actually drives instruction"
- "Title II funds could work for this if it's PD-adjacent"

## Decision Authority
- **Can approve:** Instructional tool purchases, PD budget allocation, pilot authorization
- **Can recommend:** District-wide adoption directly to superintendent
- **Can champion:** Most credible internal voice for academic technology — their endorsement carries enormous weight
- **Budget control:** Owns instructional budget, PD (Title II), and often assessment budget
- **Influenced by:** State accountability data, board priorities, teacher feedback, curriculum director recommendations

## Objection Patterns
- "How does this fit our existing curriculum framework?"
- "We just invested heavily in [publisher] — I can't justify another purchase"
- "Show me evidence this improves outcomes, not just engagement"
- "My principals already have too many initiatives"
- "I need something the board will understand in a 5-minute presentation"
- "Teachers are overwhelmed — I can't add to their plate"

## Win Triggers
- **Accountability pressure:** State ratings slipped; board demanding improvement plan
- **Subgroup gaps:** Achievement gaps widening despite investments — need targeted intervention data
- **Post-adoption evaluation:** 2-3 years into curriculum adoption, need to see if it's working
- **PLC investment:** Strong PLC culture looking for common assessment data to fuel collaboration
- **Board alignment:** Board priorities (equity, literacy, STEM) map directly to Wayground value props
- **Teacher groundswell:** Teachers already using Wayground — CAO wants to formalize and get data
- **Budget cycle:** Title I/II/IV funding refresh, ESSER wind-down forcing consolidation

## Data Points That Matter
Lead with district-level academic metrics:
- **Board priority alignment** — which board themes map to Wayground (from account_feature_store board meetings)
- **Teacher adoption curve** — voluntary adoption showing teachers want this, not just admin
- **Standards mastery data** — district-wide view of proficiency by standard, school, subgroup
- **Comparable district outcomes** — peer districts using Wayground successfully
- **Pacing consistency** — cross-school assessment data showing instructional alignment
- **Funding eligibility** — Title I, II, IV alignment for budget justification

## Value Proposition Mapping

| Value Prop | Key Features | What to Say | What to Show |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protect Curriculum Investment | BYOC alignment, AI content aligned to curriculum, district libraries, standards tagging | "Your supplemental resources should reinforce the curriculum you invested in — not pull teachers away from it." | BYOC: alignment to their curriculum; District library with vetted content |
| Evidence for the Board | Standards mastery dashboards (district-wide), ESSA Level 3, CA cross-school comparison | "Board-ready data showing exactly where students are by standard, school, and subgroup." | District dashboard; CA cross-school comparison; Board presentation view |
| Teacher Buy-In Without Mandates | Voluntary adoption, gamification, organic teacher growth, progressive complexity | "90% of US schools already have teachers using it voluntarily. This isn't a mandate — it's a formalization." | Organic adoption data for their district; Teacher satisfaction metrics |
| Close Achievement Gaps | 25+ accommodations, subgroup analytics, standards mastery by demographic, intervention grouping | "See which subgroups need support on which standards — and have the tool to intervene." | Subgroup analysis dashboard; Accommodation usage; Intervention group creation |

## How to Engage
- **Start with board priorities** — use board meeting data from account_feature_store
- Frame everything as district-wide outcomes, not individual features
- Lead with evidence and ROI: ESSA Level 3, comparable district data, academic improvement
- Show how Wayground fits their existing curriculum framework (BYOC, alignment)
- Provide a "board-ready" data story they can present
- Reference their strategic plan themes — connect Wayground to initiatives already approved
- Acknowledge mandate fatigue: "This isn't another top-down tool — teachers are already choosing it"
- Discuss funding pathways: Title I (intervention), Title II (PD), Title IV (technology)

## Pillar Routing
- **Primary:** MEASURE (academic outcomes, board data, CA analytics)
- **Secondary:** PLAN (curriculum alignment, BYOC, instructional coherence)
- **When they say:** "I need to show the board progress" → Lead with MEASURE
- **When they say:** "Teachers are going off-curriculum" → Lead with PLAN
