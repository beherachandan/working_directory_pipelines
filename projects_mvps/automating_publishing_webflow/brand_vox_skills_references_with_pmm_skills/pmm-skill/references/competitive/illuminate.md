# Battlecard: Illuminate Education / DnA (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt)
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: CA Competitive Landscape, Customer Feedback, Internal Intel*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Illuminate Education (acquired by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt / HMH)
- **Products:** Illuminate DnA (assessment), Illuminate ELD (formerly Ellevation for ELL), MTSS/intervention workflows
- **Audience:** K-12 districts — strength in assessment + MTSS/intervention combo
- **Market Share:** Part of "Renaissance DnA" category in survey data (~3.2%)
- **Key Differentiator:** Combined assessment + MTSS/intervention workflows, strong data dashboards, ELD tracking
- **Pricing:** District-level enterprise pricing
- **Category:** Tier 1 Direct CA Platform Competitor

## Internal Assessment
Illuminate/DnA has strong assessment and MTSS capabilities but is complex, expensive, and doesn't generate teacher engagement. Post-HMH acquisition, the product has seen neglect. Districts report technical issues, poor support, and difficulty crafting assessments. The competitive dynamic: Illuminate has depth in MTSS/intervention workflows that Wayground doesn't match, but their assessment tool is clinical and underused.

## Customer Quotes (Real Feedback)
- "Lots of technical issues, lack of support, issues crafting assessments, glitches." — Dinuba Unified, 7K students

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- "Assessment + intervention" — unified assessment data feeding MTSS/RTI workflows
- "Data-driven instruction" — dashboards for teachers and administrators
- "ELD tracking" — compliance and progress monitoring for English learners
- "HMH ecosystem" — part of the broader HMH curriculum suite

**What they don't talk about:**
- Technical reliability issues reported by customers
- Complex setup and administration
- No engagement layer — teachers don't use it daily
- No gamification, no daily classroom use case
- Expensive relative to value delivered
- Post-acquisition product neglect
- Teachers view it as an admin mandate, not a tool they choose

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Teacher daily use
> "Illuminate is used during assessment windows. Wayground is used every day — which means richer data and better adoption."
Same story as other CA competitors: no daily use case means low teacher engagement and sparse data between testing windows.
- *Landmine:* "How often do teachers voluntarily log into Illuminate outside of mandated assessment periods?"

### Win #2: Technical reliability
> "Districts report technical issues and support degradation post-HMH acquisition."
Real customer pain — glitches during testing windows are catastrophic for trust. Wayground's uptime and support are competitive advantages.
- *Landmine:* "How has your experience with Illuminate's technical reliability been, particularly during assessment windows?"

### Win #3: Simplicity vs. complexity
> "Illuminate is powerful but complex. Wayground gives you assessment depth with a UX teachers actually enjoy."
Illuminate's admin setup is heavy. Teachers find it difficult to create assessments. Wayground's AI-powered creation and intuitive interface are dramatically simpler.
- *Landmine:* "How long does it take to set up an assessment in Illuminate vs. create one in a modern platform?"

### Win #4: Engagement = better data
> "The best assessment data comes from engaged students. Illuminate's clinical interface gets minimum effort."
Same fundamental problem as all legacy assessment tools: no engagement design means students rush through.

## Landmine Questions
1. "How many teachers actively create assessments in Illuminate vs. just viewing reports?"
2. "What's your experience with technical reliability during testing windows?"
3. "Between formal assessment periods, where does your instructional data come from?"
4. "How do students experience Illuminate assessments? Is there any engagement design?"
5. "What's the total cost of Illuminate + your daily engagement tools + your formative assessment tools?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: Illuminate for MTSS, dissatisfied with assessment**
- Don't try to replace MTSS — complement it
- Position Wayground as the data source that feeds better information into intervention workflows
- "Better daily data in → better intervention decisions out"

**Scenario B: Technical frustration opening the door**
- Lead with reliability and support quality
- Show Wayground's uptime, responsive support, simpler administration
- Propose pilot to compare teacher experience

**Scenario C: HMH ecosystem district**
- Separate assessment from curriculum: "Keep HMH content. Replace Illuminate assessment with a tool teachers actually use."
- Show how Wayground supports HMH curriculum alignment through BYOC
