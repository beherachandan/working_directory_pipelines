# Competitive Positioning

Competitive intelligence based on win/loss analysis (1,030 won, 3,223 lost deals) and market positioning.

## Consolidation Pitch Trigger (Carrie, Mar 9 2026)

**Threshold: 2+ competitor tools.** Any time a district is running two or more competitor tools, lead with consolidation. Don't wait for 3+.

**Example:** Jordan School District (UT) runs Mastery Connect + Nearpod. That's two tools Wayground replaces with one purchase. Consolidation pitch applies.

**Why:** Districts buying 2 tools are already paying for overlapping capabilities and managing multiple vendor relationships. Consolidation ROI is real at 2, not just 3.

### The Three Layers of Tool Sprawl (Carrie, Mar 9 2026)

Tool sprawl in a district isn't just about what the district purchased. There are three layers, and each one makes the data/experience management problem worse:

1. **District-level purchases (2 tools max, typically).** Budget-approved, vendor-managed. This is the visible layer. A district might buy Nearpod + Mastery Connect, or Kahoot + i-Ready. These are the contracts admins know about.

2. **School-level point solutions.** Individual schools purchase their own tools on top of district contracts. A principal buys Edpuzzle for their building, or a school gets a Gimkit license. The district admin has no visibility into this data and can't ensure consistent experiences across schools.

3. **Teacher-level free tools.** Teachers independently use free tiers of Kahoot, Blooket, Quizlet, Canva, etc. No one at the district level knows what's happening, there's no data flowing up, and every classroom has a different student experience.

**The pain point:** A district C&I director or superintendent can't manage data or ensure consistent learning experiences when tools are being adopted at three different levels. They see their 2 vendor contracts and think they have it handled, but the real sprawl is invisible to them.

**The Wayground pitch:** "You purchased 2 tools at the district level. But how many tools are actually running in your classrooms? Wayground consolidates all three layers into one platform with one data stream. Your district gets visibility, your schools get consistency, and your teachers get everything they need without going rogue."

## Nearpod (COMPREHENSIVE BATTLECARD — Updated Mar 2026 by Carrie)

**What they are:** Interactive lesson delivery platform (acquired by Renaissance Learning, 2021). Renaissance is owned by Francisco Partners (~$5.4B valuation).

**Key stats:** 3M+ teachers (claimed), 75%+ US districts (Renaissance umbrella), $3.50–$7.50/student/yr at district level, up to 40% competitive discounts.

**Products:** Nearpod (interactive lessons) + Flocabulary (hip-hop learning videos, acquired). Part of Renaissance suite alongside Star Assessments, Accelerated Reader, myON, Lalilo, Freckle.

### ⚠️ THE KEY INSIGHT
> "Nearpod won the market as a consolidator product, but they're a point solution masking as a consolidator. Districts that purchased Nearpod believe they already played the consolidation game and won years ago. However, their slow product development means they haven't kept up with the market."

### Where they're strong:
- Interactive lesson delivery (polls, quizzes, VR field trips, simulations embedded in slides)
- VR field trips and 3D objects (unique differentiator)
- Flocabulary hip-hop video content library
- Renaissance ecosystem integration (Star, AR, myON — bundled deals)
- Google Slides integration (turn existing slides into Nearpod lessons)
- Brand recognition among lesson-delivery buyers

### Where they're weak:
- **Product stagnation** — Core product hasn't evolved meaningfully since Renaissance acquisition. Teachers report it feels the same after 3+ years.
- **Not a platform — a feature in a suite** — Post-acquisition, Nearpod is one module within Renaissance, not independent. Pricing/roadmap dictated by Renaissance priorities.
- **Assessment is Star, not Nearpod** — Nearpod's formative data is lightweight. No common assessments, no item banks, no standards-based reporting. When Renaissance talks assessment, they mean Star.
- **AI gap** — No AI tutoring, no student-facing AI, minimal AI features. While competitors shipped AI tutoring/hints/generation, Nearpod's AI story is near-zero.
- **No game-based engagement** — Has "Time to Climb" (basic gamification) but no true competitive game mode. Teachers still need a separate tool for Kahoot-style energy.
- **No IEP/504/MLL accommodation suite** — Basic accessibility only. No documented accommodation features comparable to Wayground's 25+.
- **No flashcards or study tools**
- **No ESSA evidence** for Nearpod specifically (Star has ESSA evidence, but that's a different product)
- **Instruction-assessment disconnect** — Nearpod lesson data and Star assessment data are separate systems. No unified dashboard connecting what teachers teach to how students perform.

### Feature-by-Feature (18 categories):
- **Wayground leads: 10** — Live games, async assignments, common assessments, standards alignment, real-time analytics, IEP/504 accommodations, MLL support, AI generation, AI tutoring, ESSA evidence, flashcards
- **Even: 6** — Interactive slides, interactive video, LMS integrations, content marketplace, district admin dashboard
- **Nearpod leads: 2** — VR/immersive content, Flocabulary

### Why We Win (4 pillars):

**1. Real consolidation vs. perceived consolidation**
> "You bought Nearpod to consolidate. But how many other tools are your teachers still using alongside it?"
- Districts with Nearpod still need Kahoot (games), Edpuzzle (video), flashcard tools, separate assessment platform. Wayground consolidates ALL of these.
- *Landmine:* "Since adopting Nearpod, how many other engagement and assessment tools are your teachers still using? Has your tool count actually gone down?"

**2. Product velocity**
> "Three years under Renaissance, and the product looks the same. What has Nearpod shipped lately?"
- Wayground has shipped AI tutoring, AI generation, 25+ accommodations, common assessments, interactive video, game-based engine. Nearpod feels frozen.
- *Landmine:* "What new capabilities has Nearpod added in the last two years that changed how your teachers teach?"

**3. Engagement spectrum**
> "Nearpod does lessons. But where's the energy?"
- Nearpod lives in the middle of the engagement spectrum only. Wayground spans Friday game day energy → Wednesday structured lesson → Monday district benchmark.
- *Landmine:* "When your teachers want to run a high-energy review game before a test, what tool do they use? Is that the same tool they use for lessons?"

**4. Assessment independence**
> "If Nearpod is your instruction tool and Star is your assessment tool, who connects the dots?"
- In Renaissance suite, instruction (Nearpod) and assessment (Star) are separate products with separate data. Wayground keeps instruction and assessment in one platform.
- *Landmine:* "Can your teachers see a single dashboard that connects what they teach with how students perform on assessments? Or are those two different systems?"

### Deal Strategy:

**Scenario A — Nearpod renewal:** Time to renewal window. Lead with consolidation audit + TCO comparison + innovation narrative.

**Scenario B — Just bought Renaissance suite:** Don't unseat full suite. Unbundle Nearpod from it. "Keep Star. Keep AR. Replace Nearpod." Run side-by-side pilot. Build teacher champions frustrated with Nearpod.

**Scenario C — Head-to-head eval:** Demo full spectrum (game → lesson → video → assessment → accommodations → AI tutoring). Bring ESSA evidence (we have it, they don't for Nearpod). Challenge consolidation claim directly.

### Pricing Strategy:
> Don't compete on per-student price against Nearpod alone. Compete on TCO: Nearpod + Kahoot + Edpuzzle + flashcard tool + assessment tool vs. Wayground alone.

### Landmine Questions (full set):
1. **Consolidation audit:** "You adopted Nearpod to reduce tool sprawl. Since then, how many new tools have your teachers added?"
2. **Product evolution:** "What's the biggest new feature Nearpod has shipped since Renaissance acquired them?"
3. **Assessment continuity:** "When a teacher finishes a Nearpod lesson, where does the formative data go? Can they see it alongside Star data in one place?"
4. **Engagement range:** "Do your teachers use Nearpod for review games and high-energy activities, or just for structured lessons?"
5. **Accommodation depth:** "How many built-in accommodations does your current platform offer for IEP and 504 students?"
6. **AI readiness:** "Is your current instruction platform using AI to help students learn in real-time, or is AI limited to the teacher's side?"
