# Battlecard: Performance Matters (PowerSchool)
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: CA Competitive Landscape, Customer Feedback, Internal Intel*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Performance Matters (acquired by PowerSchool) — part of the PowerSchool suite
- **Founded:** 2003
- **Products:** Comprehensive benchmark/interim testing, item banking, data analytics
- **Audience:** K-12 districts — strength in large-district benchmark testing programs
- **Market Share:** ~4.6% of district-mandated CFA tools (per CA deck survey data)
- **Key Differentiator:** PowerSchool SIS integration, comprehensive benchmark testing, deep item bank
- **Pricing:** District-level; often bundled with PowerSchool SIS contracts
- **Category:** Tier 1 Direct CA Platform Competitor

## Internal Assessment
Performance Matters is a legacy benchmark testing platform with deep district-level analytics but terrible authoring tools. The critical weakness: only 16% of teachers actually create assessments in it. It's an admin tool, not a teacher tool. The competitive dynamic: PM has comprehensive benchmarking, but teachers hate using it — which means adoption and data quality suffer.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- "Comprehensive benchmark testing" — full district-wide testing program management
- "PowerSchool integration" — tight SIS data sync, rostering, demographics
- "Item bank depth" — publisher and custom item banks
- "Data analytics" — multi-level reporting, predictive analytics, demographic analysis

**What they don't talk about:**
- Terrible authoring experience — teachers avoid creating content in it
- Only 16% of teachers create assessments in PM (per Effingham County feedback)
- Heavy admin setup and configuration burden
- No gamification, engagement, or daily classroom use case
- No accommodations suite
- Aging UX — feels dated compared to modern platforms
- No AI features for content creation or analysis
- Dependent on PowerSchool ecosystem for maximum value

## Customer Quotes (Real Feedback)
- "Authoring is terrible... Only 16% of PM teachers actually create assessments in it." — Effingham County, 14K students
- Heavy admin setup burden repeatedly cited as friction point

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Teacher authoring
> "Only 16% of PM teachers create assessments. With Wayground, teachers create content because they want to — AI makes it fast, and gamification makes it rewarding."
PM's authoring is so painful that the vast majority of teachers just administer pre-made assessments. This means districts get standardized data but no teacher-created, classroom-responsive assessments. Wayground's AI Create and intuitive authoring flip this.
- *Landmine:* "What percentage of your teachers actually create their own assessments in Performance Matters?"

### Win #2: Daily classroom presence
> "PM is used 3-4 times per year during benchmark windows. Wayground is used every day."
PM has zero daily use case. Teachers open it during mandated testing and close it. Between testing windows, there's no engagement, no formative data, no ongoing insights. Wayground fills every day with data.
- *Landmine:* "Between benchmark windows, where does your formative assessment data come from?"

### Win #3: Student engagement and data quality
> "Assessment data is only as good as student effort. When students don't care about the tool, they rush through — and your data is compromised."
PM assessments are experienced as clinical tests. No gamification, no engagement design. Students give minimum effort, particularly on benchmarks that "don't count" for their grade.
- *Landmine:* "Are you confident students give their best effort on PM benchmarks, or do they rush through?"

### Win #4: Modern UX and admin burden
> "Performance Matters requires significant admin setup. Wayground works out of the box."
PM configuration is notoriously heavy — rostering, test setup, window scheduling, item bank management all require dedicated admin time. Wayground's CA features require minimal setup.
- *Landmine:* "How much administrator time goes into setting up each testing window in PM?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: PM is legacy but entrenched**
- Lead with teacher adoption story + authoring frustration
- Show AI Create: upload a standard → instant assessment → gamified delivery
- Position as "daily formative + CA" vs. PM's "3x/year benchmark only"

**Scenario B: PowerSchool district**
- Don't fight the PowerSchool ecosystem — show integration compatibility
- Expand scope: "PM handles benchmarks. Wayground handles everything else AND can do benchmarks too."
- Build parallel adoption during school year, show data at PM renewal

**Scenario C: PM renewal approaching**
- TCO comparison: PM + daily tools vs. Wayground (all-in-one)
- Show student effort/engagement difference
- Reference the 16% teacher authoring stat as diagnostic
