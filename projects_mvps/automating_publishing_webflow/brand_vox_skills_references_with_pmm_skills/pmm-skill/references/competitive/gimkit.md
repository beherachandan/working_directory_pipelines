# Battlecard: Gimkit
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Gimkit Inc. (private, bootstrapped by a high school student in 2017)
- **HQ:** United States
- **Founded:** 2017 (by a high school student as a school project)
- **Products:** Gimkit (game-based quiz platform with creative modes), Gimkit Ink (whiteboard/drawing), Gimkit Pro
- **Audience:** K-12 students and teachers (skews middle and high school)
- **Users:** Growing; not publicly disclosed
- **Pricing:** Free tier (limited); Gimkit Pro ~$10-15/month for teachers; school plans available
- **Key Differentiator:** Most creative game mechanics in EdTech — Gimkit modes feel like actual video games, not quiz tools. Students earn in-game currency, buy power-ups, play elaborate modes (Fishtopia, Trust No One, The Floor is Lava, etc.)
- **ESSA Evidence:** None
- **Accessibility:** None documented
- **AI Features:** None documented

## Internal Assessment
Gimkit is the most creatively designed game-based learning tool in the market. Their game modes genuinely feel like video games, not quizzes dressed up as games. Students love it. However, Gimkit is even more narrowly focused than Kahoot or Blooket — it's pure entertainment with quiz questions layered in. No assessment data, no lessons, no district features, no accommodations. In a district conversation, Gimkit is invisible. It's a teacher-pocket purchase.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- "Learning feels like a game" — Game modes are so creative students forget they're learning
- Indie/bootstrapped story — Founded by a high school student, grassroots credibility
- Creative mode variety — Each mode is a completely different game genre
- In-game economy — Students earn currency, buy items, customize; creates loop engagement

**What they don't talk about:**
- Assessment data, analytics, or outcomes
- Lessons, video, or structured content
- Accommodations, accessibility, or compliance
- District administration or reporting
- Standards alignment or curriculum integration
- AI features of any kind
- Evidence of learning impact

## Feature Comparison (16 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 14** — Interactive lessons, video, flashcards, async assignments, common assessments, standards alignment, analytics, IEP/504 accommodations, MLL support, AI tutoring, AI generation, district admin, ESSA evidence, content curation
- **Even: 1** — Game-based engagement (Gimkit's game creativity vs. Wayground's game + platform)
- **Gimkit leads: 1** — Game mode creativity/variety (their modes feel like actual video games)

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Learning platform vs. entertainment
> "Gimkit is an arcade. Wayground is a classroom."
Gimkit modes (Fishtopia, Trust No One) are genuinely fun. But they're entertainment first, learning second. Teachers can't see standards mastery, can't track growth, can't run assessments. Wayground delivers the same game energy with real learning outcomes.
- *Landmine:* "When students play Gimkit, does the game tell teachers which standards students have mastered? Or is it just a score?"

### Win #2: Beyond Friday games
> "What happens Monday through Thursday?"
Gimkit handles one moment: game time. The other 90% of instruction — lessons, practice, assessments, accommodations — needs other tools. Wayground handles the full week.
- *Landmine:* "Gimkit handles game day. What handles lesson day, assessment day, and practice day?"

### Win #3: District credibility
> "Your superintendent has never heard of Gimkit."
Gimkit has zero district-level presence. No admin dashboard, no compliance, no accommodation suite, no reporting. It's a teacher pocket purchase. When budget gets scrutinized, Gimkit is indefensible.
- *Landmine:* "If you had to present Gimkit ROI to your school board, what data would you show?"

### Win #4: Accommodations gap
> "Fun should be for every student."
Gimkit has no accommodation suite. Students with IEPs, 504 plans, or MLL needs get the same experience as everyone else — or more accurately, they get NO accommodations.
- *Landmine:* "How do students with processing speed challenges or reading disabilities participate in timed Gimkit games?"
