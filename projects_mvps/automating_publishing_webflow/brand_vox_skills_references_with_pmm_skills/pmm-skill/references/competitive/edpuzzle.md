# Battlecard: Edpuzzle
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Edpuzzle Inc. (private, VC-backed)
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Founded:** 2013
- **Products:** Edpuzzle (interactive video lessons with embedded questions)
- **Audience:** K-12 teachers who use video in instruction
- **Users:** 20M+ teachers claimed
- **Pricing:** Freemium (free for individual teachers); school plans ~$2,500/school/year; district plans available
- **Key Differentiator:** Market leader in interactive video — embed questions, audio notes, and voiceovers into any video (YouTube, Khan Academy, own uploads)
- **ESSA Evidence:** None known
- **Accessibility:** Basic; no documented IEP/504/MLL accommodation suite
- **AI Features:** AI question suggestion for videos; no student-facing AI, no AI tutoring

## Internal Assessment
Edpuzzle is the clear market leader in interactive video for K-12. They own "video + questions" completely. Our competitive position: Wayground has interactive video as ONE feature of a complete platform. Edpuzzle's interactive video is their ONLY feature. In consolidation conversations, Edpuzzle gets replaced by a platform that does video plus everything else. The challenge: Edpuzzle's video experience is genuinely excellent, and teachers love it.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- Interactive video leader: "Turn any video into a lesson" — embed questions at any timestamp
- Teacher control: "Know who watched, who skipped, and who understood"
- Massive video library: Works with YouTube, Khan Academy, TED-Ed, National Geographic, or teacher uploads
- "Prevent skipping": Students can't skip ahead without answering questions

**What they don't talk about:**
- Beyond video: No quizzes, no games, no flashcards, no lesson builder outside video
- Assessment rigor: Video questions are lightweight formative checks, not assessments
- Standards alignment: No built-in standards tagging or item banking
- Accommodations: No IEP/504/MLL features
- District analytics: Limited admin/district reporting
- AI depth: AI question suggestion only — no tutoring, no generation beyond video

## Feature Comparison (18 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 13** — Live games, flashcards, interactive slides/lessons, async assignments, common assessments, standards alignment, real-time analytics, IEP/504 accommodations, MLL support, AI tutoring, AI question generation, district admin, ESSA evidence
- **Even: 2** — Interactive video (both offer it; Edpuzzle deeper on video-specific UX), LMS integrations
- **Edpuzzle leads: 3** — Video-specific UX (anti-skip, voiceover, audio notes), video content library breadth, video analytics (watch time, rewatch patterns)

## Why We Win

### Win #1: One platform vs. one feature
> "Edpuzzle is a video tool. Wayground is a platform that includes video."
Edpuzzle does video brilliantly. But teachers still need Kahoot for games, Nearpod for lessons, a separate assessment tool, and flashcard app. Wayground includes interactive video AND all of those. In consolidation, one platform beats one feature.
- *Landmine:* "Alongside Edpuzzle, what other tools are your teachers using for games, lessons, assessments, and study? What's the total cost?"

### Win #2: Assessment beyond video
> "Edpuzzle tells you who watched. Wayground tells you who learned."
Edpuzzle's formative data is limited to video interaction: who watched, who answered embedded questions correctly. Wayground tracks learning across ALL content types (video, quizzes, lessons, games, assessments) with longitudinal mastery data.
- *Landmine:* "When a student answers a video question wrong in Edpuzzle, does that data flow into their overall mastery profile? Or is it isolated?"

### Win #3: Not all learning is video
> "Video is one modality. Students need practice, games, assessments, and study tools too."
Edpuzzle is video-only. But learning isn't video-only. Some concepts need practice problems, game-based review, flashcard study, or formal assessment. Wayground supports every modality. Edpuzzle supports one.
- *Landmine:* "What percentage of your instruction is video-based? For the rest, what tools are your teachers using?"

### Win #4: Accommodations and equity
> "When a student with an IEP watches an Edpuzzle video, who handles extended time and text-to-speech?"
Edpuzzle has no accommodation suite. Wayground offers 25+ accommodations across every content type, including video lessons.
- *Landmine:* "How do your students with IEPs and 504 plans access Edpuzzle content? Are accommodations built in or handled manually?"

## Landmine Questions
1. "How many tools do your teachers use beyond Edpuzzle for quizzes, games, and assessments?"
2. "When you need to run a district benchmark, can you do it in Edpuzzle?"
3. "What percentage of your instruction is video-based? What handles the rest?"
4. "Can you see a student's mastery across video lessons AND practice quizzes in one dashboard?"
5. "How are IEP accommodations handled when students access Edpuzzle content?"
6. "What's your total edtech spend when you add Edpuzzle + quiz tool + lesson tool + assessment platform?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: Edpuzzle is entrenched, teachers love it**
- Don't fight the love. Show the gap: "Edpuzzle does video. What does everything else?"
- Layer Wayground alongside → natural attrition as teachers consolidate

**Scenario B: District evaluating Edpuzzle**
- Expand criteria: "If you're evaluating a video tool, Edpuzzle wins. If you're evaluating a learning platform, let's talk."
- Demo full workflow: video lesson → quiz → game review → assessment → data

**Scenario C: Budget consolidation conversation**
- TCO: Edpuzzle ($2,500/school) + Kahoot + quiz tool + assessment = $X. Wayground = less.
- "The cheapest tool is the one that replaces four others."

## THE ONE-LINER
> "Edpuzzle makes video interactive. Wayground makes learning interactive — video, games, lessons, and assessments, all in one."
