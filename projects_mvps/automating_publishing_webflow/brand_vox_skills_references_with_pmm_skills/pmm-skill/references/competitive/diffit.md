# Battlecard: Diffit
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Diffit Inc. (private, AI EdTech startup)
- **HQ:** United States
- **Founded:** ~2023
- **Products:** Diffit (reading level differentiation and content adaptation tool)
- **Audience:** K-12 teachers, especially those serving ELL/MLL and diverse reading levels
- **Users:** Unknown; individual teacher discovery adoption
- **Pricing:** Freemium; school/district plans ~$3-8/teacher/year
- **Key Differentiator:** AI-powered reading level adaptation — same text at multiple Lexile levels instantly
- **ESSA Evidence:** None
- **Accessibility:** None (no accommodation suite despite differentiation focus)
- **AI Features:** AI reading level adaptation only; no tutoring, hints, or engagement tools

## Internal Assessment
Diffit is narrowly focused on reading level differentiation. Solves a real problem (content at multiple Lexile levels for ELL/MLL). But no ecosystem beyond that — generates content, teachers deliver elsewhere. In consolidation conversations, Diffit gets replaced. In ELL-heavy schools with AI adoption culture, Diffit can be sticky.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- "Instantly create leveled reading passages from any content" — multiple Lexile levels
- ELL/MLL focus — Solution for high-ELL schools needing differentiated text
- Fast AI content generation — Paste URL/topic → get leveled passages + vocabulary + comprehension questions
- Teacher-friendly — "No special training needed"

**What they don't talk about:**
- No delivery platform (generates content, doesn't deliver)
- No assessment or engagement features
- No accommodations beyond reading levels
- No analytics or student performance tracking
- No platform integrations (not a delivery system)

## Feature Comparison (18 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 13** — Games, video, flashcards, assessments, standards alignment, analytics, IEP/504, district admin, LMS, AI tutoring, delivery platform, content marketplace, ESSA evidence
- **Even: 2** — Content generation, ELL/MLL scaffolding
- **Diffit leads: 3** — Lexile-specific reading level adaptation, vocabulary generation, text simplification depth

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Differentiation built-in, not bolted-on
> "Diffit is a differentiation tool. Wayground has differentiation built into everything."
Diffit → leveled passages → teacher goes elsewhere for quizzes. Wayground → leveled content + auto-generated assessments at each level, all in one place.
- *Landmine:* "When you differentiate reading content for ELL students, who creates the quiz questions at each level?"

### Win #2: End-to-end learning
> "Diffit creates passages. Wayground creates lessons, quizzes, and assessments."
Diffit is content generation only. Output goes to Google Classroom, PDF, LMS. Wayground generates AND delivers in interactive environment with assessment and analytics.
- *Landmine:* "After exporting content from Diffit, where do students take it? Printing PDFs, uploading to Google Classroom?"

### Win #3: Accommodations beyond reading levels
> "Differentiation shouldn't stop at reading levels."
Students also need extended time, text-to-speech, font adjustments, reduced distraction mode. Wayground: 25+. Diffit: zero.
- *Landmine:* "Beyond reading levels, what other accommodations do your ELL students need?"

### Win #4: Evidence and analytics
> "You can't improve what you don't measure."
Diffit generates content. No tracking of student learning. Wayground generates, delivers, and measures with ESSA Level III evidence.
- *Landmine:* "After using Diffit content, how do you know if students improved their reading comprehension?"

## Handling Objections

| They say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| "Diffit is purpose-built for ELL" | "Purpose-built for one thing = you need four other tools for everything else." |
| "Teachers love it" | "Keep it! Layer Wayground for the full workflow. Over time, one platform wins." |
| "It's cheaper/faster" | "Adoption speed ≠ adoption depth. Diffit + quiz tool + LMS + assessment > Wayground alone." |
| "Lexile adaptation is best-in-class" | "True. Wayground's is 95% as good, but embedded in a complete platform." |

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: School loves Diffit, tool sprawl problem**
- Acknowledge Diffit value → consolidation pitch → pilot with ELL teacher leaders

**Scenario B: District considering Diffit**
- "Why buy Diffit for one thing when Wayground does differentiation + assessment + games + accommodations?"

**Scenario C: Diffit embedded, teachers won't let go**
- Layer, don't replace. Let natural attrition happen as teachers use Wayground for 90% of work.

## THE ONE-LINER
> "Diffit generates content at different reading levels. Wayground generates, delivers, assesses, and accommodates at different levels. That's the difference between a tool and a platform."
