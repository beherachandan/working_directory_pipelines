# Battlecard: Blooket
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Blooket Inc. (private, self-funded/bootstrapped)
- **HQ:** United States
- **Founded:** ~2020
- **Products:** Blooket (game-based quiz platform with unique game modes and collectible "Blooks")
- **Audience:** K-8 students and teachers (skews younger)
- **Users:** Growing rapidly; not publicly disclosed. Estimated millions of monthly active users.
- **Pricing:** Free tier (generous); Blooket Plus ~$3-5/month; school plans available
- **Key Differentiator:** Unique game modes (Tower Defense, Café, Factory, etc.) + collectible characters ("Blooks") that students obsess over
- **ESSA Evidence:** None
- **Accessibility:** None documented
- **AI Features:** None documented

## Internal Assessment
Blooket is a growing threat in the engagement space, especially K-8. Unlike Kahoot (which feels dated), Blooket is fresh, creative, and students genuinely love it. The Blooks collectible mechanic creates addiction-level engagement. However, like Kahoot, Blooket is ONLY a game tool — no lessons, no video, no assessments, no accommodations. In district-level conversations, Blooket is invisible (zero board mentions). It's a teacher-champion purchase that gets cut in budget reviews.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- Creative game modes: Tower Defense, Café, Factory, Gold Quest — each mode is a different game mechanic using quiz questions
- Collectible Blooks: Characters students collect, trade, and obsess over (Pokémon-style engagement)
- Student addiction: "Students BEG to play Blooket" — strongest student pull of any EdTech tool
- Simple and free: Free tier is very generous, low barrier to adoption

**What they don't talk about:**
- No assessment data (game scores ≠ learning data)
- No lessons, video, or structured content
- No accommodations or accessibility features
- No district admin features or reporting
- No standards alignment
- No AI features
- No common assessment or item banking
- No evidence of learning impact

## Feature Comparison (16 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 14** — Interactive lessons, video, flashcards, async assignments, common assessments, standards alignment, analytics, IEP/504 accommodations, MLL support, AI tutoring, AI generation, district admin, ESSA evidence, content curation
- **Even: 1** — Game-based engagement (different strengths: Blooket's game variety vs. Wayground's game + data)
- **Blooket leads: 1** — Collectible mechanic / student addiction factor

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Fun WITH data
> "Blooket is fun. Wayground is fun AND tells you if students learned."
Blooket games are engaging but produce zero usable learning data. Wayground games are equally engaging AND produce assessment data — item-level analysis, mastery tracking, standards reporting.
- *Landmine:* "When students play Blooket, what learning data do teachers get? Can they see which standards each student has mastered?"

### Win #2: Platform vs. game
> "Your teachers need more than Friday games."
Blooket does games. Period. No lessons, no video, no assessments, no study tools. Wayground does games + everything else.
- *Landmine:* "What tools handle lessons, video, and assessments for your teachers? Is that the same tool students play games on?"

### Win #3: District readiness
> "Blooket is invisible at the district level."
Blooket has no admin dashboard, no district reporting, no compliance features, no accommodation suite. It's a teacher tool, not a district platform. When admins ask what they're getting from Blooket, the answer is "games."
- *Landmine:* "What does your admin team see from Blooket? Can they pull a district-wide report on student learning from game data?"

### Win #4: Budget vulnerability
> "When budgets get cut, game-only tools get cut first."
Blooket's only value proposition is engagement. In a budget review, engagement-only tools are the first to go. Wayground's multi-function value proposition survives budget cuts because it replaces multiple tools.
- *Landmine:* "If your budget got cut 20% tomorrow, which tools survive: the ones that do one thing, or the platform that does five?"

## Landmine Questions
1. "What learning data does your admin team get from Blooket?"
2. "Beyond games, what handles lessons, assessments, and accommodations for your teachers?"
3. "If your board asks for evidence that Blooket improves outcomes, what do you show?"
4. "How are students with IEPs accommodated during Blooket games?"
5. "What's the total cost when you add Blooket + lesson tool + assessment tool + accommodation system?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: Teachers love Blooket, you're trying to move the school/district**
- Don't fight student love. Position as upgrade: "Same engagement, plus a complete platform."
- Target admin with data gap: "Your teachers love the games. Your admin needs data. Wayground gives both."

**Scenario B: Head-to-head (unlikely at district level, common at school level)**
- Expand criteria beyond engagement
- Demo: game → lesson → assessment → data → accommodations
- "Blooket is a game. This is a platform."

## Board Meeting Intelligence
- **Zero board-level mentions** across 787 accounts. Blooket, like Kahoot and Gimkit, is a teacher-level purchase with no board/admin visibility.
