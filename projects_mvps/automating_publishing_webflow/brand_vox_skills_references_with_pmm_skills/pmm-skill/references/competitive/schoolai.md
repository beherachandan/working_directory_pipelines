# Battlecard: SchoolAI
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** SchoolAI Inc. (private, VC-backed)
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Founded:** ~2023
- **Products:** AI Spaces (custom AI chatbots for students), Admin Dashboard, Safety & Guardrails, Teacher Controls
- **Audience:** K-12 (especially grades 6-12; expanding down)
- **Users:** Growing rapidly; estimated early millions
- **Positioning:** "The safe way to bring AI into your classroom"
- **Pricing:** Freemium; estimated $5-15/teacher/year for school/district plans
- **Key Differentiator:** Student-facing AI spaces with real-time admin oversight, content filtering, student data privacy
- **ESSA Evidence:** None — not positioned as an outcomes platform
- **Accessibility:** Not a focus; no IEP/504/MLL accommodation suite
- **AI Features:** Student-facing conversational AI with teacher-created prompts, safety guardrails, admin visibility

## Internal Assessment
SchoolAI targets a genuine pain point: how to get AI into classrooms safely. Their product (conversational AI spaces) is fundamentally different from Wayground's instruction platform. SchoolAI is a sandbox; Wayground is a system. Key battleground: districts evaluating AI for K-12 will discover SchoolAI first, but they also need instruction, assessment, and evidence.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- Safe AI in classroom — Guardrails, content filtering, teacher oversight
- Student agency — Teachers create custom AI spaces for exploration
- Admin visibility — Real-time dashboard of student AI interactions
- Teacher-controlled prompts — Bespoke learning experiences, not generic chatbot
- Freemium accessibility — Low barrier to adoption

**What they don't talk about:**
- Outcomes or assessment (AI sandbox, not instruction platform)
- Curriculum or standards alignment (open-ended exploration, not aligned)
- Structured instruction (supplemental/exploratory only)
- Differentiation or scaffolding (conversational but not adaptive)
- Accessibility accommodations (data privacy focus, not accessibility)

## Feature Comparison (19 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 17** — Student-facing AI (integrated vs. sandbox), AI question gen, lessons, practice, video, assessments, common assessments, standards alignment, analytics, IEP/504, MLL, adaptive learning, LMS integration, ESSA evidence, district admin (learning), teacher PD
- **Even: 1** — Rostering
- **SchoolAI leads: 2** — AI admin oversight/safety, freemium model

## Why We Win

### Win #1: AI without assessment is a science experiment
> "AI with assessment is a learning platform."
SchoolAI = students explore topics with AI. No measurement of learning. Wayground = AI embedded in instruction workflow, feeding assessment data.
- *Landmine:* "When students use AI, how do you know it's actually improving their learning? What's the evidence?"

### Win #2: Sandbox vs. system
> "SchoolAI is a sandbox. Wayground is a system."
Teachers still need lesson delivery, practice, assessments, analytics, accommodations from other tools. Wayground does it all.
- *Landmine:* "If you adopt SchoolAI for AI spaces, what other platforms do you still need for lessons, practice, and assessments?"

### Win #3: Standards alignment
> "Open-ended exploration doesn't map to your standards."
SchoolAI spaces are generative but not curriculum-mapped. District has no way to ensure AI conversations hit grade-level standards.
- *Landmine:* "Are your AI interactions mapped to grade-level standards? Or is it open-ended exploration?"

### Win #4: Evidence of impact
> "Your board needs to know: did this AI investment improve student outcomes?"
SchoolAI answers "how do we bring AI in responsibly?" Wayground answers "how do we use AI to improve outcomes?" ESSA Level III evidence vs. none.
- *Landmine:* "Has your board asked for outcomes evidence on your edtech investments?"

## Handling Objections

| They say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| "We need to be safe with AI first" | "Safety is table stakes. Wayground has safety AND evidence of learning impact." |
| "SchoolAI is free/cheaper" | "Free AI spaces don't solve instruction and assessment. Total cost of SchoolAI + everything else > Wayground." |
| "Teachers love SchoolAI spaces" | "Wayground has AI too — embedded in lessons and assessments so it actually improves learning." |
| "Board doesn't ask for evidence" | "They will. When they do, you'll want outcome data." |

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: District evaluating AI, discovers SchoolAI**
- Acknowledge safety concern → expand criteria to instruction, assessment, evidence
- "Let's talk about SchoolAI for AI spaces AND Wayground for everything else. Or just Wayground for all of it."

**Scenario B: District has adopted SchoolAI, expanding**
- Position as instruction layer: "SchoolAI handles AI exploration. Wayground handles instruction, assessment, analytics."
- TCO: SchoolAI + LMS + content platform + assessment tool vs. Wayground alone

**Scenario C: Head-to-head RFP**
- Push for comprehensive criteria (instruction, assessment, standards, accessibility — not just AI safety)
- Demo full workflow: lesson → practice with AI hints → assessment → data analysis
- Bring ESSA evidence

## THE ONE-LINER
> "SchoolAI is a sandbox for safe AI. Wayground is a system for safe, effective learning. One is a feature. The other is a platform."
