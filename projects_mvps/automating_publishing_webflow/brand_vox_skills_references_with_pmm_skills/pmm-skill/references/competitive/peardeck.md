# Battlecard: Pear Deck (GoGuardian)
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** GoGuardian (private, VC-backed; acquired Pear Deck in 2021)
- **HQ:** San Jose, CA (Pear Deck HQ: Madison, WI)
- **Founded:** Pear Deck: 2013; acquired by GoGuardian 2021
- **Products:** Pear Deck (Google Slides add-on), Pear Deck Tutor (human 1:1 tutoring), GoGuardian (device management)
- **Audience:** K-12 teachers who use Google Slides; bundled with GoGuardian device management
- **Users:** Millions of students (exact not disclosed); lower per-teacher adoption than Kahoot
- **Pricing:** $1.56-5.78/student/year; often free/deeply discounted when bundled with GoGuardian
- **Key Differentiator:** Works INSIDE Google Slides — turns existing slide decks into interactive lessons
- **ESSA Evidence:** None
- **Accessibility:** Basic; no accommodation suite comparable to Wayground's 25+
- **AI Features:** None (Pear Deck Tutor uses human tutors, not AI)

## Internal Assessment
Pear Deck is a real but narrowly-scoped threat. Not because of capabilities — because of the GoGuardian bundle. Districts buying GoGuardian for device management get Pear Deck free or deeply discounted. Product has stagnated since acquisition. Our strategy: Don't fight the bundle; show districts that Pear Deck alone doesn't solve their full instructional problem.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- Native to Google Slides — "Don't leave your slides"
- Minimal setup — Embed questions, polls, draggables into existing presentations
- Real-time student responses — Live classroom discussion facilitation
- GoGuardian bundle value — Part of the ecosystem alongside device management

**What they don't talk about:**
- Assessment rigor (designed for interaction, not assessment)
- Standalone value (depends on GoGuardian bundle momentum)
- Game-based engagement (none)
- Video or multimedia (slides only)
- Accessibility/accommodations (no IEP/504/MLL suite)
- AI or differentiation (zero AI; Pear Deck Tutor is human-only)

## Feature Comparison (20 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 16** — Live games, async assignments, interactive video, flashcards, common assessments, standards alignment, analytics, IEP/504 accommodations, MLL support, AI generation, AI tutoring, ESSA evidence, district admin, content marketplace, interactive lessons (broader), LMS integrations
- **Even: 2** — Rostering (Clever/ClassLink), LMS integrations
- **Pear Deck leads: 2** — Google Slides native integration, GoGuardian bundle integration

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Consolidation beats integration
> "Pear Deck is an add-on to Google Slides. Wayground is a platform."
Teachers with Pear Deck still need Kahoot for games, Edpuzzle for video, separate assessment tool, flashcard app. Wayground replaces ALL of that.
- *Landmine:* "If you're using Pear Deck, what other tools are your teachers using for video, assessments, and study prep?"

### Win #2: Assessment credibility
> "Pear Deck is for classroom engagement. Wayground handles engagement AND district-wide assessment."
No common assessments, no item banks, no longitudinal mastery tracking. Wayground spans games → lessons → assessments with ESSA evidence.
- *Landmine:* "When your district runs common assessments, what tool do you use? Is that the same tool for daily interaction?"

### Win #3: Accessibility at scale
> "Pear Deck has no accommodation suite. For IDEA compliance, that's a deal-breaker."
Wayground: 25+ accommodations. Pear Deck: basic accessibility only.
- *Landmine:* "How are your special education students currently accommodated in Pear Deck?"

### Win #4: Game-based engagement
> "Pear Deck makes slides interactive. Wayground makes learning fun and competitive."
No games, leaderboards, or competition in Pear Deck. Teachers still use Kahoot alongside it.
- *Landmine:* "Do your teachers currently use Kahoot alongside Pear Deck? What if you didn't have to?"

### Win #5: No AI
> "Pear Deck has no AI. Wayground has AI tutoring, hints, and explanations."
Zero AI features. Pear Deck Tutor is human 1:1 tutoring (separate product entirely).
- *Landmine:* "Is AI part of your classroom tech stack today?"

## Handling Objections

| They say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| "Our teachers already use Google Slides" | "Wayground IMPORTS Slides and adds everything Slides + Pear Deck can't do." |
| "We get Pear Deck free with GoGuardian" | "'Free' doesn't mean complete. What's the total cost of GoGuardian + Pear Deck + everything else?" |
| "Teachers love the Pear Deck workflow" | "They'll love Wayground too. And it doesn't limit them to slides." |
| "We just signed a 3-year GoGuardian deal" | "Perfect timing for a Wayground pilot. Show your board the savings when you consolidate." |

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: District evaluating GoGuardian, getting Pear Deck bundled**
- Lead with bundle math: "You're getting slides. What about video, games, assessments, accommodations?"
- Show full stack cost. Propose a pilot before GoGuardian deal is inked.

**Scenario B: District has GoGuardian + Pear Deck, happy with bundle**
- Don't fight the bundle. Expand around it.
- "Pear Deck handles slide interactivity. Wayground handles everything outside Slides."
- Target curriculum director with data/evidence gap.

**Scenario C: Head-to-head evaluation**
- Force scope conversation: "Pear Deck is a Slides add-on. Wayground is a platform."
- Demo breadth: games → video → assignment → assessment → accommodations
- Bring ESSA evidence.

## THE ONE-LINER
> "Pear Deck is a Google Slides add-on. Wayground is the platform that makes Slides obsolete for interactive learning."
