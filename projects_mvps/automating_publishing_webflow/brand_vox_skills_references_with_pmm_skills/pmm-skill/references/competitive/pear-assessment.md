# Battlecard: Pear Assessment (formerly Edulastic)
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: CA Competitive Landscape, Market Survey Data*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Pear Assessment (formerly Edulastic) — rebranded 2023
- **Products:** Formative and benchmark assessment platform with Google Classroom integration
- **Audience:** K-12 teachers — strength in free tier adoption and Google Classroom districts
- **Market Share:** ~4.0% of district-mandated CFA tools (per CA deck survey)
- **Key Differentiator:** Strong free tier that attracts teacher adoption, good question types (TEI), Google Classroom integration
- **Pricing:** Free tier (popular); district pricing on paid tiers
- **Category:** Tier 2 Assessment-Adjacent Competitor

**Note:** Pear Assessment (assessment platform) is DIFFERENT from Pear Deck (interactive presentation tool, owned by GoGuardian). Don't confuse them.

## Internal Assessment
Pear Assessment has a strong free tier that drives teacher adoption — similar to Wayground's bottoms-up growth model. Their question types (TEI) are solid. But they're a smaller-scale operation with limited engagement features and no gamification. The competitive dynamic: they're the most similar to our bottoms-up motion, which means we could face them in the same "teacher-discovered, district-sold" deals.

## Core Weaknesses
- **Smaller scale** — Limited brand recognition compared to major players
- **No gamification** — Assessment-only, no engagement design
- **Limited engagement features** — Students don't enjoy taking Pear assessments
- **No content creation tools** — No AI Create, no content library
- **No daily instruction use case** — Assessment-only platform
- **No accommodations suite** — Can't match Wayground's 25+
- **Narrower scope** — Doesn't replace lesson delivery, flashcards, or engagement tools

## Why We Win
1. **Full platform vs. assessment only** — Same bottoms-up growth, but Wayground offers full instruction + assessment
2. **Engagement** — Gamification drives student effort and completion
3. **AI creation** — Teachers create faster with AI tools
4. **Accommodations** — 25+ automatic accommodations vs. limited support
5. **Consolidation** — Wayground replaces Pear Assessment + 4-5 other tools

## Handling Objections

| They say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| "Teachers love the free tier" | "So do ours. But Wayground's free tier includes engagement, lessons, flashcards, and assessment — not just assessment." |
| "TEI question types are solid" | "Wayground has 18+ question types too — plus gamification that makes students actually complete them." |
| "It integrates with Google Classroom" | "So does Wayground. Plus Clever, ClassLink, Canvas, Schoology, and LMS grade passback." |

## Deal Strategy
- Position on scope: "Pear Assessment does assessment. Wayground does assessment + everything else."
- If teachers already adopted Pear free tier: show how Wayground's free tier gives them more
- Emphasize: same bottoms-up motion, broader platform value

## THE ONE-LINER
> "Pear Assessment is an assessment tool teachers find. Wayground is a platform teachers find — and use for everything."
