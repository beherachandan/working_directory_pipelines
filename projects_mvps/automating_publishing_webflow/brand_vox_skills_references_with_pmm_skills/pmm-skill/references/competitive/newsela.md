# Battlecard: Newsela
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Newsela Inc. (private, VC-backed)
- **HQ:** New York, NY
- **Founded:** 2013
- **Products:** Newsela (leveled reading platform with current events, primary sources, and curriculum-aligned content)
- **Audience:** K-12 teachers (ELA, social studies, science, SEL — content-heavy subjects)
- **Users:** 2M+ teachers, 40M+ students (claimed)
- **Pricing:** Free tier (limited); Newsela Essentials+ ~$5-12/student/year; district pricing varies
- **Key Differentiator:** Auto-leveled current events and nonfiction content aligned to curricula — five Lexile reading levels per article
- **ESSA Evidence:** Level III evidence (for specific use cases)
- **Accessibility:** Some; text-to-speech and reading level adjustment. No comprehensive IEP/504 suite.
- **AI Features:** AI-powered reading level adaptation (auto-leveling); limited AI beyond content leveling

## Internal Assessment
Newsela is a content powerhouse, not a platform competitor. They own "leveled nonfiction reading" for K-12. The competitive dynamic is different from Kahoot or Nearpod: Newsela competes in the reading/content space, not engagement or assessment. Our play: Newsela is content; Wayground is a platform. Districts can use Newsela content inside Wayground workflows, or replace Newsela with Wayground's own content generation capabilities.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- "Every student reads at their level" — 5 Lexile levels per article, auto-adapted
- Current events as curriculum — Licensed content from AP, Washington Post, Scientific American, etc.
- Cross-curricular content — ELA, science, social studies, SEL articles
- Curriculum alignment — Content mapped to state standards and popular curricula

**What they don't talk about:**
- No interactive engagement (no games, no competitive quizzes, no gamification)
- No video content or interactive video
- No teacher-created content (teachers use Newsela's library, can't create their own)
- No game-based practice or flashcards
- Limited assessment beyond reading comprehension quizzes
- No common assessment or item banking
- No AI tutoring or student-facing AI
- Content dependency — value proposition tied to licensed content library

## Feature Comparison (18 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 13** — Live games, interactive video, flashcards, teacher content creation, async assignments, common assessments, AI tutoring, AI generation, game-based engagement, IEP/504 accommodations (breadth), district admin analytics, content marketplace
- **Even: 2** — Standards alignment, MLL/ELL support (different approaches)
- **Newsela leads: 3** — Leveled nonfiction reading library, current events content, cross-curricular licensed content depth

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Platform vs. content library
> "Newsela gives you articles. Wayground gives you a learning platform."
Newsela is a content library with reading comprehension quizzes. Teachers still need games, video lessons, flashcards, assessments, and accommodations from other tools. Wayground is the complete platform.
- *Landmine:* "Beyond Newsela reading, what tools do your teachers use for practice, games, video lessons, and assessments?"

### Win #2: Teacher agency
> "Newsela decides the content. Wayground lets teachers create theirs."
Newsela's content is licensed and curated — teachers choose from Newsela's library. They can't create their own leveled content. Wayground lets teachers create anything: quizzes, lessons, video content, assessments — and AI helps level it.
- *Landmine:* "When your teachers need content that isn't in Newsela's library — a local topic, a unique text, a teacher-created assessment — what tool do they use?"

### Win #3: Engagement beyond reading
> "Reading comprehension is one skill. Students also need practice, review, and assessment across every subject."
Newsela is reading-focused. Students read articles and answer comprehension questions. Wayground offers reading, practice, competitive games, interactive video, flashcards, and formal assessments across every subject and modality.
- *Landmine:* "What percentage of your instruction is reading-based? For math practice, science review, and test prep, what tools do your teachers use?"

### Win #4: Assessment depth
> "Newsela quizzes check reading comprehension. Wayground assesses mastery across the curriculum."
Newsela's built-in quizzes are reading comprehension checks — not standards-aligned formative assessments with item analysis. Wayground provides real assessment data: item-level analysis, mastery tracking, standards reporting, and common assessment support.
- *Landmine:* "When your admin team needs formative assessment data across your district, does Newsela provide that? Or do you need a separate tool?"

## Handling Objections

| They say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| "Teachers love Newsela articles" | "Keep the articles. Add Wayground for everything Newsela can't do: games, video, assessments, accommodations." |
| "We need leveled reading content" | "Wayground's AI generates leveled content too. Plus teachers can create their own." |
| "Newsela has ESSA evidence" | "So do we. And we cover engagement, assessment, and accommodations — not just reading." |
| "It covers all our content subjects" | "It covers reading in those subjects. What handles practice, review, and formative assessment?" |
| "Students like the current events" | "Current events are great content. But content needs a delivery platform with engagement and data." |
| "We're locked into a Newsela contract" | "Run a Wayground pilot alongside it. By renewal, you'll have data showing which platform does more." |

## Landmine Questions
1. "Beyond reading articles, what tools handle games, video lessons, and formative assessments for your teachers?"
2. "Can your teachers create their own leveled content in Newsela, or are they limited to the library?"
3. "What formative assessment data does Newsela provide beyond reading comprehension scores?"
4. "How are students with IEPs accommodated beyond reading level adjustments?"
5. "What's the total cost of Newsela + your game tool + your assessment tool + your video tool?"
6. "When your admin needs district-wide assessment data, does Newsela provide it?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: Newsela is entrenched for reading**
- Don't replace Newsela — complement it. Target the gaps: games, video, assessment, accommodations
- "Newsela is content. Wayground is the platform. You need both, or you need Wayground alone."

**Scenario B: District evaluating content platforms**
- Expand criteria beyond content: engagement, assessment, teacher creation, accommodations
- "If you're buying content, Newsela is great. If you're buying a learning platform, let's compare."

**Scenario C: Budget consolidation**
- TCO: Newsela + game tool + video tool + assessment platform vs. Wayground
- "Wayground's AI generates leveled content too. You may not need a separate content subscription."

## THE ONE-LINER
> "Newsela curates content. Wayground powers learning. One is a library. The other is a platform."
