# Battlecard: Brisk Teaching
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Brisk Teaching (private, venture-backed)
- **HQ:** United States
- **Founded:** ~2023
- **Products:** Brisk Teaching Chrome extension (overlay for Google Docs, Slides, Classroom, Canvas, YouTube)
- **Audience:** K-12 teachers; browser extension for existing workflows
- **Users:** Rapidly growing; exact figures not disclosed
- **Pricing:** Freemium; school/district plans ~$3-8/teacher/year (estimated)
- **Distribution:** Chrome extension only — zero adoption friction
- **Key Differentiator:** Lightweight overlay for existing tools; non-intrusive, no platform buildout
- **ESSA Evidence:** None
- **Accessibility:** None documented
- **AI Features:** Teacher-facing only — AI feedback on writing, quiz generation, lesson planning, differentiation suggestions, rubric generation

## ⚠️ Internal Assessment — REAL THREAT
Brisk is a real competitive threat despite its lightweight design. The core threat is NOT feature parity but **zero adoption friction**. Teachers install an extension, run AI commands in existing tools, and never leave their workflow. Brisk doesn't need a platform because it runs inside everyone else's. This exposes our core vulnerability: we ARE a platform, which means switching costs. **Worth tracking actively.**

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- Zero friction — Install extension, use immediately, no change to workflow
- AI copilot, not platform — AI superpowers in Google Docs, Slides, Classroom, Canvas, YouTube
- Teacher productivity — Reduces prep workload significantly
- Lightweight and extensible — Works everywhere, no data migration, no change management

**What they don't talk about:**
- Assessment rigor (generates quizzes but no standards alignment, item banking, or common assessments)
- Student data & analytics (no longitudinal tracking, no mastery analytics, no adaptive learning)
- Student-facing AI (all teacher-side only)
- Accessibility & compliance (no IEP/504, no ESSA, no admin dashboard)
- District visibility (Chrome extension = no admin console, no compliance tracking)

## Feature Comparison (20 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 13** — Interactive lessons, video, flashcards, common assessments, standards alignment, analytics, mastery tracking, IEP/504, MLL, AI tutoring (student), LMS integration (depth), district admin, ESSA evidence
- **Even: 2** — AI quiz generation, LMS integration
- **Brisk leads: 4** — AI writing feedback, AI lesson planning, AI differentiation suggestions, AI rubric generation, adoption friction (zero)
- **⚠️ KEY:** Wayground wins on 13/20 features, BUT the picture is misleading. Brisk wins on what matters most for teacher adoption: zero friction + AI prep tools.

## Why We Win

### Win #1: AI layer vs. AI platform
> "Brisk helps teachers work faster in five tools. Wayground eliminates four of those tools."
Brisk generates content for delivery through other platforms. Wayground generates AND delivers. Brisk is fragmentation with a helpful UI. Wayground is consolidation.
- *Landmine:* "How many times a week does a teacher log into a different tool to manage quizzes, lessons, video, and assessments?"

### Win #2: Data continuity
> "Brisk generates quizzes. Wayground generates quizzes AND tracks whether students learned."
Content generated in Brisk lives in disconnected systems. No longitudinal data. Wayground has complete data continuity.
- *Landmine:* "When a teacher uses AI to generate a quiz, does the system tell her which students learned? Or does she check each platform separately?"

### Win #3: Accessibility and compliance
> "Brisk has no accommodation suite. When a special ed student needs extended time, who handles it?"
Brisk-generated content relies on underlying tools' accessibility. Wayground guarantees 25+ accommodations on every piece of content.
- *Landmine:* "Do your special education students need extended time, text-to-speech, or color adjustments? If your AI tool can't guarantee that, whose job is it?"

### Win #4: Admin visibility
> "Brisk is a Chrome extension. Is IT tracking it? Are admins seeing what teachers do with AI?"
No admin console, no usage reporting, no compliance audit trail. Wayground has full district dashboard.
- *Landmine:* "If every teacher installs an AI extension, what does IT see? What does your superintendent see?"

## Landmine Questions
1. "When a teacher generates a quiz in Brisk and deploys it in Google Classroom, can you see which students mastered the concept?"
2. "If Brisk saves teachers time on prep but your stack still has five tools, how much are you really saving?"
3. "IT installed a Chrome extension on all teacher machines. What does the admin dashboard show about usage and student data?"
4. "How do you ensure a student with an IEP on a quiz generated by an AI extension gets extended time automatically?"
5. "Your board wants evidence AI is improving test scores. How do you show that when content lives across five platforms?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: District piloting Brisk, satisfied with productivity gains**
- Lead with next level: "Brisk handles prep. Wayground handles prep AND delivery AND assessment AND analytics."
- Frame as evolution, not replacement
- Target admin, not teachers

**Scenario B: Head-to-head evaluation**
- Push for assessment, accessibility, compliance, and outcomes in RFP criteria
- Show full workflow: prep → deliver → assess → analyze
- Brisk can only show prep in isolation

**Scenario C: Individual teachers adopted Brisk, you're trying to move the district**
- Offer side-by-side pilot
- "Teachers don't lose their productivity tools. They gain a complete platform."
- Focus on scalability and admin needs

## THE ONE-LINER
> "Brisk helps teachers work faster in five tools. Wayground eliminates four of those tools AND tells you if it's working."
