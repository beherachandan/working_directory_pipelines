# Battlecard: i-Ready (Curriculum Associates)
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Curriculum Associates (private, employee-owned)
- **HQ:** North Billerica, Massachusetts
- **Founded:** 1969 (i-Ready launched ~2011)
- **Products:** i-Ready Diagnostic (adaptive assessment), i-Ready Personalized Instruction (adaptive practice), i-Ready Classroom (print+digital curriculum)
- **Audience:** K-8 math and ELA (expanding to K-12)
- **Users:** 11M+ students, 1.5M+ teachers (claimed)
- **Pricing:** $12-25/student/year (varies by module); district deals $50K-500K+
- **Key Differentiator:** ESSA Level III evidence, adaptive diagnostic + instruction in one ecosystem
- **ESSA Evidence:** Yes — Level III (i-Ready Diagnostic and Personalized Instruction)
- **Accessibility:** Basic; some accommodation support but not comprehensive
- **AI Features:** Adaptive learning engine (not generative AI); no AI tutoring, no teacher-facing AI tools

## Internal Assessment
i-Ready is one of our most formidable competitors at the district level. Unlike game-based tools, i-Ready competes in the "core instruction and assessment" category with genuine ESSA evidence and deep curriculum alignment. They're the gold standard for adaptive diagnostics in K-8 math/ELA. Our play is NOT to out-assess i-Ready. It's to show that engagement, teacher agency, and cross-subject coverage matter just as much as adaptive diagnostics.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- "One connected learning path" — Diagnostic → personalized instruction → monitoring → all linked
- Growth measurement — Longitudinal student progress tracking is their signature
- ESSA evidence — Legitimate, peer-reviewed evidence of impact on student outcomes
- Curriculum integration — i-Ready Classroom provides full print+digital curriculum

**What they don't talk about:**
- Student engagement (i-Ready is functional, not fun)
- Teacher-created content (teachers can't create assessments or lessons)
- Cross-subject coverage (math and ELA only — no science, social studies, etc.)
- Game-based learning (no competitive or engaging game modes)
- Teacher autonomy (teachers follow prescribed paths, limited customization)
- AI innovation (adaptive engine, not generative AI — no AI tutoring, no creation tools)

## Feature Comparison (19 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 11** — Live games, teacher-created content, interactive video, flashcards, cross-subject coverage, AI question generation, AI tutoring, game-based engagement, teacher autonomy, content marketplace, MLL/ELL breadth
- **Even: 3** — Standards alignment, LMS integrations, district admin dashboard
- **i-Ready leads: 5** — Adaptive diagnostics, longitudinal growth data, adaptive instruction path, ESSA evidence depth, curriculum integration (print+digital)

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Engagement drives completion
> "i-Ready is medicine. Wayground is the spoonful of sugar AND the medicine."
i-Ready completion rates are a known district pain point. Students avoid i-Ready because it's not engaging. Wayground's game-based approach means students actually WANT to practice, leading to higher completion and more data for teachers.
- *Landmine:* "What are your i-Ready completion rates? Are students finishing their personalized instruction paths, or abandoning them?"

### Win #2: Teacher agency and content creation
> "i-Ready prescribes the path. Wayground lets teachers lead the way."
i-Ready is a closed system — teachers can't create their own assessments, add their own content, or customize instruction. Wayground gives teachers full creative control while still providing AI-powered recommendations.
- *Landmine:* "Can your teachers create their own formative assessments in i-Ready? Or do they use a separate tool for that?"

### Win #3: Cross-subject coverage
> "Math and ELA are two subjects. Teachers teach seven."
i-Ready covers math and ELA. Period. Science, social studies, health, world languages, CTE — none covered. Wayground works across every subject. For districts paying $12-25/student for two subjects, the per-subject cost is steep when teachers still need other tools.
- *Landmine:* "What tools do your science, social studies, and specials teachers use for formative assessment and practice? Do they have the same data visibility as math and ELA?"

### Win #4: Complementary positioning
> "Keep i-Ready for diagnostics. Add Wayground for everything else."
Don't try to replace i-Ready's diagnostic. It's best-in-class. Instead, position Wayground as the engagement and formative assessment layer that complements i-Ready and covers the subjects i-Ready doesn't touch.
- *Landmine:* "Between diagnostic windows, what are your teachers using for daily formative assessment and practice across all subjects?"

### Win #5: Cost per subject
> "You're paying $25/student for two subjects. What about the other five?"
i-Ready's per-student cost only covers math and ELA. Add up the total edtech spend across all subjects, and Wayground's cross-subject platform becomes dramatically cheaper per capability.
- *Landmine:* "What's your total edtech spend per student across all subjects? What would it look like if one platform covered formative assessment for everything?"

## Handling Objections

2. "What formative assessment tools do your science and social studies teachers have?"
3. "Between diagnostic windows, how are your teachers checking daily understanding?"
4. "Can your teachers create their own assessments in i-Ready, or do they need a separate tool?"
5. "What's your per-student cost when you add up i-Ready + tools for every other subject?"
6. "Has your board asked about engagement data alongside growth data? What does student sentiment look like?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: i-Ready is entrenched, district is renewing**
- Don't fight i-Ready — complement it. "Keep diagnostics. Add engagement."
- Target subjects i-Ready doesn't cover (science, social studies, specials)
- Position as the formative layer between diagnostic windows

**Scenario B: District is evaluating i-Ready for the first time**
- Lead with engagement + cross-subject + teacher agency
- Acknowledge i-Ready's diagnostic strength, then expand criteria
- "If the RFP is just adaptive diagnostics for math/ELA, i-Ready wins. If it's engagement + assessment across all subjects, we win."

**Scenario C: Head-to-head for limited budget**
- TCO analysis: i-Ready ($25/student × math/ELA only) vs. Wayground (all subjects, all modes)
- Completion rate data: "Students complete Wayground work because they want to, not because they have to"
- Show the gap: "i-Ready doesn't cover your other five subjects. Budget for that."

## THE ONE-LINER
> "i-Ready diagnoses the problem. Wayground helps students actually fix it — with engagement, across every subject."
