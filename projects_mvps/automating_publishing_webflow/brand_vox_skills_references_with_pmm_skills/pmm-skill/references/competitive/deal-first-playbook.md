# Deal-First Competitive Playbook
## Klue-inspired: Deal-specific, stage-aware, teacher-voice-first
## Built from 100+ call transcript analysis (Mar 7 2026)

---

## How to Use This Playbook

When a competitor is detected in a deal:
1. **Identify the competitor** from the call/CRM
2. **Identify the deal stage** (Discovery, Demo, Proposal, Follow-up, Risk Recovery)
3. **Look up the competitor section below** → Find the stage-specific play
4. **Use the teacher-frustration framing** — lead with their pain, not our features
5. **Reference real transcript quotes** when coaching reps

This is NOT a static battlecard. It's a decision tree: competitor + stage + buyer context → what to say.

## NEARPOD / RENAISSANCE

### What Prospects Actually Say (from transcripts)
- Keith (NYC D14): "Kids go through the motions on iReady" — unprompted comparison to Wayground's engagement
- Mara (South Redford): Nearpod not renewed. DNA going away. District looking for consolidation.
- Ross (Jordan SD): "Nearpod's state funding is on the chopping block" — budget uncertainty for Nearpod users
- LaTrice (Jackson): "So many different platforms" — using Nearpod + 4 other tools. Consolidation hasn't happened.

### Teacher Frustration (lead with this)
"Teachers bought Nearpod hoping to stop juggling tools. Three years later, they're still opening Kahoot for review games, Edpuzzle for videos, and a separate tool for assessments. The consolidation promise didn't deliver."

### Stage-Specific Plays

**Discovery:** Don't mention Nearpod first. Ask: "What tools are your teachers using today for instruction, assessment, and engagement?" Let them name the sprawl. Then: "How many of those did you have before Nearpod?"

**Demo:** Show the full spectrum in one session — game energy → structured lesson → video → assessment → accommodations → AI tutor. Don't compare feature-by-feature. Show the workflow a teacher actually uses in a week. The contrast speaks for itself.

**Proposal:** Lead with TCO, not per-student price. "Nearpod + Kahoot + Edpuzzle + flashcard tool + assessment tool = $X. Wayground alone = $Y." Make the consolidation math real.

**Follow-up (they have Nearpod):** Don't attack. Ask: "Since adopting Nearpod, has your tool count gone down or up?" If up: "That's what we hear from most districts. The consolidation opportunity is still open."

**Risk Recovery (they're leaning Nearpod):** Don't panic-sell. Acknowledge: "Nearpod is a solid lesson tool. The question is: do you want a lesson tool, or do you want a platform that makes three other subscriptions unnecessary?"

### Displacement Signals (from transcripts)
- Nearpod not renewed → consolidation window
- State funding uncertainty for Nearpod → budget anxiety = our stability advantage
- "Same product since acquisition" → innovation narrative
- Teachers still using 4+ tools alongside Nearpod → consolidation promise unfulfilled
- iReady/Star assessment data separate from Nearpod instruction data → data continuity gap

### The One Thing to Remember
Don't position against Nearpod. Position against TOOL SPRAWL. Nearpod is one symptom. Wayground is the cure.

## iREADY / CURRICULUM ASSOCIATES

### What Prospects Actually Say (from transcripts)
- Keith (NYC D14): "Kids go through the motions on iReady" — engagement deficit is the frustration
- Multiple districts: iReady used for diagnostics, but teachers need something ELSE for daily instruction

### Teacher Frustration (lead with this)
"Teachers tell us their students log into iReady because they have to, not because they want to. The diagnostic data is valuable — but if students aren't engaged in the daily learning, what are we diagnosing?"

### Stage-Specific Plays

**Discovery:** "What does a typical week look like for your students? When they're on iReady, what does engagement look like vs. other tools?" Let the teacher describe the gap.

**Demo:** Don't compete with iReady's diagnostic depth. Show what happens BETWEEN diagnostics — daily formative assessment with engagement. "iReady tells you where students are. Wayground helps them get where they need to be — and they actually want to do it."

**Proposal:** Complement, don't replace. "Keep iReady for your diagnostic windows. Add Wayground for the 170 days between diagnostics. The combined data gives you the full picture."

### The One Thing to Remember
Don't fight iReady on diagnostics — they're stronger there. Fight on the 170 days between assessments where students need engaged daily practice.

## MASTERYCONNECT / INSTRUCTURE

### What Prospects Actually Say (from transcripts)
- Nalin's CA SKO deck: "Performance Matters — terrible authoring, only 16% create assessments"
- MasteryConnect: "degraded post-Instructure acquisition"
- Multiple reps report districts frustrated with MC's assessment creation workflow

### Teacher Frustration (lead with this)
"Assessment coordinators spend hours building assessments in tools that were designed for data collection, not content creation. They're fighting the authoring experience instead of focusing on what matters — understanding student learning."

### Stage-Specific Plays

**Discovery:** "How do your teachers currently create and share common assessments? What's the most time-consuming part of that process?" If they mention authoring pain → we win on creation experience.

**Demo:** Lead with the authoring flow. Show: find a standard → generate questions with AI → customize → share with one click → get results with standards breakdown. The simplicity IS the differentiation.

**Proposal:** "Your district is already doing common assessments. The question is: are your teachers spending their time creating assessments or fighting the tool? Wayground CA cuts authoring time from hours to minutes."

### The One Thing to Remember
MC/Performance Matters won on data analytics. They lost on the teacher experience of CREATING the assessment. We win on authoring + distribution + the fact that teachers already use Wayground for daily instruction.

## KAHOOT / BLOOKET / GIMKIT

### What Prospects Actually Say (from transcripts)
- John Frame (Corinth): BLOCKED Kahoot and Blooket in the district. Students chose Wayground organically.
- LDeckman (Brighton): "Teachers using Kahoot, Gimkit, the whole thing. We need one that's compliant."
- Multiple districts: "We need data from game day, not just fun."

### Teacher Frustration (lead with this)
"Teachers love the energy of game day. Administrators love data. Kahoot gives you energy without data. Assessment tools give you data without energy. The question is: why choose?"

### Stage-Specific Plays

**Discovery:** "When your teachers run review games, do they get assessment data from those sessions? Can they see which standards students missed?" The answer is always no with Kahoot/Blooket.

**Demo:** Run an actual game. Let them feel the energy. THEN show the data dashboard — by standard, by student, by question. "Same energy. Actual data."

**Proposal:** Consolidation: "You're paying for Kahoot ($X) + a separate assessment tool ($Y). Wayground replaces both."

### The One Thing to Remember
Don't trash Kahoot's fun factor — teachers LOVE it. Show that Wayground has the SAME energy AND the data that administrators need. Fun + data = no compromise.

## STAGE-INDEXED PLAYBOOK

### Discovery
**Goal:** Understand their world before showing ours
**Model:** Haywood/Dan Gross — "Before we get into Wayground, do you have any instructional goals you're tying to tech? What about PLCs or coaches?"
**Good:** Prospect talks 70% of the time. You learn their tools, frustrations, budget reality, competitive landscape.
**Bad:** Jumping to product demo before understanding needs. Spending 5+ min on weather without transitioning to business.
**Empathy checkpoint:** "What's the most frustrating part of [their current workflow]?"

### Demo/Evaluation
**Goal:** Show RELEVANT features based on what you learned in discovery
**Model:** Katelin (Robeson group training) — "What would you like me to highlight?" + adapt based on audience
**Good:** Demo connects directly to stated needs. Specialist (Justin/Jess) handles product depth, AE stays strategic.
**Bad:** Generic feature walkthrough disconnected from discovery. 67-min demo that covers everything instead of what matters.
**Empathy checkpoint:** "Does this match what you're looking for, or should I adjust?"

### Proposal
**Goal:** Make the financial case while respecting their reality
**Model:** Dan Gross (Vernon) — "Go with the old quote, it's cheaper." Trust over short-term revenue.
**Good:** TCO comparison, not per-student price. Transparent about cooperative/volume pricing. Flexible options (pilot, phased, school-level start).
**Bad:** Year-end urgency when prospect has no certified budget. Standard pricing without volume/cooperative options.
**Empathy checkpoint:** "Does this pricing model work for how your district purchases?"

### Follow-up/Check-in
**Goal:** Bring value, detect expansion signals
**Model:** Michelle Noon — Bring usage data first, then ask open question about what's next
**Good:** Reference prior progress. Show impact data. Open-ended: "What's changed since we last talked?"
**Bad:** "Just checking in" with no value. Repeating the demo instead of building on it.
**Empathy checkpoint:** "What's been the biggest win since we connected last?"

### Risk Recovery
**Goal:** Rebuild trust, not re-sell
**Model:** Katelin (Robeson Call 7) — Brought Tommy (manager), LISTENED to Lisa's frustration, didn't argue
**Good:** Bring manager. Listen. Validate. Acknowledge what went wrong. Let the prospect set the pace.
**Bad:** Defending past behavior. Offering bigger discount as a band-aid. Selling harder when they said "pushy."
**Empathy checkpoint:** "I hear you. What would make this relationship feel right going forward?"

### Closing
**Goal:** Get commitment without creating pressure
**Model:** Dan Gross (PS 178) — Recognized deal was already done, didn't re-pitch, connected to CS
**Good:** Confirm decision. Connect to CS immediately. Start onboarding conversation.
**Bad:** Re-demoing at closing stage. Adding features/discounts that weren't asked for.
**Empathy checkpoint:** "What do you need from us to make this transition smooth?"

### Onboarding
**Goal:** Enable, don't sell
**Model:** Katelin (Robeson Call 8) — CS-led, AE monitors, Justin handles training
**Good:** CS takes lead. Training focused on teacher needs, not product features. AE stays available but doesn't dominate.
**Bad:** AE continuing to sell during onboarding. Generic training not tailored to the district's use case.
**Empathy checkpoint:** "What would success look like for your teachers in the first 30 days?"
