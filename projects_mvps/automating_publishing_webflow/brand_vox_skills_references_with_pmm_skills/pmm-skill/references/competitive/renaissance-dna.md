# Battlecard: Renaissance DnA (Renaissance Learning)
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: CA Competitive Landscape, Market Survey Data*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Renaissance Learning (owned by Francisco Partners, ~$5.4B valuation)
- **Products:** DnA (assessment analytics), Star Assessments (adaptive screening), Accelerated Reader, myON, Lalilo, Freckle, Nearpod, Flocabulary
- **Audience:** K-12 districts — strength in adaptive screening/diagnostics (Star) and reading (AR)
- **Market Share:** ~3.2% of district-mandated CFA tools for DnA specifically (per CA deck survey)
- **Key Differentiator:** Robust item bank, standards alignment, Renaissance ecosystem breadth
- **Pricing:** Suite-level district pricing; often bundled with Star + AR
- **Category:** Tier 1 Direct CA Platform Competitor (when bundled with Star)

## Internal Assessment
Renaissance DnA is the assessment analytics module within the Renaissance suite. Its strength is the item bank and standards alignment. Its weakness is the same as every legacy assessment tool: clinical testing feel, no engagement, teachers view it as "another test tool." The Renaissance suite pitch is powerful (Star + AR + DnA + Nearpod = "everything"), but each product is separate with separate data — it's a portfolio, not a platform.

## Core Weaknesses
- **Clinical/testing feel** — Teachers and students view DnA as pure testing, no engagement
- **No daily classroom use** — Assessment-window tool only
- **Separate products** — Star data, DnA data, Nearpod data all live separately; no unified view
- **No gamification** — Zero engagement design
- **Expensive suite** — Full Renaissance bundle is costly; often need all-or-nothing commitment
- **Suite ≠ platform** — Separate products with separate UX, separate data, separate logins

## Why We Win
1. **One platform vs. suite of products** — Wayground is one UX, one data stream, one login
2. **Daily engagement** — Students use Wayground daily; they use DnA during testing windows
3. **Teacher adoption** — Teachers choose Wayground; they're mandated to use DnA
4. **Modern AI** — AI Create, Enhance, Analyze vs. legacy item bank browsing
5. **Unified data** — Instruction and assessment data in one stream, not siloed by product

## Handling Objections

| They say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| "Renaissance gives us everything" | "Renaissance gives you separate products. Can a teacher see Nearpod lesson data alongside Star screening data alongside DnA benchmark data in one view?" |
| "We need Star for diagnostics" | "Keep Star. Star is screening/diagnostics — we don't compete there. Replace DnA + Nearpod with Wayground for instruction, daily assessment, and CA." |
| "The item bank is comprehensive" | "Wayground's AI generates items on-demand to any standard. Plus teachers build and share their own — which drives the adoption that makes data meaningful." |

## Deal Strategy
- **Don't fight the full suite** — Agree Star and AR have value
- **Unbundle the instruction + assessment layer** — "Keep Star. Replace DnA + Nearpod with Wayground."
- **Show unified data advantage** — One platform with one data stream vs. portfolio of separate products

## THE ONE-LINER
> "Renaissance sells a suite. Wayground is a platform. One data stream, one login, one experience teachers and students love."
