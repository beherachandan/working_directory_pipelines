# Battlecard: Mastery Connect (Instructure / Canvas)
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: CA Competitive Landscape, Customer Feedback, Internal Intel*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** MasteryConnect (acquired by Instructure, 2020) — part of the Canvas ecosystem
- **HQ:** Salt Lake City, UT (Instructure HQ)
- **Founded:** 2010
- **Products:** Standards-based assessment, mastery tracking, item banks, bubble sheet scanning
- **Audience:** K-12 districts — strength in standards mastery tracking and Canvas-integrated schools
- **Market Share:** ~6.4% of district-mandated CFA tools (per CA deck survey data)
- **Key Differentiator:** Deep standards mastery tracking, bubble sheet scanning, tight Canvas LMS integration
- **Pricing:** District-level pricing; typically bundled with Canvas contracts
- **Category:** Tier 1 Direct CA Platform Competitor

## Internal Assessment
Mastery Connect was once the gold standard for standards-based assessment in K-12. Since the Instructure acquisition (2020), product development has stagnated and customer satisfaction has declined significantly. The competitive dynamic: MC has deeper analytics TODAY but no engagement layer, no daily classroom use, and reliability issues that are pushing districts to evaluate alternatives.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- "Standards mastery tracking" — granular mastery views by standard, student, class
- "Canvas integration" — seamless within the Instructure ecosystem
- "Bubble sheet scanning" — bridge paper and digital assessment workflows
- "Item bank marketplace" — teacher-created and publisher item banks

**What they don't talk about:**
- No gamification or engagement — students and teachers view it as "another test tool"
- No daily classroom use case — only used during assessment windows
- Product stagnation post-acquisition — minimal new features since 2020
- Reliability issues — districts report system outages and degraded customer support
- Tied to Canvas ecosystem — less attractive for non-Canvas districts
- No content creation tools (AI or otherwise)
- No accommodation suite comparable to Wayground's 25+
- Teacher adoption is low — it's an admin tool, not a teacher tool

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Teacher adoption
> "The best assessment data comes from a tool teachers actually use. Mastery Connect is used during testing windows. Wayground is used every day."
MC has low teacher adoption because there's nothing for teachers to love. It's an assessment management tool, not a teaching tool. Wayground gets used daily for engagement, then extends to CA when districts are ready.
- *Landmine:* "How often do teachers voluntarily open Mastery Connect outside of testing windows?"

### Win #2: Engagement drives completion
> "Students engage with Wayground because it feels like learning, not testing. That means higher completion and more reliable data."
MC assessments feel like tests. Students give minimum effort. Wayground's gamification drives genuine engagement, which means the data actually reflects student knowledge.
- *Landmine:* "What's your student completion rate on MC assessments? Do students take them seriously, or rush through?"

### Win #3: Reliability and support
> "Districts are reporting reliability issues and degraded support since the Instructure acquisition."
Real customer quotes: "Reliability and customer service became terrible after acquired by Instructure." (Moriarty-Edgewood, 2K students). This is a recurring theme that opens the door.
- *Landmine:* "How has your experience with Mastery Connect support been since the Instructure acquisition?"

### Win #4: Platform consolidation
> "Mastery Connect does assessment. You still need Kahoot for engagement, Nearpod for lessons, and Google Forms for quick checks. Wayground replaces all of them."
MC is a single-purpose assessment tool. Districts using MC still maintain 4-5 other tools for daily instruction. Wayground consolidates all of them.
- *Landmine:* "Beyond MC, how many other tools do your teachers use for daily instruction? What's the total cost of that stack?"

## Landmine Questions
1. "How often do teachers voluntarily use Mastery Connect outside of mandated testing windows?"
2. "Since the Instructure acquisition, how has product development and support changed?"
3. "What's the total cost of MC + your engagement tool + your lesson delivery tool?"
4. "How do students with IEP/504 accommodations experience MC assessments? Is it automatic or manual?"
5. "Can teachers create engaging daily activities in MC, or is it assessment-only?"
6. "What percentage of your teachers actively create assessments in MC vs. just administering what's assigned?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: MC is entrenched but pain is emerging**
- Lead with teacher adoption story — "The best CA data comes from a tool teachers love"
- Reference reliability issues gently — "We're hearing some districts have had challenges post-acquisition"
- Propose parallel pilot: run Wayground alongside MC for one unit, compare adoption

**Scenario B: Canvas district considering MC**
- Don't fight the Canvas integration — show Wayground integrates too
- Expand the conversation: "MC is assessment-only. Your teachers still need engagement, lessons, and accommodations."
- Position as complement first, replacement second

**Scenario C: MC renewal approaching**
- Time the conversation to renewal window
- Show TCO: MC + engagement tools + lesson tools vs. Wayground alone
- Provide adoption data from their district: "Your teachers already use Wayground for X games — MC adoption is Y"
