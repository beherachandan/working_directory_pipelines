# Battlecard: MagicSchool AI
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** MagicSchool AI Inc. (private, VC-backed)
- **HQ:** United States
- **Founded:** ~2023
- **Products:** MagicSchool (AI tools for teachers: lesson planning, quiz generation, rubrics, IEP drafting, differentiation, email drafting)
- **Audience:** K-12 teachers (teacher-facing only, no student product)
- **Users:** 3M+ teachers claimed
- **Pricing:** Freemium; school/district plans $3-10/teacher/year (estimated)
- **Key Differentiator:** Broadest suite of teacher-facing AI tools in EdTech (40+ AI tools)
- **ESSA Evidence:** None
- **Accessibility:** None documented
- **AI Features:** Teacher-facing only — lesson plans, quiz generation, rubric creation, IEP drafting, differentiation suggestions, email writing, text leveling

## Internal Assessment
MagicSchool is the most visible AI-for-teachers brand in EdTech. Their risk is brand recognition in the "AI for education" conversation. Districts evaluating AI often discover MagicSchool first. However, MagicSchool is fundamentally a teacher productivity tool that generates content but doesn't deliver it. Teachers use MagicSchool to create → then go elsewhere to teach. Wayground creates AND delivers AND assesses.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- "AI tools for every teacher task" — 40+ AI tools covering lesson planning, quiz generation, rubrics, IEP drafting, differentiation
- Teacher time savings — "Save hours per week on prep"
- Safe AI for education — Content filtering, student data privacy emphasis
- Freemium accessibility — Free tier drives adoption

**What they don't talk about:**
- No delivery platform (generates content, doesn't deliver it)
- No student-facing experience or AI tutoring
- No assessment data or analytics
- No engagement/gamification
- No accommodations infrastructure
- No standards-aligned assessment or item banking
- No district admin dashboard with learning data

## Feature Comparison (18 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 13** — Interactive lessons, video, live games, flashcards, async assignments, common assessments, standards alignment, analytics, IEP/504 accommodations, MLL support, AI tutoring (student), district admin, ESSA evidence
- **Even: 2** — AI quiz generation, content creation speed
- **MagicSchool leads: 3** — AI lesson planning, AI rubric generation, AI IEP drafting

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Create AND deliver
> "MagicSchool generates content. Wayground generates, delivers, assesses, and tracks content."
Teachers use MagicSchool → export → paste into Google Classroom/Canvas/LMS → manually track. Wayground does the entire workflow in one platform.
- *Landmine:* "After generating a quiz in MagicSchool, what platform does the student take it on? How do you track who learned what?"

### Win #2: Student-facing AI vs. teacher-only AI
> "MagicSchool helps teachers work faster. Wayground helps students learn better."
MagicSchool AI is entirely teacher-facing. No AI tutoring, no adaptive hints, no student-facing intelligence. Wayground has AI for both sides: teacher creation + student learning support.
- *Landmine:* "Does your AI tool help teachers only, or does it also help students learn in real-time?"

### Win #3: Assessment and outcomes data
> "MagicSchool creates quizzes. Wayground creates quizzes AND tells you if students learned."
MagicSchool generated content lives in disconnected systems. No longitudinal data, no mastery tracking. Wayground has complete data continuity from content creation to student outcome measurement.
- *Landmine:* "When you generate a quiz with AI, how do you know which students mastered the concept and which didn't?"

### Win #4: Consolidation
> "MagicSchool is a layer on top of your fragmented stack. Wayground IS the stack."
Teachers using MagicSchool still need: an LMS for delivery, a game tool for engagement, an assessment tool for benchmarks, an accommodation system for compliance. Wayground replaces all of that.
- *Landmine:* "If MagicSchool saves teachers time on prep, but your stack still has five tools, how much are you really saving?"

## Handling Objections

|---|---|
| "Our teachers love MagicSchool" | "They'll love Wayground's AI too. Plus they get delivery, assessment, and analytics in the same place." |
| "MagicSchool has 40+ AI tools" | "Breadth of AI tools ≠ depth of learning impact. Does it track whether students learned?" |
| "It's free" | "Free to generate content. But you still need 4 other tools to deliver it. Let's compare total cost." |
| "We use it for IEP drafting" | "That's valuable. Wayground doesn't replace IEP drafting — it replaces the 4 other tools you're also paying for." |
| "Teachers already know MagicSchool" | "Wayground has the same AI creation tools. Plus everything MagicSchool can't do." |

## Landmine Questions
1. "After your teachers generate content with AI, how many different platforms do they need to deliver, assess, and track student learning?"
2. "Does your AI tool help teachers only, or students too? When a student is stuck, does the AI help them?"
3. "How do you measure whether AI-generated content is actually improving student outcomes?"
4. "If you adopt MagicSchool plus an LMS plus a quiz tool plus an assessment platform, what's the total cost per student?"
5. "What does your admin dashboard show about AI-generated content effectiveness across your district?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: District piloting MagicSchool**
- Acknowledge productivity wins → expand criteria to include delivery, assessment, outcomes
- Target admin with data/evidence gap
- Offer Wayground as "the next level up from AI prep tools"

**Scenario B: Head-to-head evaluation**
- Force scope conversation: "If the RFP only covers AI content generation, you're solving 20% of the problem"
- Demo full workflow: create → deliver → assess → analyze → accommodate
- Bring ESSA evidence

**Scenario C: Teachers love MagicSchool, you're trying to move the district**
- Don't fight MagicSchool love — position Wayground as the platform that makes MagicSchool unnecessary over time
- "Teachers don't lose AI features; they gain a complete platform"

## THE ONE-LINER
