# Battlecard: GoGuardian
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Competitive Intel, SKO Messaging*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** GoGuardian (acquired Pear Deck, 2021; acquired Edulastic, rebranded to Pear Assessment)
- **Products:** GoGuardian Admin (device management), GoGuardian Teacher (classroom management), Pear Deck (interactive presentations), Pear Assessment (assessment), GoGuardian Beacon (self-harm detection), GoGuardian DNS (web filtering)
- **Audience:** K-12 districts — strength in device management, classroom monitoring, Chrome/Chromebook management
- **Key Differentiator:** Device-level student safety and classroom management — screen blocking, web filtering, student monitoring
- **Pricing:** Suite pricing at district level; Pear Deck often included free with GoGuardian bundle
- **Category:** Device management / classroom management — NOT a direct assessment or instruction competitor

## Internal Assessment
GoGuardian competes in a different category (device management, not instruction). The threat is the bundle: districts already paying for GoGuardian get Pear Deck "free," which blocks our lesson delivery pitch. But GoGuardian doesn't solve instruction, assessment, or engagement — it manages devices and screens.

## Core Weaknesses vs. Wayground
- **Different category** — Device management, not instruction or assessment
- **Pear Deck stagnation** — Pear Deck (slides add-on) hasn't evolved; no games, no AI, no accommodations depth
- **No assessment engine** — GoGuardian suite has no formative assessment, no CA, no standards tracking
- **No gamification** — None of their products drive student engagement
- **No AI content creation** — No AI Create, Enhance, or Analyze
- **No accommodations** — Device-level tools, not instructional accommodations
- **Bundle ≠ value** — Getting Pear Deck "free" doesn't solve the assessment or engagement problem

## When You'll See Them
- Districts with heavy Chromebook deployments citing "we already have GoGuardian"
- The objection is usually about Pear Deck being "included" — not GoGuardian itself

## Handling Objections

| They say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| "We already have GoGuardian" | "GoGuardian manages devices. Wayground manages learning. They solve completely different problems." |
| "Pear Deck is free with GoGuardian" | "Free Pear Deck gives you interactive slides. It doesn't give you assessment, gamification, flashcards, video, accommodations, or AI creation." |
| "GoGuardian gives us screen blocking" | "That's device management, not assessment. When do students learn vs. when are they monitored? Wayground powers the learning; GoGuardian manages the device." |
| "We can't justify more spend when GoGuardian includes Pear Deck" | "What's the total cost of GoGuardian + Kahoot + Quizlet + your assessment tool? Wayground consolidates the instruction and assessment side." |

## Landmine Questions
1. "What instruction and assessment capabilities does GoGuardian provide beyond device management?"
2. "Does Pear Deck give you formative assessment data, common assessments, or standards mastery tracking?"
3. "Beyond Pear Deck, what other tools do your teachers use for engagement, practice, and assessment?"
4. "GoGuardian manages screens. Who manages learning?"

## Deal Strategy
- **Don't fight GoGuardian** — different category. Agree they need device management.
- **Fight the bundle perception** — "Free Pear Deck" isn't a learning platform
- **Expand the conversation** — "GoGuardian + Pear Deck solves device management + basic slides. What solves assessment, engagement, accommodations, and CA?"
- **Position as complementary** — "Keep GoGuardian for devices. Add Wayground for instruction."

## THE ONE-LINER
> "GoGuardian manages screens. Wayground powers what's on them."
