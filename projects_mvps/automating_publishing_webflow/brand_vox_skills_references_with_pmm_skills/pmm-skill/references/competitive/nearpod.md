# Battlecard: Nearpod (Renaissance Learning)
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Comprehensive Battlecard (competitive/info.md), SKO Messaging*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Nearpod (acquired by Renaissance Learning, 2021) — Renaissance owned by Francisco Partners (~$5.4B valuation)
- **HQ:** Fort Lauderdale, FL (Renaissance HQ: Bloomington, MN)
- **Founded:** 2012
- **Products:** Nearpod (interactive lessons) + Flocabulary (hip-hop learning videos). Part of Renaissance suite alongside Star Assessments, Accelerated Reader, myON, Lalilo, Freckle.
- **Audience:** K-12 teachers — strength in interactive lesson delivery
- **Users:** 3M+ teachers (claimed), 75%+ US districts (Renaissance umbrella)
- **Pricing:** $3.50-$7.50/student/yr at district level; up to 40% competitive discounts; often free with GoGuardian bundle (Pear Deck legacy)
- **Key Differentiator:** Interactive lesson delivery with VR field trips, slides-based instruction, Renaissance ecosystem integration
- **Category:** Mid-market engagement + lesson delivery competitor

## Internal Assessment
Nearpod is the #1 competitive displacement target for Wayground. It's the tool most often seen alongside Kahoot in district stacks. Post-Renaissance acquisition, the product has stagnated — teachers report it feels the same after 3+ years. The competitive dynamic: consolidation play. Districts pay for Nearpod + Kahoot + Edpuzzle + assessment tool = 4 subscriptions. Wayground replaces all of them.

## Core Weaknesses (4 Structural)
1. **Product stagnation** — Core product hasn't evolved meaningfully since Renaissance acquisition. Minimal new features in 3+ years.
2. **Not a platform — a feature in a suite** — Post-acquisition, Nearpod is one module within Renaissance, not independent. Pricing/roadmap dictated by Renaissance priorities.
3. **AI gap** — No AI tutoring, no student-facing AI, minimal AI features. While competitors shipped AI creation/tutoring/generation, Nearpod's AI story is near-zero.
4. **Assessment is Star, not Nearpod** — Nearpod's formative data is lightweight. No common assessments, no item banks, no standards-based reporting. When Renaissance talks assessment, they mean Star.

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- "Interactive lessons" — slides-based with embedded activities (polls, open-ended, draw it, collaborate boards)
- "VR field trips" — immersive virtual field trips (differentiator but low instructional impact)
- "Renaissance ecosystem" — bundled with Star, AR, myON for comprehensive suite
- "Flocabulary" — hip-hop video content (engaging but limited scope)

**What they don't talk about:**
- No gamification (no leaderboards, no power-ups, no competitive modes)
- No teacher-created content marketplace or AI content creation
- No common assessments, item banks, or standards-based reporting
- Limited accommodations (not comparable to 25+)
- Product roadmap controlled by Renaissance priorities
- In Renaissance suite, instruction (Nearpod) and assessment (Star) are separate products with separate data
- No flashcards, no video lessons with embedded questions, no format switching

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Platform consolidation
> "Nearpod does interactive lessons. Your teachers still need Kahoot for games, Edpuzzle for video, Quizlet for flashcards, and another tool for assessment. Wayground is all of them."
This is the kill shot. The TCO of Nearpod + 3-4 other tools vs. Wayground alone is devastating for Nearpod.
- *Landmine:* "Since adopting Nearpod, how many other tools are teachers still using? Has your tool count gone down?"

### Win #2: Product evolution
> "Three years under Renaissance, and the product looks the same. What has Nearpod shipped lately?"
Districts invest in platforms that are innovating. Wayground ships AI features monthly. Nearpod hasn't had a major release in years.
- *Landmine:* "What's the biggest new feature Nearpod has shipped since Renaissance acquired them?"

### Win #3: Assessment continuity
> "When a teacher finishes a Nearpod lesson, where does the formative data go? Can they see it alongside Star data in one place?"
In the Renaissance suite, Nearpod data and Star data live in separate systems. There's no unified instruction-to-assessment data flow. Wayground keeps everything in one platform.
- *Landmine:* "When a teacher finishes a Nearpod lesson, where does the formative data go? Can they see it alongside assessment data in one place?"

### Win #4: AI leadership
> "Wayground ships AI features that save teachers hours. AI Create, AI Enhance, AI Analyze, AI grading. What AI features does Nearpod offer?"
Wayground's AI story is comprehensive and teacher-focused. Nearpod has effectively no AI features.
- *Landmine:* "How are your teachers using AI in their lesson creation? Does Nearpod support that?"

### Win #5: Gamification and engagement
> "Students beg for Wayground in May, not just September. Nearpod lessons are interactive but not engaging in the same way."
Nearpod is teacher-led instruction — students follow along. Wayground's gamification creates student-driven engagement with competitive modes, power-ups, and mastery paths.

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: Nearpod is entrenched**
- Don't unseat directly. Expand conversation to everything Nearpod CAN'T do.
- "Keep Nearpod for now. But for engagement, assessment, flashcards, and accommodations — let's run a comparison."
- Build teacher champions who experience the difference.

**Scenario B: Just bought Renaissance suite**
- Don't unseat full suite. Unbundle Nearpod from it.
- "Keep Star. Keep AR. Replace Nearpod." Run side-by-side pilot.
- Build teacher champions frustrated with Nearpod stagnation.

**Scenario C: Renaissance renewal approaching**
- TCO blitz: Renaissance suite cost vs. Wayground + Star (if they need diagnostics)
- Show innovation gap: what Wayground shipped this year vs. Nearpod
- Reference peer districts that switched

## THE ONE-LINER
> "Nearpod delivers lessons. Wayground delivers learning — with AI, gamification, assessment, and accommodations in one platform teachers actually love."
