# Battlecard: Kahoot!
*March 2026 • Confidential • Source: Carrie's Competitive Battlecard*

## Competitor Snapshot
- **Company:** Kahoot! ASA (public, NYSE: KHAT)
- **HQ:** Oslo, Norway (US office: Austin, TX)
- **Founded:** 2012
- **Parent:** Public company; Goldman Sachs and SoftBank are major shareholders
- **Products:** Kahoot! (live quiz games), Kahoot! EDU (school plans), Kahoot! 360 (enterprise), Actimo, Motimate, Drops
- **Audience:** K-12 + corporate (dual-market)
- **Users:** 9M+ teachers; hundreds of millions of participants lifetime
- **Pricing:** Free tier (basic); Kahoot! EDU Pro ~$3-6/teacher/month; school/district $4-10/student/year
- **Key Differentiator:** Brand recognition — Kahoot! IS the category. "Let's play a Kahoot" is a verb in classrooms
- **ESSA Evidence:** None
- **Accessibility:** Basic; no comprehensive IEP/504/MLL accommodation suite
- **AI Features:** Basic AI question generation; no AI tutoring, no student-facing AI

## Internal Assessment
Kahoot is our most common competitive mention. They created the live quiz game category. Their brand is nearly universal — teachers say "Let's do a Kahoot" the way people say "Google it." However, Kahoot has stagnated as a product, invested heavily in corporate, and failed to build a true K-12 learning platform. They have no assessment story, no accommodations, no lessons, no video. In budget conversations, Kahoot is expendable because it's "just games."

## How They Position
**Messaging pillars:**
- Brand as category: "Make learning awesome!" — positioning engagement as the primary outcome
- Universal recognition: Teachers and students know the name, the music, the countdown
- Ease of use: Simple to create, simple to play
- Corporate crossover: "Kahoot! for work" drives revenue; K-12 is growth market
- Content marketplace: Large library of community-created games

**What they don't talk about:**
- Assessment data (game results ≠ assessment data)
- Learning outcomes (no ESSA evidence, no impact claims)
- Lessons, video, or study tools (none)
- Accommodations (no IEP/504/MLL features)
- District admin features (limited reporting)
- AI depth (basic generation only)
- Product evolution (minimal feature additions in recent years)

## Feature Comparison (18 categories)
- **Wayground leads: 15** — Interactive lessons, interactive video, flashcards, async assignments, common assessments, standards alignment, real-time analytics, longitudinal data, IEP/504 accommodations, MLL support, AI tutoring, AI generation, district admin dashboard, ESSA evidence, content quality/curation
- **Even: 2** — Live game-based quizzes, content marketplace/community
- **Kahoot leads: 1** — Brand recognition

## Why We Win

### Win #1: Platform vs. game tool
> "Kahoot! is a game. Wayground is a platform."
Kahoot does one thing: live quiz games. Teachers still need Nearpod for lessons, Edpuzzle for video, flashcard apps, and a separate assessment tool. Wayground replaces Kahoot + all of those. In consolidation, one platform beats one game.
- *Landmine:* "Beyond Kahoot games, what tools do your teachers use for lessons, video, assessments, and study? What's that total cost?"

### Win #2: Assessment credibility
> "Kahoot is fun. But when your superintendent asks 'is it working?', what's the answer?"
Kahoot has zero assessment story. No standards alignment, no item-level data, no mastery tracking, no ESSA evidence. Wayground provides the same fun game experience PLUS real assessment data. Fun with data beats fun without data.
- *Landmine:* "Does your board require evidence that edtech investments improve outcomes? What evidence does Kahoot provide?"

### Win #3: Accommodations and equity
> "When a student with a 504 plan plays a Kahoot, where are the accommodations?"
Kahoot has no accommodation suite. No extended time, no text-to-speech, no immersive reader, no font adjustments. Wayground has 25+. For districts under IDEA compliance pressure, this is disqualifying.
- *Landmine:* "What percentage of your students have IEPs or 504 plans? How are their accommodations handled in Kahoot today?"

### Win #4: Budget consolidation
> "The easiest tool to cut is the one that only does games."
In a budget crisis, single-function tools get cut first. Kahoot does games. That's it. Wayground does games + lessons + video + flashcards + assessments + accommodations. When the superintendent asks what to cut, Kahoot is line item #1.
- *Landmine:* "If your budget got cut 20% tomorrow, which tools survive: the one that does one thing, or the one that replaces four?"

### Win #5: Product stagnation
> "Kahoot looks the same as it did three years ago. What have they shipped?"
Kahoot's product evolution has been minimal in K-12. They've invested in corporate (Kahoot! 360, Actimo, Motimate) while the education product sits. Wayground ships AI tutoring, accommodations, common assessments, interactive video.
- *Landmine:* "What new features has Kahoot shipped in the last two years that changed how your teachers teach?"

## Deal Strategy

**Scenario A: Kahoot is entrenched, teachers love it**
- Don't fight the love. Position as upgrade: "Same games, plus everything else."
- Target admin with data/evidence/accommodation gaps
- Side-by-side pilot: "If students don't find Wayground equally fun, we walk."

**Scenario B: District evaluating Kahoot vs. Wayground**
- Expand criteria: games + lessons + video + assessments + accommodations + evidence
- Demo full spectrum: game → lesson → video → assessment → accommodations → data
- "If you're buying a game, Kahoot is fine. If you're buying a platform, there's no comparison."

**Scenario C: Budget is tight, only one tool survives**
- TCO: Kahoot + Nearpod + Edpuzzle + assessment tool vs. Wayground alone
- "One platform that does games AND everything else costs less than four tools."
- Consolidation is the district's friend and Kahoot's enemy

## Board Meeting Intelligence
- **Zero board-level mentions:** Kahoot has ZERO mentions in our board meeting data across 787 accounts. It's a teacher-level purchase, not a district-level decision.
- **This is important:** When competing against Kahoot, know that it has no board/admin buy-in. The admin hasn't heard of it or sees it as a toy.

## THE ONE-LINER
> "Kahoot is a game. Wayground is a platform. In budget conversations, games get cut."
