# AE & BDR Discovery Call Rubrics — BTS 2026
*Source: Cody's PMM/Enablement folder • March 2026*
*MEDDPICC Framework rubrics for evaluating discovery calls.*

## AE DISCOVERY CALL SCORECARD

**Maximum score: 18 (6 criteria × 3 points each)**

### 1. Pain Identification

| Developing (1) | Proficient (2) | Advanced (3) |
|---|---|---|
| Asks only generic questions; accepts surface answers; doesn't connect pain to business impact | Uncovers 2-3 specific pain points; probes with follow-ups; connects to buyer's role and reality | Uncovers layered pain across personas; gets prospect to quantify impact; prospect articulates cost of inaction |

### 2. Question Quality & Depth

| Developing (1) | Proficient (2) | Advanced (3) |
|---|---|---|
| Unrelated questions, no flow; mostly closed-ended; no adaptation | Logical progression (situation → problem → impact → outcome); balances discovery with qualifying; adapts based on responses | Conversational flow; layered questions (symptoms → root causes); future-state visioning questions |

### 3. Active Listening & Follow-Through

| Developing (1) | Proficient (2) | Advanced (3) |
|---|---|---|
| Moves to next question without acknowledging; misses key details | Paraphrases answers; picks up important details; references earlier answers | Mirrors prospect's language; catches emotional cues; synthesizes multiple answers |

### 4. Rapport & Buyer Trust

| Developing (1) | Proficient (2) | Advanced (3) |
|---|---|---|
| One-directional; no genuine curiosity; prospect gives short answers | Two-way dialogue; volunteer sharing; shows genuine empathy; adapts to prospect's style | Prospect visibly engaged; asks questions back; treats rep as advisor, not vendor |

### 5. Messaging Clarity (Value Pillars)

| Developing (1) | Proficient (2) | Advanced (3) |
|---|---|---|
| Generic feature dump; no pillar reference; can't explain differentiators | Articulates positioning; connects to relevant pillar(s); differentiates from point solutions | Tailors using prospect's words; weaves in proof naturally; creates "tell me more" moment |

### 6. Next Step Commitment

| Developing (1) | Proficient (2) | Advanced (3) |
|---|---|---|
| No concrete next step; accepts vague follow-up | Secures specific next step with date/time; proposes right action for stage; sends calendar invite | Sells value of next meeting; gets commitment to bring stakeholders; creates urgency (budget cycle, pilot timing) |

### Scoring Guide
- **16-18:** Elite — Consistently uncovers deep pain, delivers value clearly, locks in strong next steps
- **12-15:** Advanced — Strong with 1-2 areas to sharpen
- **8-11:** Proficient — Covers basics but misses depth; doesn't consistently connect pain to value
- **6-7:** Developing — Significant gaps; calls feel scripted or one-directional

## AE SALES REP DISCOVERY CALL RUBRIC (MEDDPICC Framework)
**Maximum score: 42 (14 criteria × 3 points each)**

### SECTION A: CALL OPENING & RAPPORT

#### 1. Opening & Agenda Setting
- **Dev (1):** Jumps in without agenda; no rapport-building; doesn't confirm attendees
- **Prof (2):** Sets clear agenda; confirms attendees/roles; establishes basic rapport
- **Adv (3):** References pre-call research (district initiatives); ties agenda to context; confirms desired outcome from buyer perspective

#### 2. Buyer Persona Identification
- **Dev (1):** Doesn't ask who else is involved; treats all contacts the same
- **Prof (2):** Asks about role, responsibilities, reporting structure; explores who else should be involved
- **Adv (3):** Maps full buying committee (C&I, IT, Assessment, Principal); identifies Economic Buyer by name; asks about board approval, budget cycles, timelines

---

### SECTION B: DISCOVERY & QUALIFICATION (MEDDPICC)

#### 3. Identified Pain (M, I)
- **Dev (1):** Generic questions; surface-level answers; no impact connection
- **Prof (2):** Uncovers 2-3 specific pain points; probes with follow-ups; connects to buyer's role impact
- **Adv (3):** Layered pain across personas; quantifies using ICP signals; prospect articulates cost of inaction themselves

#### 4. Metrics & Quantification (M)
- **Dev (1):** Doesn't attempt to quantify; leaves pain qualitative
- **Prof (2):** Asks what success looks like; attempts to quantify at least one pain point; references Wayground proof points (ESSA Level 3, etc.)
- **Adv (3):** Establishes 2-3 measurable metrics tied to district priorities; connects to funding eligibility (Title I, ESSA); creates before/after ROI framework

#### 5. Business Condition Signals (I)
- **Dev (1):** Fails to identify adoption/evaluation cycle; doesn't explore current tech stack
- **Prof (2):** Identifies 2-3 ideal customer signals (curriculum adoption, compliance issue, tech sprawl); asks about current tools/satisfaction
- **Adv (3):** Matches conversation to specific business conditions; identifies cross-pillar opportunities; probes for triggering events (failed adoption, budget cycle, staff cuts)

---

### SECTION C: VALUE POSITIONING & MESSAGING

#### 6. Positioning Statement Delivery (D - Decision Criteria)
- **Dev (1):** Generic pitch; features instead of outcomes; no pillar mention
- **Prof (2):** Clear articulation of positioning; frames value through relevant pillar(s); differentiates from point solutions
- **Adv (3):** Tailored to buyer persona using practiced language; weaves in "complete loop" narrative; uses prospect's words/pain to frame value

#### 7. Wayground AI Positioning
- **Dev (1):** AI as feature list; doesn't address concerns; no "AI where it counts" messaging
- **Prof (2):** Positions AI as accelerating teachers, not replacing; covers Create/Enhance/Analyze/Act framework; addresses concerns proactively
- **Adv (3):** Connects directly to prospect's workflow pain; uses "AI where it counts" to differentiate; highlights BTS 2026 enhancements (AI Grading, CTE generators)

#### 8. BTS 2026 Feature Relevance (D - Decision Criteria)
- **Dev (1):** No mention of new capabilities; no connection to prospect needs
- **Prof (2):** Mentions 2-3 relevant BTS 2026 features; connects to expressed pain; creates urgency
- **Adv (3):** Strategically sequences features as solutions to uncovered pain; uses new admin reporting as ROI proof

---

### SECTION D: COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE & CHAMPION DEVELOPMENT

#### 9. Competition Mapping (C)
- **Dev (1):** Doesn't ask about alternatives; can't articulate advantages; unaware of "business as usual" as competitor
- **Prof (2):** Asks what solutions being evaluated; positions unique advantages (joy + accommodations + complete loop); addresses status quo risk
- **Adv (3):** Maps landscape including internal solutions; differentiates with precision against named competitors; plants landmine questions only Wayground answers well

#### 10. Champion Identification (C)
- **Dev (1):** Treats all contacts equally; no influence assessment; no internal selling support
- **Prof (2):** Identifies at least one influential person with personal motivation; tests willingness to advocate; provides supporting data
- **Adv (3):** Coaches champion on internal selling; arms with persona-specific talk tracks; establishes multi-threaded relationships

---

### SECTION E: DECISION PROCESS & NEXT STEPS

#### 11. Decision Process & Paper Process (P)
- **Dev (1):** Doesn't ask about timeline or approval process; no understanding of procurement
- **Prof (2):** Asks about timeline, approval, budget cycle; understands procurement mechanics (RFP, board approval); identifies blockers
- **Adv (3):** Maps full process with dates, stakeholders, milestones; proactively addresses paper process (contracts, compliance, pilots); co-creates mutual action plan

#### 12. Next Step Commitment
- **Dev (1):** No concrete next step; accepts vague "we'll be in touch"
- **Prof (2):** Secures specific next step with date/participants; proposes appropriate action; sends calendar invite
- **Adv (3):** Next step advances deal; gets commitment for additional stakeholders; positions $950 pilot/demo as low-risk path, tied to budget timing

---

### SCORING GUIDE
- **36-42:** Elite — Ready for complex enterprise deals; mentorship candidate
- **28-35:** Advanced — Strong performer; focus on 1-2 MEDDPICC gaps
- **21-27:** Proficient — Solid foundation; develop coaching around weakest areas
- **14-20:** Developing — Intensive coaching needed; pair with senior rep; focus on pain discovery + messaging

## BDR OUTBOUND RUBRIC (MEDDPICC for Cold/Warm Calls)
**Maximum score: 33 (11 criteria × 3 points each)**
**Goal:** Book qualified discovery meeting with AE

### SECTION A: OPENING & HOOK

#### 1. Pattern Interrupt & Opening
- **Dev (1):** Generic "How are you today?"; reads script; doesn't state name/company/reason quickly
- **Prof (2):** Clear, concise opening (name, Wayground, reason); permission-based ("okay time?"); relevant trigger reference
- **Adv (3):** Prospect-specific insight (district initiative, compliance change); creates curiosity with stat/question; adapts tone to prospect energy

#### 2. Pain Hypothesis (I)
- **Dev (1):** No hypothesis; generic questions; can't articulate relevance
- **Prof (2):** Clear hypothesis based on persona/ICP; uses messaging pain points (2,700+ tools, 7+ hrs time waste); adjusts by buyer segment
- **Adv (3):** Researched, district-specific hypothesis; references their curriculum/tech stack/initiatives; empathetic delivery; seamlessly transitions to validation

---

### SECTION B: EARLY QUALIFICATION

#### 3. Persona & Authority Check (E - Economic Buyer)
- **Dev (1):** Doesn't confirm title/role/authority; pitches identically; doesn't ask about stakeholders
- **Prof (2):** Confirms role/areas of responsibility; adapts language by persona; asks who else involved
- **Adv (3):** Quickly maps Champion vs. Influencer vs. Economic Buyer; tailors value prop to segment; plants seed for multi-threaded meeting

#### 4. ICP Signal Validation (I, M)
- **Dev (1):** No ICP validation; asks no qualifying questions
- **Prof (2):** Asks 2-3 ICP-aligned questions (curriculum, tech stack, assessments); listens for business condition triggers
- **Adv (3):** Efficiently qualifies on multiple MEDDPICC dimensions; identifies specific metrics prospect cares about; recognizes disqualification signals

---

### SECTION C: VALUE MESSAGING & DIFFERENTIATION

#### 5. Elevator Pitch Delivery (D - Decision Criteria)
- **Dev (1):** Long, feature-heavy monologue; no loop or engagement mention; can't explain in 30 seconds
- **Prof (2):** Concise pitch aligned to messaging; hits key differentiators (complete loop, joy, accommodations, ESSA); tailors by persona
- **Adv (3):** Conversational, adapts in real time; creates compelling sound bite; earns right to continue with "tell me more" moment

#### 6. Competitive Differentiation (C)
- **Dev (1):** No awareness of current tools; can't explain differences; badmouths competitors
- **Prof (2):** Asks what tools they use; positions as consolidator without disparaging; mentions one unique differentiator
- **Adv (3):** Identifies competitive landscape; positions precisely; uses "Swiss Army knife" or consolidation ROI narrative; for CTE: formative-first vs. summative-only advantage

#### 7. Social Proof & Credibility (M)
- **Dev (1):** No social proof; unsupported claims; no customer scale mentioned
- **Prof (2):** References "90% of U.S. schools" + ESSA Level 3; mentions customer name/quote when appropriate; cites specific stat
- **Adv (3):** Uses customer story mirroring prospect's situation (same state, district size, pain); connects proof to metric prospect cares about; drops proof naturally

---

### SECTION D: OBJECTION HANDLING & MEETING COMMITMENT

#### 8. Objection Handling
- **Dev (1):** Freezes, argues, or concedes immediately; can't handle common objections; takes "send me info" at face value
- **Prof (2):** Structured response (acknowledge, isolate, respond, confirm); handles top 3 objections (budget, timing, existing tools) confidently
- **Adv (3):** Reframes as opportunity; uses objections to deepen qualification; has persona-specific talk tracks (IT: integrations, C&I: alignment, Assessment: rigor)

#### 9. Meeting Ask & Commitment (P - Paper Process)
- **Dev (1):** Doesn't ask for meeting or asks too late; accepts "maybe"; doesn't specify details
- **Prof (2):** Clear, specific ask within 3-4 minutes; proposes specific time/duration; suggests appropriate attendees
- **Adv (3):** Sells value of meeting itself, not just product; creates urgency (BTS planning, budget cycle, pilot windows); confirms with invite/agenda

---

### SCORING GUIDE
- **28-33:** Elite — Top performer; ready for increased quota, mentoring, AE shadowing
- **22-27:** Advanced — Strong BDR; refine 1-2 areas (objection handling or qualification depth)
- **16-21:** Proficient — Solid foundation; invest in role-play on opening + elevator pitch
- **11-15:** Developing — Intensive coaching; daily call review, scripted talk tracks, ride-alongs

## Key MEDDPICC Reminder

| **M** | METRICS | Quantifiable justification; economic impact |
| **E** | ECONOMIC BUYER | Who approves and moves budget |
| **D** | DECISION CRITERIA | Technical and business requirements |
| **D** | DECISION PROCESS | Stakeholders, influencers, approvers |
| **P** | PAPER PROCESS | Documentation needed; procurement timelines |
| **I** | IDENTIFIED PAIN | What's driving change? How acute? |
| **C** | CHAMPION | Power, influence, something to gain |
| **C** | COMPETITION | Who/what are you competing against? |
