# Funding Guide 2025
*Source: Cody's PMM/Enablement folder • 2025*

## Federal Funding Sources Aligned with Wayground

### Title I — Part A: Improving Basic Programs
**Wayground alignment:** Turn daily instruction into daily wins

**Best for:**
- States racing to meet new literacy and math requirements
- Districts turning literacy initiatives into actual gains

**Key capabilities:**
- Reading comprehension with questions designed for deep understanding
- Math thinking through drawing and modeling
- Every student gets a voice with text-to-speech and audio responses

---

### Title III — Part A: Supporting Effective Instruction
**Wayground alignment:** Let multilingual students show what they know

**Best for:**
- Districts where "English Learner support" is a high priority
- Schools ready to close gap between potential and performance

**Key capabilities:**
- 18+ languages, extended time, audio responses
- WIDA-aligned vocabulary building across any subject
- Grade-level content stays grade-level — adjust support, not standards

---

### Title IV — Part A: Student Support & Academic Enrichment Grants
**Wayground alignment:** Test prep that doesn't feel like punishment

**Best for:**
- Districts embracing digital testing
- Schools where "test prep" shouldn't mean "stop teaching"

**Key capabilities:**
- 20+ question formats matching test-day experience (goodbye format shock)
- Mastery Mode turns review into muscle memory without drill-and-kill
- Focus Mode strips distractions, keeps eyes on own work

---

### Education Innovation and Research (EIR)
**Wayground alignment:** Improve academic achievement for all students

**Evidence-based principles:**
- Gamification (engaged students learn better)
- Spaced practice (cramming never worked)
- Adaptive learning (one size fits one)

**Inclusion features:**
- Works on whatever device available
- Reads aloud for students who need it
- Actually accessible (not just technically compliant)

---

### CRRSA EANS / ARP EANS (Private Schools)
**ARP EANS eligibility:**
- Only for non-public schools with ≥40% low-income students (most states)
- Only for NEW services and assistance (no reimbursement of past spending)
- Otherwise same allowable uses as CRRSA EANS

---

## ESSA Evidence
**Wayground holds ESSA Level 3 evidence (2023)** — strengthens justification for federal funds requiring evidence-based interventions.

## Strategic Notes for Sales
- Lead with the funding source that matches the prospect's biggest pain
- Title I: literacy and math achievement
- Title III: multilingual/ELL support
- Title IV: digital assessment readiness
- EIR: innovation and evidence-based design
- Always reference ESSA Level 3 when discussing federal funding eligibility
