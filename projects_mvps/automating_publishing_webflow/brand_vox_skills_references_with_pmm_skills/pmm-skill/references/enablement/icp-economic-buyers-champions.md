# ICP — Understanding Economic Buyers & Champions
*Source: Cody's PMM/Enablement folder • March 2026*

## Our Best District Customers Are...
- Often find us through secondary STEM teachers, then expand district-wide when they see AI supports broader contexts
- Eager to consolidate engagement tools (Kahoot, Edpuzzle) and learning platforms (Pear Deck, Nearpod) into streamlined experience
- Excited about AI and Wayground's pace of iteration

## District vs. School Decision Scope

### District Decisions
- Core instructional materials
- Scope and sequence
- Supplemental tools for instruction
- Assessment systems
- LMS & SIS
- Special Education and MLL program budgets
- Compliance-driven services
- Cybersecurity and data privacy
- Devices and infrastructure
- Special state initiatives

### School Decisions
- Small subscriptions below ~$10K threshold
- Classroom libraries and miscellaneous materials
- Teacher-initiated tools
- Building-based PD

## Economic Buyer vs. Champion

### Economic Buyer = Power to Approve
- Controls the money
- Has budget authority to sign contracts
- Must answer to board on: budget, risk, ROI, compliance, sustainability

### Champion = Power to Influence
- Creates momentum
- Focused on: solving measurable pain, implementation success, teacher adoption/satisfaction, standards/HQIM alignment

## Key Personas

### Instructional Coach (Key Champion)
**"I know what teachers need, but I can't get to them"**

**Pressures:**
- Teacher resistance and change fatigue
- Expected to cover instruction + daily operations gaps
- Multiple competing district initiatives
- Lacks direct managerial authority
- Still responsible for learning outcomes

**How we help:** Reduce their burden by making Wayground easy to champion; arm them with data that proves impact

---

### Superintendent (Financial Approver)
**Tenure:** CGCS (largest districts): 2.7 years; Smaller ICP districts: ~5 years

**Pressures:**
- Show academic results quickly
- Managing budget constraints (declining enrollments, structural deficits post-ESSER)
- Parent and community communication
- Board politics and governance

**How we help:** Make them look good in front of the school board — fast. Quick wins, visible data, evidence-backed ROI.

---

### Chief Academic Officer / Asst. Superintendent (Academic Buyer)
**Owns:** Instructional budget, PD & Coaching (Title II), Assessment

**Pressures:**
- Fidelity of program adoption
- Teacher buy-in
- Pacing consistency
- Evidence of learning on high-stakes tests and benchmarks
- Board meeting presentations
- Showing progress of subgroups

**Key insight:** *They own the outcomes but don't control the conditions.*

## Discovery Questions for ICP Qualification
1. What budget does this typically come from — curriculum, tech, federal funds, Title, grants, building allocations?
2. When you evaluate instructional tools, what criteria do you weigh most heavily?
3. What must any solution do in order to move forward for your teachers or students?
4. Which committees or IT reviews need to weigh in?
5. Who will evaluate ROI for a purchase like this, and how?
6. Who handles contracts, procurement, and data privacy reviews?
7. What typically slows down contracting?
8. How does your district define success for this initiative — and how is it measured today?
9. Who inside the district is most passionate about solving this problem?
10. Who has seen early success or momentum?
11. How can we help you make the case internally?
