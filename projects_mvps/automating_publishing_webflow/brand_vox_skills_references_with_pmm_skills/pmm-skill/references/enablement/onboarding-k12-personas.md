*Source: Cody's PMM/Enablement folder • March 3, 2026*

## Part 1: A Day in the Life of US Educators

### Teacher Reality
**"Wearing Lots of Hats"**
- Lesson planning (often unpaid, after-hours)
- Classroom instruction (25-30+ students)
- Grading & feedback
- Documentation (data reporting, evaluation reporting)
- Behavior management
- Parent communication
- Committee participation
- Coaching & extracurriculars
- Continuing education for credentials

### Administrator Reality
- Teacher evaluations & classroom observations
- Leading initiatives
- Running data meetings & professional development
- Driving tool adoption
- Pushing district and state initiatives forward
- State testing coordination
- Behavior management escalation

### Tech Fatigue & Tool Overload
- Too many platforms, logins, dashboards
- Steep learning curves
- Admin-mandated tools vs. teacher-preferred tools
- Best tools = low lift, effective, teacher-driven
- Tools blocked by districts

### Assessment Pressures
- **State Testing:** High stakes, very time-consuming
- **Benchmark & Formative:** Can delay lessons
- **Teachers feel stuck between need to teach and demand to assess**

### Where Wayground Fits In
1. **Real-time feedback & auto-grading** — *Major pain point solver!*
2. **Exports data easily for meetings**
3. **Bring in existing content to supplement** — Build additional content faster
4. **Saves time during planning** — *Major pain point solver!*
5. **Quality focus:** Standards-aligned, collaborative, citable sources, not just filler content

## Part 2: K-12 Buying System

### The 4 Decision Lenses
Every district evaluates products through 4 lenses:

| Lens | Key Question |
|------|-------------|
| **Instruction** | Will teachers actually use this? |
| **Leadership** | Will it actually improve outcomes? |
| **Data** | Can we measure and justify it? |
| **IT** | Is it safe, compliant, worth it? |

**If the answer is NO at any point, the deal falls apart.**

Wayground touches all 4 → we can replace tools AND trigger more stakeholders to champion us.

## Part 3: Wayground Personas

### The Reality of K-12 Selling
It seems like it should be: Find the principal → Get a Director → Talk to IT

It actually is:
- Person who **loves** your product
- Person who **pays** for the product
- Person who **blocks** your product
- **And they seem to never be the same person!**

---

### Persona 1: Classroom Teacher — "The Boots on the Ground"
- Don't sign the contract, but can start a deal
- Turn them into internal champions who introduce you to admins
- **Make their job survivable and students successful!**

**What they care about:** Saving time, engaging students, easier assessments, fewer tools
**What they control:** Adoption (can catch like wildfire); makes or breaks pilots
**Sound like:** "I love this but I don't know if the district will pay for it" / "We're using Nearpod, Edpuzzle, Google Forms, and it's a mess!"

---

### Persona 2: Instructional Coach — "The Influencer"
- Drives huge percentage of district decisions
- Acts as confidant for admin on best practices
- **If a coach is excited about us, we're a recommended solution, not a random vendor!**

**What they care about:** Consistency, instructional quality, PD/coaching, usable data
**What they control:** Tool recommendations, pilot selections, admin narrative (good and bad)
**Sound like:** "We need something teachers will actually use" / "We can't keep supporting 7 different tools"

---

### Persona 3: School/District Admin — "The Outcome Owner"
*(Principal, AP, Director of T&L, Curriculum Director)*
- Not buying features — buying visibility, consistency, and results

**What they care about:** Student growth, teacher accountability, data for decisions, standards alignment
**What they control:** Budgets, pilots, final yes/no
**Sound like:** "How do I know teachers are actually doing this?" / "We need common assessments" / "We can't see what's working across all schools"

---

### Persona 4: Assessment Manager / Data Leader — "The Evidence Gatekeeper"
- Position Wayground as single source of truth for instructional data

**What they care about:** Common assessments, data accuracy, growth over time, data by standards/subgroups
**What they control:** Whether data is "valid enough" to use; whether Wayground is taken seriously as a system
**Sound like:** "We don't trust our current data" / "Teacher's data lives in too many places" / "We can't see progress by standard"

---

### Persona 5: IT/EdTech Director — "The Risk Manager"
- Position us as replacing tools, not adding more
- We're secure, integrated, and used

**What they care about:** Security, integrations, SSO, tool sprawl, cost vs. usage
**What they control:** Procurement, security approval
**Sound like:** "We already have too many tools" / "Does this integrate with our LMS?" / "What data are you collecting?"
