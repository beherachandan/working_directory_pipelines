# SKO Objection Round Robin
*Source: Cody's PMM/Enablement folder • March 2026*

## 4-Step Objection Handling Framework
1. **Listen & Validate** — Hear them out and acknowledge the concern
2. **Clarify & Isolate** — Identify the true objection vs. surface-level problem
3. **Reframe w/Value** — Connect our solution to their goal/pain point
4. **Resolve & Advance** — Use proof, confirm concern addressed → next steps

> If there are ZERO objections, odds are they're not paying attention.

## 5 Common Objections & Scripted Responses

### 1. "No Budget"
> "Totally understand — budget constraints are everywhere right now. Many of our partners initially felt the same, but they found ways to use Title funds, ESSER, or PD budgets once they saw how Wayground aligned with their instructional goals. Would it make sense to explore those options together for future planning?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Even if budget is tight now, can we do a quick overview for when funds do open up?"
- "Other than price, do you have any other questions or concerns?" *(isolate the real objection)*

### 2. "No Time to Meet"
> "Totally fair — we know how busy educators are. That's why Wayground may resonate. It saves teachers time through instant feedback, accommodations, ready-made resources, and Wayground AI."

**Follow-up:**
- "Can I send you a quick 2-minute video overview? Watch on your own time, then we touch base when it works for you."

### 3. "That's a District Decision"
> "Thank you for sharing. We work with a lot of schools that play a major role in informing the district's decisions, especially when tools show strong classroom impact. Would it be helpful if I shared how other schools have influenced district rollouts?"

**Follow-up:**
- "Even if the decision comes from above, it can help to know what's working at classroom level. Can I share some examples you might want to elevate to district leaders?"

### 4. "Usage Isn't High Enough"
> "That's actually something we can help with! When usage is low, it can often be because there hasn't been enough visibility or training, not necessarily because educators don't find value. We've helped other schools turn this around."

**Follow-up:**
- "Sometimes a 15-minute walkthrough is all it takes for teachers to see how easy and impactful Wayground can be."

### 5. "Programs Already Chosen for Next Year"
> "That makes sense — it's great that you're planned out. What some schools find helpful is looking at how Wayground complements existing tools, especially around assessment, engagement, or Tier 1/2 instruction."

**Follow-up:**
- "Could I share a few ideas you can keep in your back pocket for mid-year review or next year planning?"

## Differentiating Wayground
- **Works with what teachers already have** — PDFs, slide decks, videos; we build upon their materials
- **Built-in accommodations** — one of our most valuable features
- **Real-time data** — instant feedback loop for instruction decisions

## Strategic Guidance
- Don't just list features → translate into benefits for the individual, school, and students
- Develop talk tracks for each benefit
- Keep it conversational (2-way dialogue)
- Zero objections = prospect isn't engaged
