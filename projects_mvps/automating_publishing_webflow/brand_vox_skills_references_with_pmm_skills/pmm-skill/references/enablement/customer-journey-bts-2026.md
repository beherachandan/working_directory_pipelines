## THE ENTERPRISE CUSTOMER JOURNEY (Circular Model)

### Stage Flow
1. **PQA – 5 Teachers** (Product Qualified Account — bottoms-up growth)
2. **First School Sale**
3. **Additional School Sales**
4. **Pre-Qualifying**
5. **MEDDPICC Qualification**
6. **Pilot (Paid) – 6 weeks**
7. **Evaluation**
8. **Purchase**
9. **Onboarding**
10. **Implementation**
11. **Expansion**
*(cycles back to PQA)*

---

## STAGE 1: BOTTOMS-UP GROWTH
**Sub-stages:** PQA – 5 Teachers → First School Sale → Additional School Sales

Building champions through organic use and early partnership investment.

**Key:** Monitor organic growth to trigger district engagement. Early signals (multiple schools paying, game changers identified) = open door to district sale (not required for sale).

---

## STAGE 2: DISCOVERY & QUALIFICATION (MEDDPICC)
**Sub-stages:** Pre-Qualifying → MEDDPICC Qualification

Engage district customer to open opportunity and conduct "Meaningful Discovery."

**Meaningful questions** → Move through MEDDPICC → Understand district needs and where Wayground fits.

---

## PAID PILOT (6 weeks, $950)

### What It Is
- Paid ($950, credited back on purchase)
- Narrow audience with specific goals
- Highly structured and supported
- 6-week duration

### What It's NOT
- Endless in time
- Opportunity to give away Wayground
- Open-ended

### When We Need a Pilot
**Most of the time** — strengthens the case for purchase, once goals and a small subset are identified.

### When We MIGHT NOT Need a Pilot
- High levels of school use with strong activation
- Data story highlighting measurable success against key metrics

### Pilot Decision Framework
```
Do they have free users?
├─ NO → Pilot (6 weeks)
└─ YES: Do they have paid teachers?
   ├─ NO → Pilot (6 weeks)
   └─ YES: Do they have a school or more purchased?
      ├─ NO → Pilot (6 weeks)
      └─ YES: Does implementation meet district goals?
         ├─ NO → Pilot (6 weeks)
         └─ YES → Share data points + testimonial → Pricing & Sale
```

### Pilot Process (6-week structure)
1. Identify measurable goals; align EB, Champions, stakeholders
2. Kickoff and tailored PD
3. Weekly check-ins sharing data
4. AE + CSM work together for continuity
5. → **Pricing & Sale**

---

## STAGE 3: "PROVE IT"
**Sub-stages:** Evaluation → Purchase

**Pilot = Proof** in evaluation process.

**Providing the WHY:** M (Metrics), E (Economic Buyer), D (Decision), C (Champion)

**Data required:** From Stage 1 or pilot to prove Wayground is the right choice.

Onboarding begins: identify users, POC, goals.

---

## STAGE 4: CUSTOMER SUCCESS
**Sub-stages:** Onboarding → Implementation → Expansion

### Warm Handshake (Sales → CS)
Continue conversation; build on goals. All team members nurture relationship.

### Set Clear Expectations
- Timeframe
- Usage targets
- Who's responsible?
- What will teachers be doing?
- How many teachers used since PD?

**This info helps Sales for neighboring district expansion.**

---

## INTERNAL ROLES & PODS

### BDR — The EARS
Sets stage for discovery. Reaches out, identifies specific user roles. **Listen for:** new initiatives, district problems.

### AE — The EARS & EYES
Researches district, collaborates with CSMs/EMs. Solutions tailored to needs. Sets stage for demo targeting district solutions, leads pilot.

### EM — The BRAIN
Identifies discovery questions, talking points, researches district. Showcases Wayground with intention. Collaborates internally, shares product updates and value.

### CSM — The MUSCLE
Works with each district, implements goals, identifies new needs, unlocks Wayground magic. Deep knowledge = warm referrals, first-hand insights, state-level awareness.

## MEDDPICC FRAMEWORK

| Letter | Category | Definition |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **M** | METRICS | Quantifiable justification; economic impact |
| **E** | ECONOMIC BUYER | Who approves and moves budget |
| **D** | DECISION CRITERIA | Technical and business requirements |
| **D** | DECISION PROCESS | Stakeholders, influencers, approvers |
| **P** | PAPER PROCESS | Documentation needed; procurement timelines |
| **I** | IDENTIFIED PAIN | What's driving change? How acute? |
| **C** | CHAMPION | Power, influence, vested interest |
| **C** | COMPETITION | Who/what are you competing against? (Often "business as usual") |

## DISTRICT SALES ARE DIFFERENT

**Key differences from transactional sales:**
- Ongoing support and data check-ins
- Differentiate messaging by role
- Follow up, check-in, provide artifacts
- Share example districts/users
- Establish shared meaning of success
- Identify champions on both sides

---

## DISTRICT BUYING CONTEXT VARIABLES

- Strategic leadership support
- Vision for teaching & learning
- Staff culture
- Teacher beliefs & knowledge
- Teacher agency
- Infrastructure & operations
- Implementation systems & processes
- Professional learning
- Competing priorities
- Selection processes

## DISTRICT SALES PROCESS FLOW

## POST-SALE HANDSHAKE (Sales ↔ CS)

### Before Sale
- CS and Sales collaborate in pod

### After Sale
- CS and AE meet internally
- CS kickoff (AE included for warm handoff)
- CS onboards + PD
- Intentional user activation
- Quarterly meeting with primary stakeholder

**→ Renewal & Expansion via MEDDPICC**

### The Handshake Principle
Not a true handoff — **both teams have vested interest in district success.**

Periodically invite Sales to meetings → maintains relationship, provides new insights.

**Everyone is an account owner.**

## ONGOING SUPPORT & DATA CHECK-INS

Develop quarterly meetings with stakeholders to:
- Set goals
- Review what's been accomplished
- Smaller building-level meetings to show measurable VALUE

## KEY SUCCESS PRINCIPLES

### Differentiate Based on Role
District leaders, principals, teachers ≠ same.

Just like classrooms, each role needs different information to succeed.

*What admin needs ≠ what teacher needs*

### Presence & Confidence
Districts want a **partnership**, not a vendor transaction.

Working WITH the district, learning about their needs = **KEY**.

**Relationships matter.**

### Follow Up, Check-In, Provide Artifacts
- Demo good
- Training good
- **But districts need more than one-time presentations**

Keep Wayground top of mind with follow-ups, check-ins, materials.

### Example Districts or Users
Share successes from your community. Principals/admin want to see "we're not alone."

Opportunity for real-world examples.

### Shared Meaning of Success
At all stages, identify and call out success metrics.

Set timeframes, come back to them often.

**Everyone loves a win (big or small).**

### Champions on Both Sides
Identify early.

Champions = vested interest + will "sing praises" in critical meetings.
