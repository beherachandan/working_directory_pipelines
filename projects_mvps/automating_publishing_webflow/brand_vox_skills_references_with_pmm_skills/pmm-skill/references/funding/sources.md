## Federal Funding Sources (Detail)

### Title I, Part A (ESSA) — Improving Basic Programs
- **Annual funding:** ~$18B nationally
- **Who qualifies:** Schools with ≥40% low-income students (~60% of US public schools)
- **What it funds:** Instructional technology, supplemental programs, and interventions that improve achievement for disadvantaged students
- **Eligible products:** Core Wayground, CA, Subject Libraries
- **Justification language:** "Standards-aligned formative assessment and differentiated practice platform that supports data-driven instruction for underperforming subgroups"
- **Key requirement:** Must demonstrate connection to school improvement plan
- **Buying signal:** "We're a Title I school" — they have dedicated money AND higher accountability
- **Budget cycle:** Annual; typically allocated at beginning of school year (Aug-Sep)

### Title II, Part A — Supporting Effective Instruction
- **Annual funding:** ~$2.2B nationally
- **What it funds:** Professional development and teacher training — NOT software licenses
- **Eligible products:** PD services only ($1,500–$75,000)
- **Justification language:** "Professional development on formative assessment best practices and data-driven instruction using Wayground platform"
- **Deal structuring tip:** Bundle PD services funded by Title II with software funded by another source — splits the cost across two budget lines
- **Key requirement:** PD must improve teacher effectiveness in core academic subjects

### Title III — Language Instruction for English Learners
- **Annual funding:** ~$800M nationally
- **Who qualifies:** Districts with English Learner (EL/ML) populations
- **Eligible products:** Core Wayground (strongest fit — multilingual accommodations)
- **Justification language:** "Platform with read-aloud in 90+ languages, text-to-speech, extended time, and language scaffolding that directly serves EL student achievement goals"
- **Key requirement:** Must serve EL students specifically
- **Deal structuring tip:** If district has significant EL population, use Title III for the accommodations/access portion while funding core platform from another source

### Title IV, Part A — Student Support and Academic Enrichment (SSAE)
- **Annual funding:** ~$1.2B nationally
- **Three buckets:** (1) Well-rounded education, (2) Safe/healthy students, (3) Effective use of technology
- **Eligible products:** Core Wayground, CA, Subject Libraries, PD — bucket 3 specifically covers ed-tech
- **Justification language:** "Instructional technology platform that supports effective use of technology for improved academic outcomes across all subjects"
- **Key advantage:** This is often the EASIEST federal path to an ed-tech purchase — bucket 3 was specifically created for technology adoption
- **Deal structuring tip:** Title IV-A is the catch-all. When other funding sources don't quite fit, check if the district has unspent Title IV-A

### IDEA, Part B — Individuals with Disabilities Education Act
- **Annual funding:** ~$14B nationally
- **⚠️ NOT A VIABLE FUNDING PATH FOR WAYGROUND** (Carrie, Mar 2026)
- **Why not:**
  1. IDEA can only be used AFTER a SPED department has exhausted all other funding options (funding of last resort)
  2. Purchases must be tied to specific students on a special education caseload — not general classroom use
  3. When an indirect purchase benefits students outside SPED caseload, IDEA only covers the percentage attributed to caseload students (e.g., 15% SPED population in a 25K-student district buying at $7/student → IDEA covers only ~$26K of a $175K deal)
  4. Wayground covers digital/assessment-based accommodations, but many IEP/504 accommodations are physical or environmental (preferential seating, separate testing locations, physical writing supports) — Wayground is not comprehensive enough to be the primary tool IDEA is designed to support
  5. IDEA only covers IEP plans — there is NO dedicated funding source for 504 plans (which is why 504 implementation failure rates are so high)
- **If IDEA comes up in a district conversation:** Do NOT position it as a funding pathway. Acknowledge the accommodation value of Wayground but redirect to other funding sources (Title I, Title IV-A, general fund) that are better fits for the full deployment
- **What we CAN say:** "Wayground's 25+ accommodations directly serve IEP and 504 compliance requirements — but the funding path for Wayground is typically Title I, Title IV-A, or general fund, not IDEA. The accommodation features are a value argument, not a funding argument."

### Perkins V — Carl D. Perkins CTE Act
- **Annual funding:** ~$1.4B nationally
- **Who qualifies:** Schools with CTE programs (85% of HS students take at least 1 CTE course)
- **Eligible products:** Core Wayground, CTE Subject Library, PD
- **Justification language:** "Assessment and practice platform for CTE certification prep, industry vocabulary, safety training, and technical skill development"
- **Key advantage:** CTE is UNDERSERVED by ed-tech — fewer competitors, dedicated federal funding, growing demand
- **Deal structuring tip:** Perkins V can fund the CTE library + core platform for CTE programs, while other sources fund the broader school deployment
- **Board meeting signal:** CTE and Perkins frequently appear in TX district budgets

### ESSER / ARP — COVID Relief ⚠️ LARGELY EXPIRED
- **Total funding:** $190B across three rounds (2020–2024)
- **Status:** ESSER III funds had to be obligated by Sep 2024. Liquidation extensions may carry into 2025-2026
- **Deal structuring tip:** Ask about unobligated carryover. Some districts may have remaining funds during liquidation period
- **Note:** Do not lead with ESSER — but if a district mentions it, explore remaining balances

### CSI/TSI School Improvement Funds
- **What:** Additional federal/state funding for schools identified as Comprehensive Support (CSI) or Targeted Support (TSI) under ESSA
- **Eligible products:** Core Wayground, CA, Subject Libraries, PD — ALL products
- **Key advantage:** CSI/TSI schools have BOTH extra money AND extra accountability pressure — one of the strongest buying signals
- **Justification language:** "Comprehensive assessment and intervention platform with real-time data to accelerate improvement across priority subgroups"

## State Funding Sources (Detail)

### State Instructional Materials & Technology Allotments
- **Examples:** Texas IMTA, California LCFF supplemental/concentration grants, Florida FEFP categorical
- **Eligible products:** Core Wayground, CA, Subject Libraries
- **Key:** These vary significantly by state. Always ask: "What funding source would you use for instructional technology?"
- **Texas IMTA:** Frequently used for ed-tech in TX districts (confirmed from board meeting data across 787 districts)
- **Deal structuring tip:** State allotments are often the most flexible and least competitive funding sources — districts have more discretion

### State Assessment / Accountability Funds
- **Eligible products:** Common Assessment (strongest), Core Wayground (formative assessment)
- **Justification:** "Assessment platform with lockdown browser, state-aligned test experience, and standards-aligned reporting"

### State CTE Matching Funds
- Many states match federal Perkins V dollars or have separate CTE grant programs
- **Eligible products:** CTE Subject Library, Core Wayground for CTE courses
- **Deal structuring tip:** Stack state CTE + federal Perkins V for maximum CTE coverage

### State-Specific Technology Grants
- Texas: TEA technology-focused grants
- New York: Smart Schools Bond Act
- California: K-12 High-Speed Network initiatives
- **Note:** Highly variable. Research specific state programs for target districts

## Local Funding Sources (Detail)

### General Fund / Operating Budget
- **What:** Largest single source (~45% of K-12 revenue), primarily from property taxes
- **Eligible products:** ALL — Core Wayground, CA, Libraries, PD
- **Decision path:** <$10K = principal/curriculum director; $10K-$50K = superintendent; $50K+ = school board vote
- **Deal structuring tip:** General fund is the fallback when specific grants don't apply. Most reliable but also most competitive (everyone wants general fund dollars)

### Technology Budget (IT Line Item)
- **Eligible products:** Core Wayground, CA
- **Key stakeholder:** IT Director (gatekeeper)
- **Selling angle:** Tool consolidation — "Replace 5 point solutions with one platform" (avg school uses 1,400+ tools)

### Curriculum / Instructional Materials Budget
- **Eligible products:** Subject Libraries (most natural), Core Wayground
- **Key stakeholder:** Curriculum Director
- **Deal structuring tip:** Subject Libraries fit most naturally here — frame as digital instructional materials

### Bond Measures / Capital Funds
- **Eligible products:** Multi-year district-wide deployments
- **Deal structuring tip:** If a district recently passed a technology bond, there's earmarked money. Ask about recent ballot measures

### PTA/PTO and Education Foundation Grants
- **Eligible products:** Single-school Core Wayground
- **Scale:** Typically smaller ($1,000-$5,000), fits school-level purchases at $1,850 minimum

## Creative Deal Structuring Patterns

### Pattern 1: "Split the Check" — Multi-Source Funding
Split a deal across multiple funding sources to reduce impact on any single budget:
- Core Wayground → Title I or general fund
- CA add-on → State assessment funds
- Subject Libraries → Curriculum budget
- PD services → Title II-A
- **When to use:** Large district deals where no single budget can absorb the full cost

### Pattern 2: "Land and Expand" — Pilot to District
- Start with $950 6-week pilot (any budget can absorb this)
- Build usage data and teacher champions
- Use pilot data to justify full school purchase ($1,850-$9,999)
- Expand to district-wide with volume discounts
- **When to use:** "No budget" objection, new relationships, risk-averse buyers

### Pattern 3: "Compliance Value Sell" — Accommodations as Value Argument (NOT Funding Argument)
- Wayground's 25+ accommodations serve IEP/504 compliance — but this is a VALUE argument, not a funding path
- IDEA funds are NOT viable for Wayground (too restricted, last-resort funding, covers only SPED caseload percentage)
- Instead: Use accommodation value to justify Title I or general fund purchase — "One platform that handles assessment, engagement, AND legally required accommodations"
- **When to use:** Districts with IEP/504 compliance pressure, but fund through Title I, Title IV-A, or general fund
- **Key talking point:** "504 plans have no dedicated funding source at all — yet schools are legally required to provide accommodations. Wayground handles this within the platform at no additional cost to the SPED department."

### Pattern 4: "CTE Wedge" — Perkins V Entry
- Use Perkins V to fund CTE Subject Library + Core for CTE programs
- Demonstrate value in CTE → expand to math/ELA/science with other funding
- **When to use:** High schools with strong CTE programs, underserved market

### Pattern 5: "Assessment Play" — CA as the Anchor
- Lead with Common Assessment as the anchor purchase (state assessment funds)
- Add Core Wayground for formative assessment (Title I or general fund)
- Bundle for 10% discount
- **When to use:** Districts focused on accountability, CSI/TSI schools, testing season

### Pattern 6: "Max Stack" — Maximize Discounts for Large Districts
- All-In bundle (15% off): Core + CA + 1 subject library
- Volume discount at their tier (up to 47% off at 100K+)
- 3-year term (additional 10% off)
- Research partner discount (additional 5%)
- Sales discretionary (up to 5%)
- **Floor:** $3.00/student minimum regardless of stacking
- **When to use:** Very large districts (20K+ students), strategic accounts

### Pattern 7: "CSI Urgency" — Improvement Funds + Short Timeline
- CSI/TSI schools have dedicated improvement funds AND accountability pressure
- Offer immediate deployment with included onboarding ($0)
- Add virtual workshop ($1,500 from Title II) for quick teacher activation
- **When to use:** CSI/TSI-designated schools in improvement cycles

### Pattern 8: "EL Compliance" — Title III + Core
- Use Title III for the multilingual/accommodation capabilities
- Layer Core Wayground on general fund for full school
- Emphasize: 90+ language read-aloud, extended time, scaffolding
- **When to use:** Districts with significant EL populations (>15%)

## Deal Sizing Quick Calculator

For a given student count and product mix:
1. Look up volume tier for student count
2. Apply bundle discount if WG+CA (10%) or All-In (15%)
3. Apply duration discount if multi-year (5% for 2yr, 10% for 3yr)
4. Apply additional discounts (sales discretionary, research partner)
5. Check against $3.00/student floor
6. Check against school min ($1,850) and max ($9,999) if single school
7. Multiply final per-student price × student count = annual contract value

### Example: 12,000-student district, All-In bundle, 3-year term
- Volume tier: 10,000–19,999
- All-In rate: $8.64/student
- 3-year discount: $8.64 × 0.90 = $7.78/student
- Annual value: 12,000 × $7.78 = $93,360/year
- Total contract: $93,360 × 3 = $280,080
- Funding suggestion: Title I (core) + state assessment (CA) + curriculum budget (library) + Title II (PD)
