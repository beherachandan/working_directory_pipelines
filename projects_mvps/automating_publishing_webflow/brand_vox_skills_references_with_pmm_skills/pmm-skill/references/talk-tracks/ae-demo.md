# Talk Track: AE Demo

## When to Use
Product demonstration after discovery. Demo should be tailored to persona and priorities identified in discovery.

## Demo Principles
1. **Never do a generic demo** — tailor to their priorities from discovery
2. **Lead with their data** — show their actual teacher/school usage when possible
3. **Demo the workflow, not the feature list** — show how it fits their day
4. **Match the persona** — superintendent sees district view, teacher sees classroom view
5. **Leave time for questions** — 60% demo, 40% discussion

## Demo Flow by Persona

### Superintendent Demo (30 min)
**Goal:** District-wide value, ROI, board-ready narrative

1. **Open (5 min):** Recap discovery, show their district's current usage data
   - "Last time we spoke, you mentioned [priority]. Here's what we're seeing in your district..."
   - Show: X active teachers, Y games, Z students — growth over time
2. **District Dashboard (10 min):** Admin analytics and reporting
   - Building-by-building usage comparison
   - Teacher adoption tracking
   - Assessment data aggregation
   - Board-ready report export
3. **ROI Story (5 min):** Cost framing
   - Cost per teacher calculation
   - Time savings projection (auto-grading hours)
   - Comparison to current tool spending
4. **Implementation (5 min):** How rollout works
   - Phased approach: pilot → expand → district-wide
   - Training and PD support
   - Timeline aligned to their calendar
5. **Discussion (5 min):** Questions, next steps, who else to involve

### Curriculum Director Demo (30 min)
**Goal:** Assessment capabilities, standards alignment, instructional value

1. **Open (5 min):** Recap assessment/curriculum priorities from discovery
2. **Assessment Workflow (10 min):**
   - Teacher creating a standards-aligned assessment
   - Common Assessment setup across buildings
   - Item-level analytics and student performance data
   - PLC data sharing workflow
3. **Content & Differentiation (10 min):**
   - Content library and teacher-created content
   - Accommodation settings (IEP/504)
   - AI quiz generation aligned to their standards
4. **Admin View (5 min):** Curriculum-level reporting and standards coverage
5. **Discussion:** Pilot structure, evaluation criteria, next steps

### Principal Demo (20 min)
**Goal:** Building-level value, teacher adoption, low-lift implementation

1. **Open (3 min):** Show their school's current usage — "Here's what your teachers are already doing"
2. **Teacher Experience (7 min):** Quick quiz creation → game launch → auto-grading → results
3. **Admin View (5 min):** Building dashboard, teacher activity, student engagement
4. **Implementation (3 min):** "Your teachers already know how to use it — premium just unlocks more"
5. **Discussion (2 min):** Pilot interest, connecting to district leadership

### Teacher Champion Demo (15 min)
**Goal:** Show premium features they've been hitting paywalls on

1. **Open (2 min):** Acknowledge their power user status — specific usage data
2. **Premium Features (8 min):** Focus on their specific paywall hits
   - Advanced reports and analytics
   - Full content library access
   - AI quiz generation
   - Accommodation features
   - Additional game modes
3. **Champion Enablement (5 min):**
   - "Here's how to share this with your admin"
   - Usage summary they can forward
   - ROI talking points for their principal

## Handling Demo Objections
- **"Can you show me [specific feature]?"** → Pivot to it immediately, tie back to their need
- **"We already do this with [tool]"** → "Tell me more about that workflow — let me show you how this compares"
- **"This is nice but my teachers won't use it"** → Show adoption data: "X teachers already are"
- **"What about data privacy?"** → Transition to security/compliance resources
