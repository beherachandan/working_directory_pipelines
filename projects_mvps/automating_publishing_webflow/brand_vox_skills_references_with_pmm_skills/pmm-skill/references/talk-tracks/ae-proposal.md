# Talk Track: AE Proposal & Negotiation

## When to Use
Presenting pricing, negotiating terms, and closing the deal. Prospect has seen a demo and expressed intent to move forward.

## Proposal Framework

### Structure
1. **Executive Summary** — Recap their needs and how Wayground addresses them
2. **Usage Evidence** — Their current usage data proving product-market fit
3. **Recommended Package** — Pricing tied to their specific needs
4. **ROI Projection** — Cost justification with their data
5. **Implementation Plan** — Timeline, training, support
6. **Next Steps** — Decision timeline and process

### ROI Framing
Always calculate and present:
- **Cost per teacher** = ACV ÷ total teachers in scope
- **Cost per student** = ACV ÷ total students reached
- **Time savings** = auto-graded assessments × avg grading time per assessment
- **Consolidation value** = cost of tools Wayground replaces (Kahoot + assessment tool + etc.)

### Data-Backed Proof Points
Pull from BigQuery before presenting:
- Current active teachers and growth trajectory
- Games run and students reached (engagement proof)
- Paywall hits (demand proof)
- Content created (investment/switching cost proof)

## Negotiation Scenarios

### "The price is too high"
> "I understand budget is a consideration. Let me break this down — at [cost per teacher], you're getting assessment, engagement, and reporting in one platform. Most districts are spending more than that on just one of those categories with separate tools. Plus, [X] teachers are already active — you're paying to unlock what they're already using, not buying something new."

### "We need to see a pilot first"
> "Absolutely — a pilot makes sense. Here's what I'd recommend: [X schools] for [timeframe], measuring [specific metrics]. The data from the pilot will give you exactly what you need to bring this to the board. Let me draft a pilot proposal with success criteria."

### "We need to go through procurement"
> "Understood. I'll prepare everything procurement needs — security documentation, data privacy agreements, references. What's your procurement timeline? I want to make sure we align so your teachers have access by [target date]."

### "Can we get a discount for multi-year?"
> "Multi-year commitments do come with better pricing. A [2/3]-year agreement at [X] saves you [Y]% compared to annual — and locks in the rate before any future increases. It also gives your teachers continuity and your IT team one less renewal to manage."

### "We need board approval"
> "Let me help with that. I can prepare a board-ready summary with your district's usage data, ROI projections, and peer district references. What does your board need to see to approve? When is the next board meeting?"

## Urgency Creation (Ethical)
- **Budget cycle:** "To have this in place for [next semester/school year], we'd need to finalize by [date]"
- **Teacher momentum:** "Your teachers are active now — maintaining momentum matters"
- **Pricing:** "Current pricing is locked through [date] — I can hold this rate if we move by then"
- **Competitive:** "I know you're also evaluating [competitor] — happy to address any comparisons"

## Closing Language
> "Based on everything we've discussed — your [X] active teachers, your board's focus on [priority], and the [outcome] you're looking for — here's what I recommend: [package]. This gives you [key capabilities] at [cost per teacher]. If we can get this finalized by [date], your teachers will have full access for [semester/year]. What questions do you have?"

## Deal Acceleration Signals
Watch for these in account data — they indicate readiness to close:
- Paywall hits increasing (teachers actively wanting premium)
- New teachers joining the platform (organic growth)
- Multiple stakeholders engaged (multi-threaded)
- Board meeting discussing relevant topics
- Budget cycle timing aligns
