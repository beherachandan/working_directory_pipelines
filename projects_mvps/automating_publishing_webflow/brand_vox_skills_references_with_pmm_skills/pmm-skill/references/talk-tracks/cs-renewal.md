# Talk Track: CS Renewal & Expansion

## When to Use
Approaching contract renewal or identifying expansion opportunities within existing accounts.

## Renewal Framework

### 90-Day Pre-Renewal Checklist
1. **Pull usage data** — active teachers, games run, students reached, feature adoption
2. **Calculate health score** — adoption rate vs. licensed seats, trend direction
3. **Identify champions** — most active teachers, admin users
4. **Check expansion signals** — new schools, growing teacher count, new use cases
5. **Board meeting scan** — any new priorities that align with upsell opportunities

### Health Indicators

**Healthy (Renew + Expand):**
- Active teacher rate > 60% of licensed seats
- Games per teacher per month increasing or stable
- Multiple schools/buildings active
- Admin dashboard being used
- Feature adoption deepening (CA, accommodations, reports)

**At Risk (Intervention Needed):**
- Active teacher rate < 40% of licensed seats
- Declining games per month
- Usage concentrated in 1-2 buildings
- No admin login activity
- No new teachers joining

**Critical (Save Required):**
- Active teacher rate < 20%
- Sharp usage decline
- Champion teacher left or went quiet
- No admin engagement
- Competitor tools appearing in usage patterns

### Renewal Conversation

**Healthy Account:**
> "Great news — your district has had a strong year with Wayground. [X] teachers ran [Y] games reaching [Z] students. That's [growth%] over last year. Based on this momentum, I want to talk about how we can build on this success — both renewing your current plan and potentially expanding to [additional schools/features]."

**At-Risk Account:**
> "I wanted to check in on your Wayground experience. I see [X] teachers are active, which is [below/around] where we'd expect for your plan. I want to make sure you're getting full value. Can we talk about what's working and where we might need to adjust?"

**Critical Account:**
> "I noticed usage has dropped at [District]. I want to understand what's happening — has anything changed in your instructional strategy? I'd like to work with you to get your teachers re-engaged before your renewal comes up."

## Expansion Opportunities

### Signals to Watch
- **School-level growth:** Active teachers at unlicensed schools (organic spread)
- **Feature ceiling:** Teachers hitting usage limits or requesting features not in their plan
- **New priorities:** Board meetings discussing topics that map to unused Wayground features
- **Teacher count growth:** More teachers joining than originally licensed
- **Adjacent use cases:** CA rollout, accommodation needs, new grade levels

### Expansion Talk Track
> "Your usage data tells an interesting story. [School A] and [School B] have been your strongest adopters, but I'm also seeing [X] teachers at [unlicensed schools] who've started using the free version. There's clear demand beyond your current plan. If we expand to include [additional schools], your cost per teacher actually goes down to [amount] and those teachers get immediate access to premium features."

### Upsell Framing by Feature
- **Common Assessment:** "Your curriculum team could standardize assessments across buildings"
- **Advanced Reports:** "Your admin team could get district-wide analytics without manual aggregation"
- **AI Generation:** "Teachers could create aligned content in minutes instead of hours"
- **Accommodations:** "Built-in IEP/504 support without a separate tool"

## Renewal Objections

### "We're not sure we're getting enough value"
> "Let me pull your usage report. [Show data.] Your active teachers are using it [X] times per week — that's [comparison to peer districts]. Where specifically are you not seeing value? I want to address that directly."

### "Budget is tighter this year"
> "I understand budget pressures. Let me show you the ROI — at [cost per active teacher], this is one of the most cost-effective tools in your stack. We can also discuss [right-sizing the plan / multi-year pricing / phased approach] to work within your budget."

### "We're evaluating other options"
> "That's smart to look at the market. Here's what I'd consider: your teachers have created [X] quizzes with [Y] questions — that's a significant content investment. Plus, [Z] teachers are trained and active. Switching costs are real. I'd love to understand what you're looking for that we might not be providing."

## Key Metrics for Renewal Conversations
Always pull before renewal discussions:
- Active teachers / licensed seats (adoption rate)
- Games per teacher per month (depth)
- Students reached (breadth)
- Content created (switching cost)
- Feature adoption breakdown (which premium features used)
- YoY trends (trajectory)
