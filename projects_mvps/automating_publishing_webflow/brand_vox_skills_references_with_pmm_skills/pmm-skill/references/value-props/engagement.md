# Value Prop: Engagement

## One-Liner
"Wayground turns review and practice into something students actually ask for — game-based learning that drives participation across every subject and grade level."

## The Data Proof
Query these BigQuery metrics to back this claim:
- **Games played** — `clean.game` count with `is_classroom_game = TRUE`
- **Students per game** — average player count per game
- **Repeat usage** — games per teacher per week/month (habitual use)
- **Subject/grade spread** — games across diverse content areas
- **Student completion rates** — players who finish vs. drop off
- **NPS feedback** — `track.nps_feedback` for student satisfaction signals

## By Persona

### Superintendent
- Frame: "District-wide student engagement that shows up in participation and attendance data"
- Lead with: Total students reached, district-wide participation metrics, equity of access
- Proof point: "X thousand students across your district are actively engaged — that's Y% of your student body"

### Curriculum Director
- Frame: "Engagement that serves instruction — not just fun, but fun with learning outcomes"
- Lead with: Content quality, standards alignment, engagement paired with assessment data
- Proof point: "Teachers are running games aligned to your standards — engagement drives completion and data"

### Principal
- Frame: "Students ask for this. Teachers use it. You see the data. Easiest win in your building."
- Lead with: Teacher adoption rate, student participation, building-level engagement trends
- Proof point: "X teachers running Y games per week — students are participating at Z% completion"

### Teacher Champion
- Frame: "Your students actually want to do the review. That's not normal. Let's keep it going."
- Lead with: Student response, ease of creating engaging content, peer teacher adoption
- Proof point: "You've engaged Y students across Z games — premium unlocks more game modes and customization"

## Competitive Angle
- **vs. Kahoot:** Similar engagement hook, but Wayground adds assessment depth on top of the fun
- **vs. Gimkit:** Gimkit is purely gamification — less content creation, no district features, no assessment
- **vs. Nearpod:** Nearpod is lesson delivery — less teacher-created, less replay value for students
- **vs. Blooket:** Fun but shallow — no real instructional data, no district-level features
- **Wayground advantage:** Engagement + assessment in one platform — fun that produces actionable data
