## EAR Coverage Is D1's Most Wayground-Specific Dimension

EAR Coverage (18% weight in D1) is the only dimension that requires a content brief — competitors can't score on it. A well-structured B2 EAR map is a structural advantage unique to the pipeline.

## EAR Depth Principles

### Entities to always include for K-12 education topics
- The core concept (entity being defined)
- The practitioner implementing it (teacher/admin/student)
- The context (classroom, school, district)
- The outcome (student learning, achievement, engagement)
- Related concepts (what it differs from, what it complements)

### Attributes that distinguish high-coverage articles
From calibration: articles cited by AI cover these attribute types:
1. **Definition** — what it is (definitional)
2. **Mechanism** — how it works (explanatory)
3. **Evidence** — why it works (research-backed)
4. **Implementation** — how to do it (practical)
5. **Examples** — what it looks like (concrete)
6. **Misconceptions** — what it's not (clarifying)
7. **Measurement** — how to assess it (actionable)

### Relationships that create self-contained passages
Each relationship in the EAR map should be expressible as a standalone extractable passage (134–167 words optimal).
If a relationship requires multiple sections to explain → split into two relationships.

## Topic-Specific EAR Notes

### Student Engagement
Core entity: Student engagement
Must-cover attributes: 3 dimensions (behavioral/cognitive/emotional), measurement methods, drop-off data, specific strategies per dimension, technology integration
Key relationship: engagement ↔ academic outcomes (most cited connection in field)

### Differentiated Instruction
Core entity: Differentiated instruction
Must-cover attributes: 4 pillars (content/process/product/environment), flexible grouping, formative assessment integration, UDL relationship, implementation steps
Key relationship: DI ↔ diverse learner needs (the why)
