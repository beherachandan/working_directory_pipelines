## What Makes a Brief Produce a High-Scoring Article

### Finding 1: Stats targets must be explicit in the brief
Articles fail D1 on Stats most often when the brief doesn't specify what statistics to acquire.
**Rule:** Every brief must include a "Must-have statistics" section with ≥5 named sources/stats to find.
Stats are the #1 citation predictor (delta +1.55). If C4 doesn't know what stats to find, they won't.

### Finding 2: Unique data angle must be identified upfront
Uniqueness is the #2 citation predictor (delta +1.45). If the brief doesn't specify a Wayground-specific data angle, C4 can't create it.
**Rule:** Every brief must include a "Wayground unique angle" — at minimum one platform metric or first-person signal.

### Finding 3: Format must match field's best-in-class, not just intent type
Generic "informational" format produces mid-tier articles. The brief should specify the exact format template.
**From D3 competitor analysis:**
- Student engagement → Xello 3-dimension framework (behavioral/cognitive/emotional) as spine
- Differentiated instruction → Vanderbilt comparison table (Traditional vs DI) as anchor element
- Formative assessment → Renaissance EdWords Q&A format as model
- Retrieval practice → learningscientists.org definition + why + how + FAQ as model

### Finding 4: Brief quality = article ceiling
C4 and D1 scores are bounded by brief quality. A thin brief produces a thin article.
The brief should pre-answer: What is the win condition? What stats anchor this? What is uniquely Wayground's?

## Rubric Weight Context (v1.1)

Brief architecture should prioritise these dimensions in order:
1. **Statistical Density (17%)** — most predictive of citation; must be explicit in brief
2. **Uniqueness (16%)** — second most predictive; must have a Wayground-specific angle
3. **QAPE (18%)** — table stakes; every H2 must be a question with answer-first structure
4. **EAR Coverage (18%)** — D1-specific; must cover all B2 attributes
5. **Trust (12%)** — expert quotes + research citations; C4 handles but brief must flag key experts
