| Growth mindset | Carol Dweck | Stanford University | Mindset definition |
| Student engagement | Eric Jensen | Brain-based learning | Neuroscience angle |

### How to attribute quotes
- Full name + title + institution: *"Carol Ann Tomlinson, Professor Emerita at the University of Virginia, defines…"*
- Named study: *"According to a 2023 Gallup poll of 5.4 million students…"*
- Never: *"experts say"*, *"research shows"*, *"studies suggest"* — these don't count as citations

## Statistics Worth Acquiring Per Topic

### Student Engagement
- 49% of K-12 students report being engaged at school (Gallup 2023)
- Active learning reduces failure rates by 55% vs traditional lecture (Freeman et al., PNAS 2014)
- Engaged students are 2.5x more likely to report excellent grades (Gallup)
- Student engagement drops 60% between 5th and 11th grade (Quaglia Institute)

### Differentiated Instruction
- Students in DI classrooms show 2-9 month learning gains vs traditional (Firmender et al.)
- 76% of teachers report DI improves student outcomes (EdWeek survey)
- Flexible grouping improves low-achievers' performance by 15-20% (Vygotsky ZPD research)

### Formative Assessment
- Students in formative assessment classrooms score 0.4–0.7 standard deviations higher (Black & Wiliam 1998)
- Teachers using regular formative checks identify gaps 3x faster than end-of-unit tests
- 68% of teachers report formative assessment improves lesson planning (NWEA 2022)

## What NOT to Count as a Citation
- "Many studies show…"
- "Research suggests…"
- "According to experts…"
- Percentages without named source or year
- "A recent study found…" without naming the study
