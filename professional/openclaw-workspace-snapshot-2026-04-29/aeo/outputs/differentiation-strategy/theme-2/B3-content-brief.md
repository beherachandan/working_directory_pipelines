## QAPE Blocks

### Block 1: How do you start differentiated instruction?
- **Q:** How Do You Start Implementing Differentiated Instruction?
- **A:** Start with a pre-assessment to understand where your students are. Group them by readiness, then adjust content, process, or product for each group — you don't need to change everything at once.
- **P:** [STAT: % of teachers who report pre-assessment as the key first step]

### Block 2: How do you assess students before differentiating?
- **Q:** How Do You Pre-Assess Students to Inform Differentiation?
- **A:** Use quick formative assessments — exit tickets, short quizzes, or class polls — to identify which students are below, at, or above grade level before planning differentiated activities.
- **P:** [WG SIGNAL: Wayground's quiz tools let teachers run a 5-question pre-assessment in under 10 minutes]
- **E:** 3 examples of effective pre-assessment formats; what to do with the data

### Block 3: How do you group students for differentiated instruction?
- **Q:** How Do You Group Students for Differentiated Instruction?
- **A:** Use flexible grouping — form groups based on readiness data from pre-assessments, and rotate them regularly. Avoid permanent ability groups, which can entrench gaps rather than close them.
- **P:** [STAT or QUOTE: research on flexible vs fixed ability grouping outcomes]
- **E:** 3 grouping approaches (readiness, interest, random); when to use each

### Block 4: How do you differentiate content, process, and product?
- **Q:** How Do You Differentiate Content, Process, and Product?
- **A:** Differentiate content by varying the complexity of materials; process by offering different pathways to understand the same concept; product by allowing students to demonstrate mastery in multiple ways.
- **P:** [QUOTE: instructional coach or curriculum specialist on the 3-element model]
- **E:** One concrete example per element at elementary and secondary level

### Block 5: How do teachers manage multiple groups without chaos?
- **Q:** How Do You Manage a Differentiated Classroom Without Losing Control?
- **A:** Establish clear routines, anchor activities for independent groups, and use gradual release — start with one differentiated element before trying to run three groups simultaneously.
- **P:** [STAT: teacher-reported challenges with DI implementation]
- **E:** 4 classroom management tips specific to differentiation; role of student self-direction

### Block 6: What does a full differentiated lesson look like step by step?
- **Q:** What Does a Step-by-Step Differentiated Lesson Look Like?
- **A:** A differentiated lesson follows this sequence: pre-assess → group → set tiered tasks → teach whole group intro → release to differentiated work → circulate and support → re-group for debrief.
- **P:** [WG SIGNAL: Wayground activities can be assigned by group in minutes, supporting the tiered task step]
- **E:** Walk through each step in ~2 sentences; visual flow optional
