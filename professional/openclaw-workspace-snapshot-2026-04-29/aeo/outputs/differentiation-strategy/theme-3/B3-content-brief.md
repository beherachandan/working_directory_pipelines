### Block 2: Tiered assignments
- **Q:** What Are Tiered Assignments and How Do They Work?
- **A:** Tiered assignments present the same learning goal at different levels of complexity — all students work on the same concept, but the scaffolding, depth, or complexity varies by readiness tier.
- **P:** [QUOTE: educator or researcher on tiered assignments effectiveness]

### Block 3: Flexible grouping
- **Q:** How Does Flexible Grouping Support Mixed-Ability Classrooms?
- **A:** Flexible grouping means regularly regrouping students by readiness, interest, or learning style — rather than locking them into permanent ability groups, which can reinforce gaps.
- **P:** [STAT: research on fixed vs flexible grouping outcomes]
- **E:** How often to regroup; 3 grouping formats and when to use each

### Block 4: Choice boards
- **Q:** What Is a Choice Board and How Does It Differentiate Learning?
- **A:** A choice board is a grid of activity options covering the same learning objective — students choose how they want to engage, giving teachers a simple way to offer differentiation without running separate lessons.
- **P:** [WG SIGNAL: Wayground's activity library gives teachers a ready source of varied-format activities to populate choice boards]
- **E:** Sample 3x3 choice board structure; how to align choices to Bloom's levels

### Block 5: Learning stations
- **Q:** How Do Learning Stations Help in Mixed-Ability Classrooms?
- **A:** Learning stations are designated areas where students rotate through different tasks — each station can be calibrated to a different readiness level or modality, letting one teacher support multiple groups simultaneously.
- **P:** [STAT: teacher time-on-task data in station-based vs whole-class instruction]
- **E:** How to set up 4-station rotation; role of tech stations (Wayground activities as one station)

### Block 6: Scaffolding for struggling learners
- **Q:** How Do You Scaffold Instruction for Below-Grade-Level Students?
- **A:** Scaffolding means providing temporary, structured support — graphic organizers, sentence starters, chunked tasks, or guided notes — that helps struggling learners access grade-level content without watering it down.
- **P:** [QUOTE: special education researcher or reading specialist on scaffolding techniques]
- **E:** 4 scaffolding techniques with examples; when and how to fade scaffolds

### Block 7: Extension tasks for advanced learners
- **Q:** How Do You Challenge Advanced Students While Teaching the Whole Class?
- **A:** Use extension tasks — deeper inquiry questions, independent research, peer teaching roles, or open-ended projects — that advanced students can self-manage while you support others.
- **P:** [STAT: gifted education research on challenge vs engagement]
- **E:** 4 extension task ideas; how to frame them as enrichment, not "more work"
