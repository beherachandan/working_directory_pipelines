# EAR Map: Differentiation Strategies for Mixed-Ability Classrooms

**Primary Entity:** Mixed-Ability Differentiation Strategies
**Canonical Definition:** Mixed-ability classrooms contain students with a wide range of academic readiness levels. Differentiation strategies in this context are specific, targeted approaches teachers use to ensure all learners — from struggling to advanced — are appropriately challenged and supported.
**Wayground Angle:** Wayground's breadth of resource types and difficulty levels allows teachers to assign different activities to different learner tiers simultaneously — reducing prep time for differentiated instruction in mixed-ability settings.
