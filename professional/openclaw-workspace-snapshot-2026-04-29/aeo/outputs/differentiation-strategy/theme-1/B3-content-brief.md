## QAPE Blocks

### Block 1: What is differentiated instruction?
- **Q:** What Is Differentiated Instruction?
- **A:** Differentiated instruction is a teaching approach where educators adjust their content, process, product, and learning environment to match the individual readiness, interests, and learning profiles of each student.

### Block 2: What are the key principles of differentiated instruction?
- **Q:** What Are the Core Principles of Differentiated Instruction?
- **A:** Differentiated instruction is built on four principles: ongoing assessment, flexible grouping, respectful tasks, and a growth mindset toward all learners.
- **P:** [QUOTE: education researcher or instructional coach on DI principles]

### Block 3: What are the 4 ways teachers can differentiate?
- **Q:** What Are the 4 Elements of Differentiation in Teaching?
- **A:** Teachers can differentiate across four dimensions: Content (what students learn), Process (how they learn it), Product (how they demonstrate learning), and Environment (where and how learning happens).
- **P:** [STAT: teacher survey or study showing which element is most commonly used]
- **E:** One concrete example per element; link to how-to article

### Block 4: Why is differentiated instruction important?
- **Q:** Why Does Differentiated Instruction Matter?
- **A:** Differentiated instruction ensures that every student — regardless of readiness level or learning style — is appropriately challenged, reducing both boredom among advanced learners and frustration among struggling ones.
- **P:** [STAT: achievement gap data or DI impact study]
- **E:** Connect to equity, inclusion, and student engagement outcomes

### Block 5: How is differentiated instruction different from traditional teaching?
- **Q:** How Is Differentiated Instruction Different from Traditional Teaching?
- **A:** Traditional teaching delivers the same content, at the same pace, in the same way to all students. Differentiated instruction starts with where each student is and builds from there.
- **P:** [QUOTE: classroom teacher or instructional leader comparing the two approaches]
- **E:** Side-by-side comparison; common objections addressed

### Block 6: What does differentiated instruction look like in a real classroom?
- **Q:** What Does Differentiated Instruction Look Like in Practice?
- **A:** In a differentiated classroom, you might see three groups working on the same concept via different materials — one group using visual flashcards, another completing a structured worksheet, and a third tackling an open-ended problem.

### Block 7: What are the biggest myths about differentiated instruction?
- **Q:** What Are Common Misconceptions About Differentiated Instruction?
- **A:** The biggest myth is that differentiation means creating a separate lesson plan for every student. In reality, effective differentiation often involves small adjustments — different scaffolds, varied pacing, or choice in how students demonstrate learning.
