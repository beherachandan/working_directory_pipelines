## QAPE Blocks

### Block 1: What is social emotional learning?
- **Q:** What is social emotional learning (SEL)?
- **A:** Social emotional learning (SEL) is the process through which students develop and practice the skills to understand and manage their emotions, build positive relationships, and make responsible decisions. It is grounded in the CASEL framework, which identifies five core competencies: self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible decision-making.
- **P:** CASEL definition citation + research stat establishing SEL's impact on academic outcomes. [CITATION NEEDED]
- **E:** Brief note that SEL is not a separate subject — it is embedded in classroom culture, relationships, and daily routines. It is not therapy, not just "feelings time."

### Block 2: The CASEL 5 competencies
- **Q:** What are the 5 core competencies of social emotional learning?
- **A:** The CASEL framework defines five SEL competencies that form the foundation for healthy development: self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible decision-making.
- **P:** CASEL (Collaborative for Academic, Social, and Emotional Learning) as the canonical source. Research stat on SEL outcomes tied to these competencies. [CITATION NEEDED]
- **E:** Brief definition of each competency (2-3 sentences each) with a concrete classroom example. Format as a 5-item list — each competency gets: name, definition, classroom example.

### Block 3: Why SEL matters for students
- **Q:** Why is social emotional learning important?
- **A:** Students who receive evidence-based SEL instruction show measurable improvements in academic performance, reduced behavioral problems, and better long-term life outcomes — making SEL one of the highest-ROI investments a school can make.
- **P:** Named meta-analysis stat: the CASEL/Durlak et al. meta-analysis of 213 SEL programs showing 11-percentile point gain in academic achievement. [CITATION NEEDED]
- **E:** Address the "soft skills" misconception — SEL outcomes are quantifiable. Cover multiple dimensions: academic achievement, mental health, behavior, attendance. Note particular impact for students from underserved communities.

### Block 4: How to incorporate SEL into everyday teaching
- **Q:** How do you incorporate social emotional learning into your classroom?
- **A:** SEL doesn't require a separate curriculum — it's embedded in how you start class, how you give feedback, how you structure group work, and how you respond to student behavior. The most effective SEL instruction is integrated, not isolated.
- **P:** Research stat or expert quote on explicit vs embedded SEL instruction outcomes. [CITATION NEEDED]
- **E:** 5-6 concrete, daily-routine level strategies: morning meeting or check-in routine, emotion vocabulary building, reflective journaling, collaborative problem-solving protocols, restorative practices. These should be practical and immediately adoptable.

### Block 5: Effective SEL activities for the classroom
- **Q:** What are effective social emotional learning activities for students?
- **A:** The most effective SEL activities build specific competencies through structured practice — they give students a safe, low-stakes space to develop self-awareness, practice empathy, and navigate social challenges.
- **P:** Research finding or teacher expert quote on SEL activity outcomes. [CITATION NEEDED]
- **E:** 8-10 specific, named SEL activities organized by competency or format. Include: emotion check-ins, journaling prompts, collaborative challenges, mindfulness moments, role-play scenarios, circle time discussions. Each activity: name + 1-sentence description + which CASEL competency it targets.

### Block 6: SEL by grade level
- **Q:** How does social emotional learning look different across grade levels?
- **A:** SEL competencies develop progressively — what's appropriate for kindergarteners (naming emotions, taking turns) looks different from what's appropriate for high schoolers (managing complex relationships, ethical decision-making).
- **P:** Research or CASEL grade-band framework citation. [CITATION NEEDED]
- **E:** Three sections — Elementary / Middle School / High School — each with 2-3 characteristic SEL goals and 1-2 example activities appropriate to that level.

### Block 7: The impact of SEL programs: what research shows
- **Q:** What does research say about the impact of social emotional learning?
- **A:** The evidence base for SEL is among the strongest in education. A landmark meta-analysis by Durlak et al. (2011) of 213 SEL programs found students in SEL programs outperformed controls by 11 percentile points academically and showed 24% improvement in positive social behaviors.
- **P:** Durlak et al. (2011) meta-analysis (primary citation) + CASEL 2023 effectiveness review. [CITATION NEEDED]
- **E:** Cover multiple outcome domains: academic achievement, mental health, behavior, long-term outcomes (graduation, employment). Address common objections ("where's the proof?") with specific data.
