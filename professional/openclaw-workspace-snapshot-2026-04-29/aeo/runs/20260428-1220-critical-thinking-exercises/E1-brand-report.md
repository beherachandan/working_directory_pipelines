## Writing Standards Issues

### H2 opener length (minor)
- [H2 OPENER] "What are critical thinking exercises for students?" section opener is ~43 words (limit: 40)
  - Current: "Critical thinking exercises are structured classroom activities that teach students to analyze information, evaluate evidence, identify assumptions, and reach reasoned conclusions. Unlike recall-based tasks, they require students to engage with _why_ something is true, not just _what_ is true."
  - Fix: Trim the second sentence. Suggested revision: "Critical thinking exercises are structured classroom activities that teach students to analyze information, evaluate evidence, identify assumptions, and reach reasoned conclusions. They ask students to engage with _why_, not just _what_."
  - New count: ~38 words ✅

### Long sentence (minor)
- [SENTENCE LENGTH] Abrami citation sentence in "What are critical thinking exercises?" section: ~52 words
  - Current: "A landmark meta-analysis by Abrami et al. (2015), drawing on 341 effect sizes across quasi- and true-experimental studies, found a weighted mean effect size of g+ = 0.30 (p < 0.001) for CT instruction compared to no instruction -- meaning students who received explicit, structured CT teaching significantly outperformed those who did not."
  - Fix: Break into two sentences. Suggested revision: "A landmark meta-analysis by Abrami et al. (2015) drew on 341 effect sizes across quasi- and true-experimental studies. It found a weighted mean effect size of g+ = 0.30 (p < 0.001) -- meaning students who received explicit CT teaching significantly outperformed those who did not."
  - New counts: ~24 words + ~28 words ✅
