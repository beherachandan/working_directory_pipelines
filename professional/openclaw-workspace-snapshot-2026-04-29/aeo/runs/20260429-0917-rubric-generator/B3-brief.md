### Block 2: What types of rubrics exist?
- **Q:** What are the types of rubrics?
- **A:** Three main types: holistic, analytic, and single-point. State in first sentence.
- **P:** [QUOTE: assessment researcher or educator on rubric types]
- **E:** (1) Analytic rubric: separate criteria rows, each scored independently — most detailed, most feedback-rich, most common in K-12. (2) Holistic rubric: one overall score with descriptions per level — faster to apply, less specific feedback. (3) Single-point rubric: describes only the proficiency level, with space for teacher notes on above/below — increasingly popular for reducing evaluation overwhelm. Include comparison table.
