### Step 2: Product Mention Quality

**Only product mention (Team Mode, digital section):**

> *"If you want a ready-made digital analog that works across all three contexts, [Wayground's Team Mode](/features/team-mode) replicates the partner-discussion structure in a digital setting — letting students respond to each other's answers in real time, with built-in accountability that shows you which pairs engaged and which ones drifted."*

**Assessment:** ✅ Passes.

- Placement is earned — comes *after* three paragraphs explaining the logistics of digital/hybrid Turn and Talk. The reader has already absorbed the problem; the product is introduced as a solution, not a pitch.
- "Ready-made digital analog" is precisely the right framing: it maps the product to something the teacher already understands and wants.
- "Built-in accountability that shows you which pairs engaged" adds a concrete teacher benefit (monitoring without standing at the front) rather than a feature list.
- Tone reads as colleague recommendation, not marketing copy. ✅

**Adjacent soft CTA:**

> *"Browse [ready-made discussion activities](/activities/discussion) to find prompts you can launch immediately."*

This is a content resource link, not a second product mention. It follows naturally from the Team Mode paragraph and gives teachers an immediate next step. Acceptable within the product mention count — this is a resource library link, not a brand/product anchor. ✅ No count issue.

**Product mention total: 1** ✅
