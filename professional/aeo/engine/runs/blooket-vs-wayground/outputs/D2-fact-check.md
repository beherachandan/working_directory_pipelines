### ❌ INCORRECT #2: Black & Wiliam Citation - Potential Misrepresentation

**Location:** 
- FAQ section: "learning gains equivalent to 8 months" (Revision #2 note says this was fixed, but checking for residual issues)
- Main body (revised): "effect sizes equivalent to several months of additional progress"

**Claim:** Black & Wiliam (1998) research shows formative assessment produces "several months of additional progress"

**Citation provided:** *Assessment in Education: Principles, Policy & Practice*, 5(1), 7-74, 1998

**Verification status:** ⚠️ **LIKELY INCORRECT CITATION FORMAT**

**Issue:** The famous "Black box" paper by Black and Wiliam is actually:
- **Correct citation:** Black, P., & Wiliam, D. (1998). Inside the Black Box: Raising Standards Through Classroom Assessment. *Phi Delta Kappan*, 80(2), 139-148.

OR

- Black, P., & Wiliam, D. (1998). Assessment and Classroom Learning. *Assessment in Education: Principles, Policy & Practice*, 5(1), 7-74.

The journal name matches, but the claim about "5-8 months" or "several months" is often cited but requires careful verification of exact wording and context. The article hedges appropriately ("varied across studies and contexts"), but the citation needs verification.

**Recommendation:** 
- Verify the exact citation (journal correct, but check page numbers and volume)
- Access the original paper to confirm the "several months" claim is accurately represented
- If the paper does not explicitly state this, either remove the claim or cite a secondary source that interprets the effect sizes this way

**Status:** ⚠️ NEEDS VERIFICATION - do not publish until original source checked
